The Closure

Annual General Meeting, 1980

The final Annual General Meeting of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club was held in the new Club House of the Calypso Diving Club, the Metropole Theatre, B'kara, on Friday, 24th October, 1980.

Members present 14.

In view of the forthcoming closure, and the vital decisions appertaining to it, I will print in full the three Motions submitted at the A.G.M. by the General Committee, and which had previously been promulgated in an A.G.M. Notice to Members:

  1. In view of all the circumstances, the General Committee has no alternative but to recommend the winding-up of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club as from the date of this General Meeting of Members.
  2. In accordance with the terms of No. 13 of the Club Rules, the surplus funds be put in a Suspense Account.
    The Committee proposes that three Trustees be elected to review the financial position not later than the end of September, 1981. The Committee has agreed that the three Trustees should be Messrs. C. Fox, J. Sharp and G. Attard.
  3. That a Caretaker Committee of the present nine members viz. C. Fox, J. Sharp, J. Young, J. Micallef, C. Borda, L. Vella Brincat, D. Schembri, C. Mock and G. Attard be elected to safeguard the interests of the Club and to consider the use of these funds in the furtherance of the object of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club should a favourable occasion arise.

Mr. C. Fox asked the Secretary, Mr. G. Attard, to read the Notice convening the Meeting. The Members present then confirmed the Committee's decision to appoint Mr. Fox as Chairman.

The Chairman read out a detailed report outlining the history of the Club and the reasons for its imminent winding-up.

After the normal Committee Reports for the 1979/80 season were given, the Secretary reported that no amendments had been forwarded to him regarding the three resolutions as promulgated in the Notice.

All three resolutions were read out and accepted unanimously.

Presentation of Club's Regalia

Details of this special ceremony were given to me by Mr. C. Fox. To get the true feeling of those attending I will quote Cyril's account:

"The final ceremony of the winding-up of Overseas took place at St. Edward's College in November, 1980, when, at the kind invitation of the Headmaster (Mr. A. Cachia Caruana), the members of the Caretaker Committee attended a Drinks party at the College. The Prefects and senior boys of the School XV were entertaining our party and were most attentive in keeping our glasses re-charged.

Mr. Cyril Fox, as President/Chairman, led the Overseas party and thanked the Headmaster for his hospitality and for his invaluable co-operation during the past several years, when his boys had played such an active part on the Marsa pitch. In his speech Mr. Fox made reference to the services which Mr. Charles Mock, the School's sports master, had given to Overseas in his coaching and physical training classes. He also singled out Tim Agius-Ferrante, a former Head Boy at Tal Handaq School, who had given yeoman service to Overseas, both on the field and in the committee room. Then came the handing-over ceremony. Mr. Fox, on behalf of the club, handed over all the trophies, plaques and banners which had been collected over the years and had been decorating the Club House (and as members will recall we had a goodly collection). The Headmaster was very gracious in his reply. A pleasant, though sad occasion, which ended an era of rugby football in Malta."

Caretaker Committee

The Caretaker Committee met initially on the 10th November, 1980, and during 1981 'minuted meetings' were held at infrequent intervals. Informal meetings were also held. Meetings and a monthly social gathering took place at the offices of K.R.S. in Zachary Street, Valletta.

On the 10th November, Cyril Fox and George Attard were elected Chairman and Hon. Secretary respectively. Tim Agius-Ferrante was co-opted onto the Caretaker Committee to become Hon. Treasurer. At this meeting the Chairman reminded those present that the Club had been placed in moth balls, so to speak, until September, 1981, when the final depositions would be made. The Caretaker Committee's first moves were to implement decisions made in Committee previous to the A.G.M. as regards the new arrangements of Club funds, also presentations. The following organisations had to be officially informed of the winding-up of Overseas: Andrews Feeds, the Press, St. Edwards, the University, Insurance, the Armed Forces, the Ministry of Sport, the Police and the R.F.U.

Jack Sharp continued living in Malta until his return to England at the beginning of 1982. But right up to the date of his departure he kept his interest in Overseas and regularly held his well-known and always appreciated drinks and film evenings. His invitations always included the ladies and with Jack and Dorothy the Committee kept the spirit of fellowship in the great sport of rugby football going to the end.

The final meeting of the Caretaker Committee was deferred to the 11th January, 1982. The main item on the agenda at this meeting was to dispose of the balance of the Club funds as required by the resolutions passed during the A.G.M. and according to the three Trustees' recommendations. The Overseas Rugby and Sports Club did not have a large amount of money to dispose of and a great deal of thought must have been put into its method of disposal. So ended a great Club.

The Chairman, Cyril Fox, also gave up his residence in Malta and left for home in England in June, 1982. The last of the expatriates. Now, having achieved his 80th birthday, Cyril can sit back and enjoy rugger on the television. Since his return he has received a most kind letter from Mr. R.H.G. Weighill, the Hon. Secretary of the R.F.U.

A copy of Mr. Weighill's letter to Cyril Fox is included in this Final Chapter.

Always first and foremost a Rugby Club, but latterly also a Sports Club, Overseas promoted rugby in Malta for 36 years and it is to be hoped left seeds of the game to be developed in future years.

10th May, 1982

C. Fox, Esq.,
Chairman,
Overseas Rugby and Sports Club (Malta),
47 Tigne Street,
Sliema,
MALTA G.C.

Dear Mr Fox,

Thank you so much for your letter which has been forwarded to me by George Attard and informs us of the demise of the Malta Rugby Club.

We do fully appreciate the difficulties under which you have been operating during the past few years and were very conscious of the immense amount of energy and work which was involved in keeping going.

It is always sad when Clubs of this nature have to wind up their affairs but we do sincerely hope that the game will have been given a good foundation in Malta and that some of the schools will continue to play and hopefully in due course the Club may arise again.

Thank you so much also for the donation to the Rugby Football Union. We will try and think of some way in which we can use this money to commemorate the activities of your Club so that it will not be entirely lost to our records and also in the minds of rugby people throughout the world.

I do sincerely hope that we shall have an opportunity of meeting you if and when you come to the United Kingdom and would welcome you at Twickenham whenever that may be.

Yours sincerely, Bob Weighill

Long Serving Members

Cyril Fox

Cyril Fox joined Overseas in 1972. In his first year as a member, and in all years between 1974 and 1982, Cyril served the Club in the capacity of Vice-President. In 1973/74, the year Overseas became a Rugby and Sports Club, he was elected President. Although intensely interested in both rugby and cricket, watching from the touchline or boundary on a Saturday and/or Sunday afternoon, his forte was to work for the Club as a whole, and not trying to pick the winning team on a Selection Committee. This was particularly so over the last 4 years of the Club's existence, when, as one of a Committee, he first fought to save the Club from closure, and, when this was a fait accompli, he helped to settle the Club's affairs with equal dedication. Always willing to take on extra Committee work, usually through difficult times, all members came to rely on his sound judgement. I personally have never seen Cyril ruffled or lose his calm and always he was interested in people, and had the time and courtesy to speak to everyone. Twice during the last 3 years he stepped into the breach to act as President/Chairman, and his last Office was to Chair the final Committee Meeting of the Club.

Jack Sharp (Hon. Life Member)

In the History of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club (p. 152) I gave a full account of the many ways in which Jack served the Club up to May, 1978. At that time Jack was due to retire and his future movements unknown, at least to the writer. Fortunately for Overseas, he remained with M.U.F.C., later called K.R.S., after May, 1978, and was deeply involved in the Club right up to its closure.

I repeat what I wrote in the History. Although I have listed below many of the Offices undertaken by Jack, it is impossible to name the many diverse ways he has helped the Club. It is impossible to overestimate his contact with the Maltese Ministry, particularly the Minister of Sport, on the Club's behalf; the many times he has acted as host to Overseas Committees and Members; his direct contact with the Colleges appertaining to rugby participation; and not least for providing all the necessities for Film Evenings at the Club House. This last mentioned service did much to boost the morale of members towards the end when times were difficult, and a Club House was of vital necessity for keeping members together.

Don Alexander

Don Alexander, Overseas and Moseley, served on Overseas General and Rugby Committees from the time he joined the Club until he left Malta in September, 1980. Don rarely seemed to wish to be in the limelight, but preferred to give his sound advice in Committee, particularly on rugby matters. His opinion was always respected. I think, for Don, the highlights of his service to Overseas were the visits to Malta by Moseley teams. Moseley played good rugby and they were amongst the best to visit Malta. Don left Malta quietly, giving the Committee no chance to arrange any farewell occasion in his honour intimating he was not in favour of such functions. Approximate departure, September, 1980.

Member of Club 1972/73 8 years
Vice-President 1972-80 8 years
Bar Committee 1972/73, 1974-76 & 1977/78 4 years
Ground work for Moseley R.U.F.C. Tours to Malta, 1974/75 & 1977/78

Tim Agius-Ferrante

Tim Agius-Ferrante, Committee Member and Rugby Player for Overseas for 9 years, was honoured in 1979/80 by being elected President of the Club. Tim was not only the first Maltese President, but also the youngest, in the Club's history. He took over as President at a very difficult time, when the Club was fighting for its very existence, and did a difficult job well, until forced by circumstances to resign.

In his 'younger days' Tim was Head Boy at Tal Handaq Combined Services School and Captain of the school 1st XV. As a schoolboy he occasionally played for Overseas XVs. Gone are the days. Tim was 'B' Team Captain in 1974/75, and in the same year he was awarded the 'Young Player of the Year' Cup.

Member of Club 1973/74 9 years
President 1979/80
Hon. General Treasurer 1978-82 4 years
Caretaker Committee, October, 1980, to January, 1982

Dave Schembri

Dave Schembri was a good Captain both on the field, where he led by example, and socially. For the 2 years he was Captain he had a task which became increasingly difficult as the number of players fell, games became fewer, and morale suffered accordingly. From his early days with the Club when he wrote an article in the Times of Malta (History of the Club, page 130) called 'Rugby is a Fascinating Game', and later as Liaison Government Schools, he did his utmost to build a side for the future (page 155). Right to the end he fought to keep Overseas alive and for the game to continue in Malta. Perhaps he will succeed with the latter.

From watching David play rugby in Malta, it is my belief that, if he had played for a U.K. team such as Moseley, with their high standard of players, opposition and training, he would have been a class player. Nevertheless, David really enjoyed his rugby.

Charles Borda

For most of his 8 years with the Club Charlie Borda was either Bar Manager or served as a Volunteer Barman. In this he gave valuable service to Overseas especially as from at least 1979 onwards the Club House was a vital link in holding Members, and so the Club, together. Charles, together with his wife Doris, organised the last two Annual Dinner Dances held by the Club.

Charlie's dedication and hard work as Bar Manager was matched only by his love for the game of rugby, and his keenness for anything appertaining to it. He also worked hard to stave off the inevitable closure.
