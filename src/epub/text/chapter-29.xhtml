Foreword (1978-1982) by Cyril Fox

In his foreword to the History of the Overseas Rugby & Sports Club published in 1979, Trevor Ricketts, as the then President of the Club, paid a fitting tribute to Norman Morgan for producing such a wonderful documentary history of the Club since the year of its formation in 1946. This was a veritable encyclopaedia of rugby football played in Malta and showed Norman's dedication and devotion to the sport. Its 183 pages, packed with such detailed information, was a grand tribute to his love of the Club. And now the final Chapter, compiled with his customary meticulous regard for detail to bring us up-to-date with the closing to 1982.

When his duties finally took him away from us in 1976, we were sad indeed to lose him, but though far away he never lost his interest in the Club and now we are more than grateful for this his "swan-song", which will make interesting reading for years to come for those of us who still have fond memories of our days in Malta and the fellowship in sport which we were privileged to enjoy.

Though in my younger years I played hockey rather than rugger, when I retired to Malta in 1968 I found my allegiance changed and I spent many enjoyable hours cheering 'Seas from the touchline and then after the match celebrating (or otherwise) in the Clubhouse.

I sincerely hope that there will be a wide circulation of Norman's book.

Preface (1978-1982)

The history of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Malta, was completed up to and including the Extraordinary General Meeting on the future of the Club held on Thursday, 8th June, 1978. This meeting had been called in view of the fact that the withdrawal of the British Forces and U.K. Based Civilians from Malta, then due to take place on the 31st March, 1979, placed in jeopardy the existence of the Club, and in the extreme, the continuation of the game of rugby on the Island.

The Members at this meeting decided that the Club should continue under the same name, with the same objectives, and that the main emphasis of the Club's activities would revolve around the Rugby Section. Sadly, although with no alternative under the circumstances, the Cricket, Soccer and Hockey Sections were closed.

The very fact that this final chapter to the History of 'Overseas' is written informs readers that, in spite of all efforts by a dedicated few, the Club was finally forced to close. The Closure, or 'Winding-up', took place at the Annual General Meeting held on the 24th October, 1980. This placed the Club under a Caretaker Committee which held its last meeting on the 11th January, 1982.

From the time it was first accepted that the Overseas Club might be forced to close, Overseas Committees have loosely referred to its closure, and events leading up to it, as the 'Winding-up'. In this chapter I have referred to the A.G.M. on the 24th October, 1980, as the 'Winding-up', but not the 'Closure'. From this date the Club was in the hands of a Caretaker Committee, and although the chances were remote, there was still in the hearts of a few the possibility that the Club could be revived. As one Committeeman wrote, "RIP Overseas R.U.F.C. laid to rest end of 1980. Now awaits the resurrection, but doubtful if it can be resuscitated". I have referred to the last Committee Meeting of the Caretaker Committee, and so of the Club, held on the 11th January, 1982, as the 'Closure'.

As in the main history, I have written of events in chronological order within the year they happened. On the other hand, I have completed that part of the history on any subject considered to be of sufficient importance, and covering a number of years, whilst writing about the year in which it was initiated.

The Winding-up, when it came, was due basically to lack of sufficient rugby playing members interested enough to turn out on the day, and of enough teams to give any impetus to the playing of rugby in Malta. There were of course a number of other serious problems which caused the closure. Under the circumstances the knowledgeable believed this to be inevitable, but at the E.G.M. on the 8th June, 1978, there was great determination to succeed, not only to keep the Club alive, but to make rugby a Maltese game. The hard core of dedicated Maltese members, named in that part of the history dealing with the E.G.M.s, supported by the Overseas Committee, made this seem possible. This determination made the season of 1978/79 full of interest and rewarding in the initial interest and keenness shown by the Maltese, specially the schoolboys.
