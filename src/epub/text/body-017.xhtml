There was no shortage of opponents, although then, as now, the only civilian opponents were touring sides. There was a reasonable fleet in Malta, and we played many teams from H.M. ships and Shore Establishments, besides army and R.A.F. Most of our games were played on Sundays. Our standard was good. The highlight of the rugby season was the game against the Mediterranean Fleet. Before proceeding on its Spring cruise to Gibraltar and there playing against the Home Fleet, the Med. Fleet used to play the Overseas XV as a trial match. It was a means of blooding (not literally) the navy team. There is a record of one such match at Tal Qali on the 11th December, 1949, with the score, Overseas 3 - Med. Fleet 8. Overseas used to help the mid- shipmen of the Med. Fleet in the same way.

One person who played occasionally for Overseas between 1949 and 1951 is the present First Sea Lord, Admiral Sir Terence Lewin. Admiral Lewin was a very keen rugby enthusiast, as indeed he still is, and he kindly replied to my enquiry with the following: "I served in HMS Chequers based in Malta from August 1949 until October, 1951. During that time I played for anybody that was running a side and turned out several times for the Overseas, roped in, as far as I can remember, by Bill Wren; this was because my ship was not big enough to run a side of its own. I was, however, at the same time playing for the Flotilla side and also for the Royal Navy."

George Holt writes of the period 1950-51 when Jack Asbury was Team Captain. George has no doubt that under Jack Asbury's leadership the team standard improved. He cites two games in this season when Overseas played against two touring sides, the French Air Force from Tunisia and the Grenadier Guards from Tripoli. The games were watched by the Queen, Princess Margaret and Lord Louis Mountbatten. The French beat Overseas by the odd try and George Holt blames himself for this (one of those memories). The first half was played with the smaller French rugby ball and the second half with larger type that we use. The try was scored when a Frenchman holding the small ball in the palm of his hand waved it in front of George. George was mesmerised by it and tackled the spot where the ball had been, instead of the man.

Information about the Overseas XV in the 1951/52 season comes from a copy of the R.A.F. news sheet called 'Luqa Lens' dated January, 1952. This particular copy was passed on to me by Trev Rickettes, the present President of Overseas, and although it is appertaining to R.A.F. rugby, it is also part of Malta and Overseas rugby, and I quote:-

LUQA LENS

January, 1952

Congratulations to the Station Rugby Team on putting up such a fine showing this season.

Up to date the team has played thirteen matches, won 11 - lost 2, with a total of 228 pts for, and 62 pts against - a very creditable performance indeed.
