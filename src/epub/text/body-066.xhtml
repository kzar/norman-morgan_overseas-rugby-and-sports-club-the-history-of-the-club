Last game of the season, on the 12th March, 1966 Overseas played a touring side (RAF) from Cyprus, losing 6 - 9.

One of the mainstays of the Overseas pack, Peter Cantello, left Malta in March, 1966. During his 2 years' service in Malta, Peter had played for the Royal Navy, captaining a winning side in 1964/65, also for Overseas and St. Angelo. Previously he had played for the R.N. (U.K.) and Hampshire County.

Adrian Wirth, one of the few Maltese players to have played for Overseas up to 1973, won the annual award of the Sam Ward Cup for the most improved player of season 1965/66. It was an exceedingly well earned award, both for the improvement in play and very courageous defence. He had absolutely no fear.

SOCIAL EVENTS

The main recorded Social Events of the playing season were the monthly Film Evenings at Marsa Lodge. These functions proved popular and financially successful, once again thanks to Jack Sharp.

A Christmas Party for the children of Overseas Club Members was held on the 30th December, 1965, at Marsa Lodge. This was organised by Janet Daley, wife of the Social Secretary, and a Ladies Committee formed for the purpose. The Overseas Committee were of the opinion that the venture was an unqualified success. The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the United Services Sports Club, Marsa, on Friday, 25th March, 1966. V. Valente and his band attended. John Hitt (Hitty) was Master of Ceremonies. This was voted another memorable evening. There was a loss of £33. 22 guests were invited.

The Club House remained open during the close season under a Summer Committee.
