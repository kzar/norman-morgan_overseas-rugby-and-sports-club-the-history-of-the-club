1959/60

Annual General Meeting, Queen's Hall, Old College Street, Sliema, 26th October, 1959.

The following Officials were elected:

President Major G.C.D. Scott Lowe, T.D.
Hon. Secretary and Hon. Team Secretary Mr. N.J. Swain
Hon. Treasurer Mr. H.W. Polkinghorne
Hon. Fixture Secretary Mr. C.V. Morris
Hon. Social Secretary Mrs. M. Rushworth
Club House/Bar Manager Mr. G. Rushworth
Team Captain Mr. N.J. Swain

The meeting was presided over by the President and Chaired by Mr. Polkinghorne. A point of interest is the election of a woman to the Office of Social Secretary. This was the first and only time that a woman was elected at the A.G.M. as an Officer of the Club. Later in the Club's history it would have taken a brave man to even propose that a member of the fairer sex be elected.

When Mr. Polkinghorne, Treasurer, gave his statement of accounts for the 1958/59 season it showed a credit balance of £40.10s. A definite improvement on the £11 we started the season with. A great deal of hard work but plenty of fun.

The President, Major G.C.D. Scott Lowe, left Malta in February, 1960.

A Cocktail Party was given in his honour at the residence of Mr. & Mrs. N.J. Swain on the 15th February, 1960. The President was toasted 'Godspeed' and presented with an illuminated vote of thanks, made artistically on vellum by a Tal Handaq artist. The Major was a very kind and human person and Overseas have a great deal to thank him for.

During this season, Viv Morris, Hon. Fixture Secretary, who was a teacher at the R.N. School Tal Handaq, interested the staffs of the Service Schools and the senior pupils at Tal Handaq in the Overseas Club. This was the start of very close ties between the Club and the teachers and pupils. Progressively, as the number of Civilian players on the island declined, the Club became more dependent on support from the schools. In April, 1969, Tal Handaq changed its name to the Services Secondary Comprehensive School and became known as S.C.S. Tal Handaq. In November, 1959, the yearly subscription for schoolboys was set at 2/6d with a match fee of 1/-.

The Hon. Secretary, Mr. N.J. Swain, left Malta in May, 1960 and on the 25th April, 1960, Mr. C.V. Morris took over as Caretaker Secretary. Besides being Hon. Secretary and Team Secretary for the season 1959/60, Jim Swain had been Team Captain for 3 seasons 1957-60, and Fixture Secretary for the 1958/59 season. Quite a character on and off the field. Jim played a great part in reforming Overseas at the beginning of the 1958/59 season.

Playing Season

Permission was given by the R.A.F. for Overseas to use the grounds and changing facilities at Ta' Qali for the 1959/60 season. The season started with a trial at Hal Far on the 28th October. The first game took place at Ta' Qali on the 21st November against the Nomads with Overseas the victors by 6 points to 3 and the final game was on the 5th March when Overseas drew 6-6 with H.M.S. Ark Royal.

From the playing point of view the season was not a success. At a meeting on the 14th December, 1959, the Committee stated how perturbed they were at the continuing losses on the field. In the words of the Hon. Secretary, the Club had taken a pounding on the field of play and it was especially disappointing as the objects of the Club had been adhered to more so than previously. The dearth of playing members was mentioned. It was decided to play all games on a Sunday. This meant that members who were in the Services could play for Overseas regularly and so we could put out a stronger side. Most Service games have always been during the week or on a Saturday. The leading scorer for the season was Geoff Rushworth and this with only 15 points, i.e., 4 tries and one penalty. As a winger of some repute Geoff must rarely have seen the ball except in defence.

The opponents of the 1959/60 season were as follows:

35 Squadron (R.A.F.), Nomads, Hawks (Small units R.A.F.) H.M.S. Ausonia (Depot Ship), H.M.S. Forth (Depot Ship), H.M.S. Falcon (Hal Far), Forts, Royal Fusiliers, 224 Squadron (R.A.F.), 45 Cdo R.M., H.M.S. Daring and H.M.S. Ark Royal. The main point is that at least eight of these teams were resident in Malta at that time and our fixture list has always been based on this. Possibly also one or both of the above mentioned R.A.F. squadrons were based in Malta at the time. Most certainly it was not as impressive a list as at 1946/47, when the Club first started, but the number became less as the years passed and more British Servicemen were withdrawn.

Social Events

Two dances were held during the 1959/60 season at the Phoenicia Hotel. Once again a great deal of hard work by the Social Secretary, Mrs. M. Rushworth, and the Social Committee, Messrs D. Milton, C. Easter and D. Isted. Bad attendance and profit tax cut down profit on the two dances to £5. At this stage in the Club's life one must realize that dances were run, not only for the social side, but also to help to keep the Club solvent. Later in its history the Club could afford to subsidise a dance if necessary. At the end of the season the credit balance of the Club stood at £45.16s

Two very hard working and valuable members of the Club, Geoff Rushworth, Club House Manager, and Margaret Rushworth, Social Secretary, left Malta during the summer of 1960. Both had given a great deal of time and effort into organising social events during the 1959/60 season, and Margaret had served on the Social Committee during the 1958/59 season.
