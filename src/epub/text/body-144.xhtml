A new competition called the 'Government Tourist Board Trophy' was started in 1975 but through forces of circumstances the final, between 41 Cdo and the M.S.C., had to be left for early 1976 Season. The Government Tourist Board Trophy had taken the place of the traditional Governor's Cup Knock-out.

Cricket Section expenses for 1975 were high. To offset this the Club advanced £M40 for local expenses, also £34 Stg. to buy kit in the U.K. All but a marginal amount was repaid. In order to boost Section income the Treasurer, Lew Finnis, organized a weekly raffle so successfully that he raised approximately £M25 over the season. In anticipation of this financial need the match fee had been raised at the start of the season to 30 cents per game and this had raised £M47.40 This effort speaks highly for all members of the Committee and players.

In common with other sections the Cricket Section lost a number of players leaving Malta during the season, these included Pete Wright, the most consistent and highest scoring batsman, also Jim Glover, Malcolm Panter and Dave Snaith. All were hard to replace.
