Playing Season

The 1964/65 season started off with two trial matches, the normal Overseas start of the season Trial Match and a Demonstration/Teaching Match under the new rules coming into force that season. This season the Overseas Trial Match was held at Ta' Qali, versus R.A.F. Luqa, on the 31st October, 1964. The Demonstration Match was organized by the Army (Malta) R.F.U. and all arrangements were made by their Hon. Secretary, Major J. W. S. Dominy. Overseas were invited to attend. The Team Captains of all invited Clubs forwarded the names of 5 players and from these names two XVs were selected and two Captains nominated. Overseas was represented by Messrs. J. Handford, J. Why, P. Henshall, D. Henry and I. O'Brien. The Malta Rugby Referees Panel appointed four referees to take this match and thereby gave the referees an opportunity to try their hand under the new laws. The Demonstration Match was held at the Marsa on the 7th November, 1964.

From the 1st October, 1964, each Thursday evening during the playing season, training facilities were made available at Tal Handaq School gymnasium when John Bowen, the school senior P.E. Instructor, kindly ran training sessions. If Overseas players were not fit it was not John's fault. His circuits were killers (I speak from experience), you had to be fit to get round once. Overseas Club has been indebted to the school Authorities for allowing us these facilities each rugby season since 1964. John personally ran these training sessions for three years. Trevor Ricketts, who succeeded John as Senior P.E. Instructor at Tal Handaq in September, 1967, has continued the Thursday evening training sessions to this date (1976/77). The Club is indeed indebted to John and Trevor.

During this season, up to the 9th January, 1965, Mr. P. Garnons-Williams, Fixture Secretary, gave an excellent press coverage in the local papers, 'Times of Malta' and 'Malta News', on each game in which Overseas participated. Unfortunately his reports faded out after the 9th January. These reports, together with the Team Captain's end of season list of games and results, have been used to record the season's results, also the main scorers. In his report on the opening match against the Duke of Edinburgh Royal Regiment Wanderers, Mr. Garnons-Williams said that, unlike previous seasons, the majority of our players were fit. In his end of season report the Team Captain said 'for the first time ever the Overseas were amongst the fittest on the field. The team owes a great deal to John Bowen for giving up his time to achieve this.'

On the 31st January, 1965 Overseas entered two Invitation Teams in the Navy 7's Tournament. In the final of the Cup, Overseas 'A' lost to Falcon 'B' by 8 points to 3, and in the final of the Plate, Overseas 'B' lost to St. Angelo by 24 points to nil. We also entered two teams in the Inter Service 7's on the 27th February, 1965, with not quite as impressive results.

Throughout the season Overseas had a fairly strong and consistent XV. After a slow start the forwards improved, with Jack Handford (Team Captain) and Peter Henshall most often mentioned in dispatches, whilst the back division was strong and potentially always dangerous. The backs mentioned in the Team Captain's report on the 14th January, 1965, were Brian Jackson (Senior), full back, David Agar, wing, John Evans and Ken Lawrence (Centre or stand off) and Keith Donavon, scrum half. Also mentioned were the good reserves available over the Christmas holidays with the arrival of Roger Wilson, Jim O'Doire and Patrick Hamilton-Hill, not forgetting Brian Jackson (Junior) (no relation of Brian Jackson senior) who was a very good stand-in as scrum half. The Captain expressed the opinion that the Club was in for a record season and today (14th January) one record had been broken when Brian Jackson (senior) had scored 108 points. The Team Captain's end of season report confirmed that Overseas played much improved rugby winning 17 out of their 26 games, which beat their previous best season of 10 wins in 1962/63, and scored 467 points with 167 against.

Fortunately, although no final complete results of player's averages is available, Brian Jackson has been able to confirm that he scored the record number of 187 points in the season (1964/65), including 13 tries. 13 tries from the full back position is pretty good. Brian also confirmed that David Agar scored 12 tries during the same season although there is no record of David's final points tally.

Ralph Daley was awarded the Sam Ward Cup as the most improved player of the season. Ralph McGillivray, Overseas Team Captain of 1961/62, was selected to referee the Inter Service match on the 13th February, 1965, between the Army (Malta) and the Navy (Malta).

At the start of the season concern was shown by Overseas General Committee in the shortage of players. This problem has always been with us, increasingly so each year as the number of Service personnel stationed on the Island has decreased. The Overseas Secretary, Vivian Morris, wrote to a number of local firms, including Swan & Hunters, Shell Malta, Cable & Wireless, R.F.A’s, and also to the Personnel Officer of the Malta Drydocks, trying to interest members of their staff rugbywise. The Secretary also wrote to the Rector of St. Edward’s College trying to interest the Maltese Students in the game, with no success. Fortunately, as has already been shown by the number of reserves arriving during season 1964/65, the team was considerably strengthened.

Social Events

The innovation of Film Evenings for members and ladies proved a success. Through the kind services of Mr. J. Sharp, Overseas President, in providing films, equipment and projectionist, four Film Evenings were held at the Club House between November and March. All were well attended and overall were financially successful. Buffet arrangements were made by Mr. Ben Bush.

A successful Christmas Draw made a handsome profit of £29. Once again Ben Bush provided the buffet. A Stag Hot Pot Supper was held at the Club House on the 4th February, 1965. This was judged to be an excellent evening.

Games Nights were held at the W.O.'s & Sgts Mess, Mtarfa, on the 16th January and on the 10th April, 1965, also at the Ordnance Social Club, Ospizio, on the 20th March, 1965. The second games night at Mtarfa was in the nature of a reciprocation by the Overseas R.F.C. who were unable to act as hosts at Marsa Lodge. No reason was given.

The Playing season social activities ended with the Annual Dinner Dance at the United Services Sports Club, Marsa on Friday, 2nd April, 1965. Joe Camilleri's band attended. Once again a high number of Official Guests were invited, fourteen in all, and resulted in a loss of £12 against the loss of £34 the previous season.

The Social Secretary, Mr. D. Henry left Malta during April, 1965, and he was succeeded by Ben Bush who was co-opted onto the General Committee for the summer months.

Initially, it was proposed to close the Club House during the close season as from the end of April, 1965. However, because of the keenness of members to keep the Club open, the General Committee authorised and set up a Sub-Committee to be responsible to the Main Committee for all activities which took place during the summer. This Sub-Committee has already been mentioned under the heading "Club House - Marsa Lodge". All functions arranged during the summer were well attended including the normal Thursday evenings. The purchases of a large refrigerator from M.P.B.W., Imriehel, for £20, at the beginning of the summer undoubtedly contributed to the success of the venture.

Overseas Cricket Section

During the summer months of 1965 an Overseas Cricket Team was formed. A previous attempt had been made in 1964 but the idea had been dropped through lack of support and lack of cricket gear. Initially, the cricket team was formed as an activity to keep members of the 'Rugger Club' in touch throughout the summer months. Indeed, the 'games of cricket at the Marsa' helped to make the summer social life at the Club House during this period such a success. A meeting of all Overseas members interested in cricket took place at Marsa Lodge on the 6th May, 1965, when a Captain and two Vice-Captains were elected and a Selection Committee formed. For this first season games were played outside the league programme.

The pioneers mainly responsible for the formation of the Cricket Team Section were, amongst others, John Osman in 1964, and Peter Ratcliffe (affectionately known as 'Ratty'), Brian Jackson (Jacko), Ralph Daley, David Agar, Peter Cantello, Jack Handford, John Evans, Jack Roberts, Vic Jones, Peter Garnons-Williams, and big Ben Bush, in 1965. Not forgetting the General Committee behind them.

Following is a copy of Ben's Letter:

30th April, 1965

Dear Members,

On Thursday, May 6th, 1965, there will be a meeting at Marsa Lodge of all members interested in the formation of an Overseas Cricket Team. This will not be a separate club but an activity to keep members of the Rugger Club in touch throughout the summer months.

Business to be discussed includes the election of a captain, vice-captain and selection committee, availability of players, grounds and fixtures, etc.

I should be grateful if you would complete and return the attached form at your earliest convenience, to give some idea of the numbers likely to attend.

I should like to take this opportunity to introduce myself as the new Social Secretary. I am taking over from Derek Henry, who is leaving the island, and I will endeavour to uphold the standards set by him.

At an Overseas General Committee Meeting on the 16th March, 1966, an 'Official' Cricket Section was formed and it was agreed that all new members of the Club would pay a composite subscription to cover the Rugby and Cricket Sections. The Cricket Section to be administered by a Sub-Committee composed of a Chairman and two other officers to be elected by the Cricket Section. Peter Ratcliffe was elected the first Chairman. In his account of the 1966 summer season, Ratty said that the main item was the entry of the cricket team into the Combined Services League, which Overseas won. Between 1965 and 1970 the Cricket Section was 'sponsored' by the summer bar takings although all expenditure was sanctioned by the main Committee. In March, 1970, Mr. J. Christie, Cricket Section Chairman, requested a grant of £30 per annum as a subsidy from the main Club to the Cricket Section. The main Committee agreed that the Cricket Section should be considered as a Sub-Section and that any reasonable expenditure necessitated by this Section would be viewed with consideration by the General Committee, also that for the year 1970 the Cricket Section be allowed to purchase equipment to the value of £30. From this point (1970 season) the Cricket Section, whilst accepting a subsidy from the main Club at the start of each season to cover immediate expenses, always attempted to be self-supporting by covering this subsidy from match subscriptions and other methods of fund raising.

The Cricket Section, as a Section of Overseas, was temporarily disbanded between the 30th December, 1971, and the 21st September, 1972, due to the British Services withdrawal. In the summer of 1973, Overseas Cricket Team was back in action once again and in this season became Malta League Champions. Also in 1973 season a kind donor presented the Cricket Section with £50 and enabled them to more than return the £30 subsidy for that year. From January, 1972, the Marsa Club came under the management of the Maltese and its name was changed from the United Services Sports Club to the Malta Sports Club. Cricket, with its centre at the Marsa, came under the control of the Malta Sports Club Cricket Section Committee. Although it is for the Cricket Section to tell of their fortunes on the field, it must be stated, that like the Rugby Section, the Cricket Section has made a great contribution to sport in Malta, and helped to make 'Overseas' a respected name.
