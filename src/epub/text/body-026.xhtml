PART II

1958/59

REFORMING OVERSEAS ON A CLUB BASIS

During the close season, on the 20th May, 1958, the Hon. Secretary of the Overseas, Captain Jim Osbourne, left Malta. During the evening of the 20th he called at my house whilst I was at work and left all the worldly possessions of the 'Overseas' in my care. My wife suggested that he should wait until I came home and ask me himself if I wanted to be 'Caretaker Secretary' before he left. Jim blithely told my wife that he was in transit that night and he was off. When I returned home from work I was the Caretaker Secretary, like it or not. The Club assets at that time consisted of £11 in cash, two sets of jerseys, two rugby balls, a battered type- writer, a lacer, pump and some forms. The only Minutes left with me were those of a single meeting held on the 27th November, 1954.

With no Committee to work with, and no Service attachment, I decided immediately to reform Overseas on a Club basis, to the same ideals and outlook as I had known in 1948/50. Perhaps this had been in Jim Osbourne's mind when he left Overseas in my care. Fortunately, such rugby stalwarts as Major G.C.D. Scott Lowe, Jim Swain, the previous year Team Captain, and Jim Stalworthy were around at the right time. The Major, Army R.U. (Malta) Chairmen, readily agreed to be President of the Overseas Club. Circulars re Overseas, with the Major's authority, were put out to Service Units and at the same time contact was made with Civilian firms such as Wimpeys. At that time the main Wimpey construction unit was at Has Saptan.

It is a peculiarity of rugby in Malta, that even as Servicemen like to join the Overseas, the only civilian club on the island, the very existence of a civilian club depends so much on the help of the Services and also on Servicemen joining the Club. For instance, all rugby pitches were on Service property. We in the Overseas have been thankful of the good relations which have always existed.

The first meeting of the 1958/59 season was held at 77, Grenfell Street, St. Julians, on the 6th September, 1958. The three members present were Messrs. N.C. Morgan, Secretary, N.J. Swain, Fixture Secretary, and J. Stalworthy, Committee Member. Jim Stalworthy turned down the Office of Treasurer, but agreed to act as Chairman. At this meeting it was agreed to drop the issue of inscribed tankards to those leaving the Club and so enabling subscriptions to be lowered. The second meeting was held on the 11th October, same address, when we were joined by Bill Polkinghorne, another rugby stalwart. Bill agreed to act as Treasurer. At the second meeting it was decided, that although it was only by nominating ourselves as Officials of the Club could it be restarted on a 'Club Basis', we must legalize ourselves in positions held, by being elected at a General Meeting, or others elected to take over. It was therefore decided to call an Annual General Meeting at the first available date.
