Playing Season

The President, Jim Christie, and the Team Captain, Mike Pearey, both stated that 1970/71 was a most successful season, and that we were, throughout the whole season, the best team on the Island. This was in spite of the fact that we were desperately short of players, we were plagued by injuries, and we lost three of our key players in January. In February we were down to 16 playing members. Jim Christie summed it up when he stated, "The Rugby team under Mike Pearey had a successful season, and his experience, tactics, and sense of discipline, were reflected on the field of play." Unfortunately, for Overseas, Mike Pearey left Malta at the end of the 1970/71 playing season. Mike represented the Royal Navy on the R.F.U. Committee and before leaving Malta he had been appointed to sit on the RFU Sub-Committee on laws.

Training started on the 1st October, 1970, at Tal Handaq under the direction of Trevor Ricketts, and the first game of the season was on the 24th October against H.M.S. Ark Royal. At the start of the season 22 players were in regular training. Wednesday and Saturday were ball training sessions, with Thursday evening being fitness training. As far as I know, this is the only season in the Club's history that such strict training has been carried out. I was told that on one occasion Charlie Rice was dropped for not attending training, this says a lot for Captain and players and it reflected on the Club's record for the season, losing only once to a resident team, the R.A.F. and we in turn defeated them twice.

The Team Captain in his end of season report mentioned only one player. He described Derek Kiddle, from a Captain's point of view, as the ideal player, never missing a training session or a game, always on good time, and always going hard. The President, when particularly referring to people leaving, mentioned B. Jackson, a grand stalwart of all sports and of the Overseas Club, Peter Miller, whose forceful runs down the touchline thrilled all, and Derek Kiddle, who tackled all he did with the utmost vigour, whether on the field or in the Club. John Symes won the Cup for the 'Most Improved Player' of the year.

For the fourth successive year John Evans brought a touring party to Malta from South Wales, on this occasion from the 8th to 14th April, 1971. This time, due to the rundown of the Services, the Army (Malta) authorities did not give permission for them to be accommodated in the Services Transit Accommodation Lintorn Barracks, Floriana. The party of 20 booked themselves into flats. John Evans XV played against Overseas on Saturday, 10th April, and against the Combined Services on Monday, 12th April. No record remains of the names of players and officials in the party, or of the results and scores. Only because the teams which Overseas lost to during the season were named, do I know that Overseas were victors in the Overseas/John Evans XV game. In spite of this, John Evans spoke of it as being an excellent tour. This was the last tour that John Evans' party made to Malta. During their four tours they made a great contribution to rugby on the Island, they played magnificent entertaining rugby, and Overseas has much to thank them for, particularly John Evans.

The Army (Cyprus) under Major P. Winchcombe, R.E.M.E., toured Malta in December, 1970 playing against Overseas on 5th December. The R.A.F. (Cyprus) toured Malta in January, 1971, playing against Overseas on the 30th January. Overseas lost to the Army 17 points to 0, and the R.A.F. 30 points to 0.

The Tours Secretary, Eddie McClure, also carried out correspondence with Clifton R.U.F.C., and the Secretary or Manager of the Paris Team, Didier Dorsemaine, brother of Philippe, former Overseas Player. Both these teams expressed a wish to tour Malta at Easter, 1972, and it was proposed by Overseas Committee to hold a Rugby Festival over the period covered by their intended visits. John Evans also expressed a wish to take part in the Festival. It was further proposed that a Committee, consisting of two Overseas Members, T.N. Ricketts, and M. Lewis, and three other Members, one from each of the Services, be formed to prepare the Festival. Major R. Hurley, Chairman Army R.U. (Malta), agreed that this was an excellent proposal and stated that he would suggest at the following Committee Meeting of the Inter Service Rugby Union (Malta) that it be accepted. This was not to be. With the withdrawal of the British Forces in January, 1972, and the consequent temporary disbandment of Overseas R.U.F.C., no rugby was played on the Island during the second half of season 1971/72.

Social Events 1970/71

Once again the films provided by Jack Sharp proved to be the mainstay of the Club's Social life during the season. Film evening took place at the Club House once or twice a month. Two Cheese and Wine Parties were held. The first on Wednesday, 18th November, 1970, held mainly for the R.A.F. and H.M.S. Ark Royal. The attendance was very poor, only 10 full members being present. The second on Thursday, 8th April, 1971, held for John Evans Touring Party, was a success. A Hot Pot was provided for the Army (Cyprus) Touring side.

In November, 1970, the Inter Service R.U., Malta, asked the Overseas Committee to make a grant to the Inter Services (Malta) to help cover the cost of entertainment of visiting teams etc.. Their point of view was that Overseas Rugby Club had the benefits of a Service maintained ground and Club House, also that with the rundown of the Services there was very little money available for allocation to rugby from Service non-public funds in Malta. The Army, for example, had only £200 to cover all sports for a full year. At a meeting held in January, 1971, Overseas Committee agreed that, of the expenses incurred by the Malta XVs which could not be met by any other means, one third would be paid by the Overseas Club, the Club's contribution for entertainment of any one team not to exceed four pounds.

The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the United Services Sports Club on the 23rd April, 1971. In spite of the fact that the total Membership was stated in Committee on the 18th February, 1971, as being as low as 44, the dance was attended by 147 paying guests, and proclaimed to be a tremendous success. The dance was organised by Mr. R. Walker and Committee, and for the second year in succession, Trevor Ricketts acted as Master of Ceremonies.
