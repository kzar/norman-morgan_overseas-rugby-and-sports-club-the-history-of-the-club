This was the first A.G.M. to be held as the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club and Officials elected at this meeting were concerned only with the general running of the Club. Committee and Section reports were submitted.

In his address the retiring President, Mr. C. Fox, said that this year had seen a dramatic change in the formation of the Overseas Club, from being mainly a Rugby Club to that of a Rugby and Sports Club. Mr. Fox also said that this step had, during the course of the year, proved to be a means of enhancing the name of Overseas, and for the first time for many a year, we can boast membership of almost a century. In fact, the membership at the end of the 1973/74 season stood at 68 players, of all sections, and 28 social.

As expected, such a change in the formation of the Club had its teething troubles and, where possible, consequent changes in the Rules to help to correct them.

The Rules were changed as follows:

Rule 6 amended to read

Each Section shall be governed by its own Committee which shall consist of: Chairman, Hon. Secretary, Hon. Treasurer, Captain, and Vice-Captain. The Officers to hold office for one year and be elected by Members at the Annual General Meeting of each Section. Each Section Committee shall be fully accountable to the General Committee for its management and finance and its Chairman and Hon. Secretary shall be Officers of the General Committee.

Rule 12 amended to read

Membership Fees:

  1. Playing Member: Annual Subscription £M1

  2. Social Member: Annual Subscription £M1

  3. Student Member: Annual Subscription 50cents

  4. Temporary Membership: 10cents/match

a) and b) only have full voting rights

Addition to Rule 2

2(d) Student Members who are still in full time education who wish to become Playing Members of the Club.

One of the initial problems facing the Club, in giving notice of the first Annual General Meetings of the General Committee and of the different sections, was that the majority of Members were interested in the General Committee A.G.M. and also one or more section A.G.M.s. This particularly concerned Members of the Rugby Section, the largest faction of the Club. The different notices would be addressed in many cases to the same individual Members. This could mean as many as four notices going out, and also there was the difficulty in trying to fit the meetings of the Rugby, Soccer and Hockey Sections in before the main A.G.M. This is accentuated by the fact that a big percentage of Members, including Committee Members, are Schoolmasters and are away from the Island during the summer months, returning early September.

The Club rules as formulated at the 1973 A.G.M. did not specifically state that each Section must hold its own A.G.M., hence the already mentioned change to Rule 6. No separate notice for the Rugby Section A.G.M. of 1974 was sent out, but advantage was taken of the word 'Rugby' in the name of the Club, i.e. anyone interested in Rugby would attend the main Club A.G.M., after having been informed of such. In fact, all Members attending the main A.G.M. in 1974, and who were interested in Rugby, were asked to attend the Rugby Section A.G.M. immediately following. For the following year (1975) this was resolved by the General Secretary sending out one notice to all Members giving dates of the different Annual General Meetings. The same information was broadcast over the B.F.B.S. and the local radio, also inserted in the local press and the Dolphin & Eagle.

'Overseas' Funds, United Kingdom and Malta

During this season it was increasingly felt by Overseas Members, in view of the belief that Overseas might cease to exist once the British presence in Malta ended in March, 1979, that the Club funds, now in the U.K. in Cdr. M. Pearey's name, should be utilised for the benefit of the Club at the present time. Consequently, following decisions made in Committee, the Hon. General Treasurer Mr. D. Taylor, wrote to Cdr. Pearey asking him to make arrangements to transfer the Club monies, then in his name, to a bank accessible to and under control of the Hon. General Treasurer. Accordingly, Cdr. Pearey made arrangements and following resolutions passed in the General Committee on the 24th March, 1975, an account was opened with Lloyds Bank, Cox's and King's Branch, Pall Mall, London, in the name of Overseas Rugby and Sports Club (Malta) with a starting balance of £231.37. The account was immediately made use of to purchase training films from the R.F.U. Twickenham (£36.30) and for Cricket balls (£34.20).

As previously stated in this history under year 1959, Overseas first opened an account in Malta with Barclays Bank on the 23rd March, 1959. This account was closed and transferred to the National Bank of Malta in approximately 1964 until it was transferred back to Barclays Bank International in December, 1972. The account was held with Barclays Bank International, Sliema, until this bank ceased to operate as such in Malta on the 30th September, 1975. On the 1st October, 1975, the banking business of Barclays Bank International in Malta and Gozo, and the account of Overseas, was transferred to Mid-Med Bank Limited, a Company registered in Malta. The Barclays Bank (Transfer of Business) Act 1975 was specially enacted by the Maltese Parliament for the purpose of facilitating the transfer of the business to Mid-Med Bank Limited and to ensure the smooth continuance of that business. The new bank had a share capital subscribed as 60% Malta Government and 40% by Barclays Bank International Limited.

Election of Life Member - Trevor Ricketts

At the Annual General Meeting on the 19th September, 1974 Trevor Ricketts was honoured by being elected an Hon. Life Member of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club. It was an honour well deserved. Mr. C. Fox, the retiring President, said of Trevor 'Your Committee feel that there is a Member of the Club without whose unremitting hard work, helpful guidance and efficient organization we would not be held in such high regard in the sporting world of the Island. No Club can be successful without a dedicated Hon. Secretary and we have such a Secretary who has given up a tremendous amount of his time during the past 6 years in the running of our Club'.

Besides his contribution to sport in general in Malta, Trevor had served Overseas continuously, in one office or another, since arriving in Malta in September, 1967, and played for, or had played for, the 1st teams of all four Sections of the Club. He will always be remembered for his sterling work in reforming Overseas after the temporary withdrawal of the British Forces from Malta early 1972, and for his being the prime mover in Overseas becoming a Sports Club in September, 1973. Trevor also played a great part in training youth and teaching rugby to the pupils at the Maltese Colleges.

Mr. N. Armstrong, Team Captain, described 1974/75 as a very successful season, as a Club we had a high percentage of players selected for the Malta XVs against touring sides. Again we ran two teams with most of the 1st and 'B' Team games taking place on Sundays and Saturdays respectively. Field training for the season, on the traditional Thursday evenings, started on the 24th September continuing until the evenings darkened earlier. Fitness training then took over, under the instruction of John Hodgeon, in Tal Handaq Gymnasium. The first games were at the Marsa on the 12th October, 'B' Team vs H.M.S.  Ark Royal Colts, and on the 13th October, 1st team vs H.M.S. Hampshire. Both comfortable wins for Overseas. Throughout the season 1st team opposition proved difficult to obtain, the already difficult situation of shortage of local teams being worsened by the fact that 41 Commando Unit was temporarily serving in Cyprus. Of the 15 games played by the 1st team only 4 were against local opposition, and this included playing R.A.F. 'A' 3 times. The other local opposition, being 79 Kirkee Battery. 'B' Team opposition was easier to find, 7 out of 16 games being against local opposition. For the first time, at least since Overseas started entering the competition in 1961/62, and excepting for 1971/72 during the withdrawal, the Inter Service 7's were not held. This was mainly because the army rugby was disorganized due to 41 Cdo serving in Cyprus.

The 'B' team, ably led by Tim Agius-Ferrante, again gave a good account of itself, but how the make up of the team had changed! During the previous season (1973/74) the Club had 25 Student Members, mostly from S.C.S. Tal Handaq, who, strengthened by a number of older and more experienced players, had formed the 'B' team. In 1974/75 only one Student Member remained, most of the other had returned to the U.K., and the 'B' team took on a different complex. The main strength came from the staff of Tal Handaq and local U.K. Servicemen, but to the great pleasure of the Overseas Committee, a number of Maltese, one or two quite experienced, showed an interest in playing and learning the game. A great deal of credit for this must go to the 'B' team Captain, Tim Agius-Ferrante, himself an ex Head Boy of Tal Handaq, to Marcel Cassar and David Schembri, also to the comradeship which was built up in the previous season.

Overseas Representatives on the Joint Services Rugby Committee for 1974/75 were the Team Captain, Niall Armstrong, and the Junior Member Representative, Jim Masuwale.

Once again, Jack Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, worked hard behind the scenes arranging with the Secretary of the National Sports Board, Mr. Colin Cauchi, for Overseas to use the rugby pitches at the Marsa. Two pitches remained. We were guaranteed use of the main pitch (Ex Navy no 1), running parallel to and about 100 yards from the Stadium, for the season, and use of the smaller pitch, below the main pitch and parallel to the golf course, for the start of the season. The smaller pitch had been used mainly for the 'B' team games. Unfortunately, early March, 1975, we lost the 'B' team pitch completely and the main pitch was re-sited by moving it approximately 15 yards nearer to the Stadium. This situation arose when part of the rugby playing area was taken to enlarge the golf course. To mark the new boundary for the golf course a fence was erected across the 'B' team pitch, continuing along the length of and a number of yards inside the main pitch, right up to the existing fence which guarded the ditch running through the Marsa. Work on the new boundary fence was already in progress when Dave Snaith, Overseas Grounds Manager, went to mark the pitches on Friday, 7th March, and by this time it was across the 'B' team pitch and into the dead ball line of the main pitch. Jack Sharp immediately contacted the National Sports Board and managed to delay work on the fence until the following week, thereby allowing the 1st team game arranged for Sunday, 9th March, to be played, and also for the main pitch to be re-sited before completion of the fence.

A great deal of hard work was put in, and time given, by Dave Snaith and also the Team Captain, Niall Armstrong, in looking after the grounds, especially when the one ground was lost to us and the main pitch, now the only remaining pitch at the Marsa, was re-sited. Both were very active in attempts to have the pitch re-seeded, without success. Fortunately, the pitch was watered by the National Sports Board throughout the summer of 1975 and so the pitch was kept in good condition without seeding.

Rugby in Malta during the second half of the 1974/75 season was enriched by three touring sides from the U.K. and one from Sicily, namely, Bedford Athletic, Pembroke, Moseley, and the Messina Rugby Clan, and the season ended with a feast of good rugby. Arrangements for the visits of the teams from the U.K. were mostly made by the Joint Services Rugby Committee. However, the ground work for Moseley's visit was done by one of our Vice-Presidents, Mr. D. Alexander, himself a Member of the Moseley Club. Moseley, a superbly drilled and strong side were easily the best of the visiting teams and remained unbeaten during their tour. When Moseley beat a Malta XV by 26 points to 6 on the 2nd April, the score represented the difference between the two teams. The team from Sicily, the Messina Rugby Clan, toured Malta mainly because of their own insistence in making the trip to play Overseas. Initially, Overseas approached Messina University (CUS) for Overseas to make a tour to Sicily with the possibility of CUS making a return visit. Unfortunately, CUS replied saying it was impractical this season and at the same time they informed Messina Rugby Clan of our interest. When Messina Rugby Clan approached Overseas asking if they could tour Malta at Easter, 1975, they refused to be put off when informed that we already had a full programme with other touring sides at that time. In answer to a last minute telegram from Overseas Team Captain they sent their own telegram which said 'Possibility Tournament with visitors and yourselves or armed forces? Visit already programmed will arrive 28th (March) evening'. Arrive they did. Overseas put out a 13-a-side to play them and although 'Seas beat them 34 points to 12, they played entertaining rugby and enjoyed their visit. They lacked the experience of the Overseas XV. Provisional arrangements were made for Overseas to visit Sicily January, 1976. The referees were organized by Mr. D. Snaith, and included in the panel of referees was Mr. D. Davis who accompanied the Bedford Athletic party.

In the 1974/75 season Tal Handaq did not have the playing strength to provide opposition for the Exiles during the Christmas holidays. Two games were arranged, Exiles versus an Overseas 'B' Team, for Saturday, 28th December, and Wednesday, 1st January. The first ended in a 10 - 10 draw and the second with an easy win for the Exiles 26 - 0. Major Ian Robinson, O.I.C. 234 Signal Squadron, his son an Exile, together with Dave Taylor for Overseas, organized the series.

At the Annual Dinner Dance on the 19th April, 1975, the 'Most Improved Player' cup was presented to David Schembri, and the 'Young Player of the Year' Cup to Tim Agius-Ferrante. It was great satisfaction to the Club that the awards were won by Maltese players. It was also a pleasure that David, a regular 1st team player, was selected to play on occasions for the Malta XV.

The Marsa was proving to be a popular area for the Maltese people on a Sunday afternoon. The main attraction was of course the horse racing, but many made use of the playing area where the rugger pitches are situated, some I am pleased to say even stopping to watch what is a 'foreign' game to them. The National Sports Board started to control the use of the area. At the beginning of November, 1974, Overseas Members were issued with permit cards by the N.S.B. allowing them to use the 'Marsa Sports Club Rugger Pitches' and so the Marsa Car parking area by Marsa Lodge. The permits were signed by Mr. C. Cauchi, for the N.S.B., and Mr. T. N. Ricketts, Overseas General Secretary. For the 1974/75 season no serious effort was made to control cars entering the Marsa playing area from the Marsa end and these permits were never asked for on entry.

At the end of January, 1975, one of our most popular and 'colourful' characters, namely, Usaia Masuwale, known to all as Jim, left Malta. Jim, a Fijian, always with a great sense of humour and with an interest in all sports, gave a great deal of his time in representing and coaching the Junior Members in rugby. At the end of the season we lost many Rugby Section Members, including Peter Joerning, Vice-Captain and Social Secretary, Dave Snaith, Grounds Manager and referee, and Jim Glover, Tours Secretary and all rounds sportsman. All men hard to replace, both as Officials and and players, and for his ability on the field perhaps none more than Jim Glover. Approximately 15 Section Members left Malta at the end of the season or during the close season, too many to mention all by name, but all a loss to the Club.

For the Soccer Section the season was one of mixed fortunes. In the Major League we were unable to maintain our high standard and managed only 7 points from 8 games. This can be attributed in part to the fact that the league programme was affected by the withdrawal of 41 Commando Group. The Team Captain, Jim Glover, reported in December, 1974, that games were only every two weeks (due to the withdrawal of 41 Cdo), the league was in a shambles (at the time of the report), and this was showing in our play. In spite of this, under his Captaincy, they played entertaining football and the team spirit remained high.

In the Minor League it was a different story. Against strong opposition, especially from Engineering Wing and 203 Squadron, we remained unbeaten winning 15 out of 16 games, finishing the season as champions of Division I. The only point dropped was in a 4 - 4 draw with Ops. Wing.

The Minor Units Cup Competition was run more on a League basis than a Knock-out. After a good win over 840 Signals Unit in the semi-final, 5 goals to 3, we were crushed by Engineering Wing in the final, 5 goals to nil.

The main goal-scorers were again Pete Wright, who finished the season with a tally of 37, and Jeff Bonner with a creditable 18. 19 players appeared for the team during the season, 9 of these playing more than 15 of the 21 games. Pete Wright and Mike Novak were the two ever-presents in the side.

During the close season of 1975 the Section lost at least 4 good players in Jim Glover (Team Captain), Peter Wright (Vice-Captain), Mike Novak and Malcolm Panter, the first 3 being regular 1st team players. Pete Wright had been Secretary for two years, 1973-75, and Malcolm Panter had been Treasurer for at least the year 1973/74.

My thanks to Trevor Ricketts, Soccer Section Chairman, for details of the Soccer Section results for 1974/75. In his end of season report Trevor said the following:

"The Team spirit of the side was again very high and thanks for this are due to Jim Glover who skippered so well. Also in his absence I would like to thank Pete Wright who has organized the Club (Soccer Section) as Secretary so well for the last two seasons. We hope his successor will be able to maintain his high standard. A vote of thanks must also go to our Treasurer, Gerry Porter. It is always satisfying to be able to report to the main Committee that not only has the Soccer Section been self supporting but has in actual fact made a profit this year. Finally, I would like to thank Jeff Bonner for his work in organizing all the Social Functions."

The forced withdrawal of 41 Commando from sporting activities in Malta at the start of the season affected the Hockey Section considerably, perhaps even more than the other Sections. Even without this problem it was difficult to build up a complete fixture list. The Joint Services Hockey League was a scrappy affair. Initially the commando withdrawal left only 5 teams to contest the title, although from the final league table published in the Dolphin & Eagle it would appear that the Commandos did complete the fixture list with a weakened team. For Overseas the league programme proved hard going with many players committed in other sports. In summing up Overseas play and difficulties in the League, Dave Taylor, Section Chairman, said, to quote "As usual the Overseas fielded a changed XI for each fixture, those who turned out were those who happened to be available at short notice. On occasions some good Hockey was played, but generally we lacked the smooth teamwork of the Services' sides. Nevertheless, we enjoyed our games. In the final league table we were placed 4/6, having won 1, lost 3 and taken 1 W.O."

In the Joint Services 6 a-side Competition held on Sunday, 9th March, 1975, Overseas gave a creditable performance. We were beaten 1 goal to nil in the quarter finals by 234 Signal Squadron, the eventual winners. Dave Ditcham, Hockey Section Secretary, describes our performance in this competition as our best during the 2 seasons 1973/74 and 1974/75. Fourteen teams entered and these were split into two leagues of seven teams, the top three teams in each league going into a Knock-out competition. It was a long competition, and those who played in the final played a total of 2 hours 8 minutes actual play. The Cup was presented by Rear-Admiral David Loram.

As with all Sections of Overseas, the Hockey Section had to take stock of players who had returned to U.K. during or since the previous season. For the 1974/75 season we had lost our Captain, Rob Ward, and Mike Lewis, but gained other players in Dave Walker, Dave Longlands and Martin Crossfield. Barrie Jones and Mike Newton also put in appearances.

At the beginning of the season Overseas applied to join the Malta Hockey League. This was turned down because of the rule which said that no side in this league could field more than two non-Maltese. According to Dave Ditcham this rule was introduced to prevent local sides getting hold of all the best Services players and to prevent the R.N., R.A.F. and the Army entering teams.

During this period a ladies' team called 'Overseas' played in the Ladies League, and although they were not connected with the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, I think readers will be interested in learning something about them. The ladies did consider applying to become a section of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club in September, 1973, but decided against it at the time. The Ladies Overseas began as an effort by Maureen Kearns (now Mrs. Whittle) and Marylin Fletcher (now Mrs. Manley-Harris), to organize a Ladies League to give the Tal Handaq Schoolgirls' team some competitive sport. A Ladies League operated in both 1973/74 and 1974/75 season. Overseas ladies team won in the former and the Wrens in the latter with Overseas runners-up. Other teams were entered by Mtarfa wives, Hospital, and Tal Handaq entered two teams Tal Handaq 'A' and 'B'. Half of the Overseas team was from Luqa. Notable players included Pat Greenwood, Hilary Miller, Norma Lovett and Janet Campbell (all Luqa), Marjorie Ridsdale (D.O.E.) and Bridget Lowery. Mrs. Ditcham was the organizer. It was all a most loose arrangement started by adverts in Dolphin & Eagle and on B.F.B.S. - but most successful.

Once again I must thank Dave Ditcham and Dave Taylor for details of the Hockey Section, also Dave Ditcham for details of the Ladies Overseas. My thanks also to the Dolphin & Eagle for details of the Joint Services 6 a-side competition and for final results of the league table.

Overseas were fortunate to retain most players from the successful season of 1974, and again to be Captained by Trevor Ricketts. We did not reach the height of the previous seasons but nevertheless acquitted ourselves well, being runners-up in both the league and in the 6 a-side competition.

Net practice took place at the Malta Sports Club on Tuesday evenings at 1600 commencing on the 1st April, and the season started with the Malta Cricket League 6 a-side competition on the 26th/27th April. For the 6 a-side Overseas entered four teams out of a total of sixteen taking part. The eight first round games were played on Saturday, 26th, and the second round, semi-finals and final on Sunday, 27th.

The Malta Cricket League started on the 3rd May and ended on the 10th August, although the last game for Overseas was on the 19th July, all games taking place at the Marsa. Seven teams took part, all playing each other twice. During the games each side was restricted to 36 overs batting and no bowler could bowl more than eight overs. Perhaps the best game for Overseas was against the previously unbeaten Puffins on the 15th July. Set a target of 162 runs Overseas knocked them off for a loss of 4 wickets. The two Overseas batsmen in form were Pete Wright, 87 not out, and Jim Glover, 43.

A new competition called the 'Government Tourist Board Trophy' was started in 1975 but through forces of circumstances the final, between 41 Cdo and the M.S.C., had to be left for early 1976 Season. The Government Tourist Board Trophy had taken the place of the traditional Governor's Cup Knock-out.

Cricket Section expenses for 1975 were high. To offset this the Club advanced £M40 for local expenses, also £34 Stg. to buy kit in the U.K. All but a marginal amount was repaid. In order to boost Section income the Treasurer, Lew Finnis, organized a weekly raffle so successfully that he raised approximately £M25 over the season. In anticipation of this financial need the match fee had been raised at the start of the season to 30 cents per game and this had raised £M47.40 This effort speaks highly for all members of the Committee and players.

In common with other sections the Cricket Section lost a number of players leaving Malta during the season, these included Pete Wright, the most consistent and highest scoring batsman, also Jim Glover, Malcolm Panter and Dave Snaith. All were hard to replace.

Social Events 1974/75

The social life of the Club continued to thrive with a variety of events arranged by the different sections. The General Social Secretary, Mr. P. Joerning, arranged all the main and Rugby Section functions, including the Annual Dinner Dance, quietly and efficiently with the added advantage of being an active member of all four sections, which enabled him to work easily with the Section Social Secretaries.

The film shows continued to play a major part in the order of events, taking place at the Club House on the 2nd and 4th Thursdays of each month. Films and equipment provided by Jack Sharp. Also, thanks to Jack, General and Rugby Section Committee Meetings held at Zachary Street were remarkably short of hot air with the knowledge of a film show afterwards. Our thanks also to Niall Armstrong for looking after us when Rugby Section Committee Meetings were held in his office.

A Children's Christmas party was held in the Club House on Saturday, 14th December, 1974.

A number of very successful dinner dances were held throughout the year, the main one being, of course, the Club Annual Dinner Dance held this year at the Malta Sports Club on the 19th April, 1975. After initial enquiries by Peter Joerning appertaining to venue and prices a Sub-Committee was formed to work out details of the Annual Dinner Dance. The Sub-committee consisted of Messrs. W. Dansie (President), T. N. Ricketts (Hon. General Secretary), P. Joerning, and D. Taylor. Peter had written to 14 possible venues before submitting details to the General Committee. The dance was a great success with over 150 members and guests attending. Trev Ricketts carried out the duties of Master of Ceremonies. In accordance with the decision taken by the General Committee, because of the large number of members leaving before the end of the season, the only 'going away' presentation given on this occasion was a tankard to Peter Joerning for his work as Social Secretary of the Club. Overseas General Secretary, Trevor Ricketts, as a member of the Malta Sports Club, took advantage of the M.S.C. Rule which said that the fee of £15 for hire of the hall would be waived if it was booked by a member of that club.

The other Dinner Dances included those organized by the Cricket Section on the 12th October, 1974, and the 24th May, 1975, both held at the Malta Sports Club, and by the Soccer Section at the Crystal Hotel, St. Paul's Bay, Bugibba, on the 9th May, 1975. The General Social Secretary also organized a pleasant Dinner Dance for the General Committee at the Armada Restaurant, Mosta, on the 25th January, 1975.

On Sunday, 2nd March, 1975, Overseas played the touring team, Bedford Athletic R.F.C., and the same evening, acting as hosts, held a Disco-Dance in their honour at Marsa Lodge. The Disco hire was called ASCO. A most practical cold buffet was organized by John Hodgeon and Niall Armstrong.

The final function of the season arranged by the Club was a barbecue held at the Malta Health Farm, Tarxien on the 14th June. This was a final get together for those leaving Malta before the next season. On this occasion those leaving were presented with Club ties. Jack Sharp also provided a film for those leaving.
