The main problems to be overcome will be our own lack of membership and so the ability to put out a XV when required, the lack of opposition, and referees. After March, 1979, Overseas will be the only senior team on the Island, and in 1977/78 the only referees to officiate in senior games at least, have been British Servicemen. At junior level in the Colleges, particularly over two seasons, 1976-78, the interest in rugby has increased, and this must surely be where the answer lies. The Club will also need the blessing of the Minister of Sport in order to continue to use the rugby pitch at the Marsa.

With the knowledge that in July, 1978, almost all members of the General Committee, including the President, would be leaving Malta, the General Committee has decided to call an Extraordinary General Meeting of all interested members on the 8th June, 1978. The purpose of this meeting is to discuss the future composition of the Club and to elect Committee Members to cover the vital interim period prior to the next A.G.M., normally held in September.

Terminal Awards

With so many Members leaving Malta during the present season it was apparent that to present all with the normal 'going away tankard' would be beyond the Club's means. To alleviate the problem the President advised the Sections to bear in mind the criteria, that those members qualifying should have performed good service to the Club, either as players or in the sphere of administration, not just be good players.

CLUB TIES

A further, and perhaps final order, for Club Ties was placed with Alex Brook Ltd., London. The design chosen was a single Club motif, white on navy blue, incorporating 30 years. A tie for members to value and to remind them of a great Club.

Long Serving Members

To try to write about all members leaving in this final year (definitely final for many) is an impossible task. Besides the numbers leaving, there are those who have taken office whilst I have been away from Malta, and I have not had sufficient written material to draw from. In saying this, I am thinking of such members as Charlie Fitches, who to say the least, must have been a very good and competent General Secretary to have taken over from such a person as Trevor Ricketts. However, I would like to mention the following long serving members.

Frank Kitson

On the 4th January, 1978, a dinner was given in Frank's honour shortly before he left Malta after 18 years as a valued member of Overseas. As one of his contemporaries, Jeff Bonner, wrote, certainly a very sad loss to Club, School, and indeed to anyone that knew him. It is easier to list the service Frank gave to the Club, although I have previously mentioned him as an outstanding player in 1962/63.
