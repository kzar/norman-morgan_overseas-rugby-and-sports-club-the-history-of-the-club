CRICKET SECTION 1975

Annual General Meeting, Club House, 21, Flagstone Wharf,
Marsa, Monday, 3rd March.

The following Officials were elected:-
Chairman Mr. A. Latham
Hon. Secretary Mr. M. Newton
Hon. Treasurer Mr. L. Finnis
Social Representative Mr. G. Porter
Team Captain Mr. T.N. Ricketts
Vice-Captain Mr. M. Newton
Selection Committee Messrs T. Ricketts, M. Newton and P. Wright

Overseas were fortunate to retain most players from the
successful season of 1974, and again to be Captained by Trevor
Ricketts. We did not reach the height of the previous seasons
but nevertheless acquitted ourselves well, being runners-up
in both the league and in the 6 a-side competition.

Net practice took place at the Malta Sports Club on
Tuesday evenings at 1600 commencing on the 1st April, and the
season started with the Malta Cricket League 6 a-side competition
on the 26th/27th April. For the 6 a-side Overseas entered
four teams out of a total of sixteen taking part. The eight
first round games were played on Saturday, 26th, and the
second round, semi-finals and final on Sunday, 27th.

The Malta Cricket League started on the 3rd May and ended
on the 10th August, although the last game for Overseas was on
the 19th July, all games taking place at the Marsa. Seven teams
took part, all playing each other twice. During the games each
side was restricted to 36 overs batting and no bowler could bowl
more than eight overs. Perhaps the best game for Overseas was
against the previously unbeaten Puffins on the 15th July. Set
a target of 162 runs Overseas knocked them off for a loss of 4
wickets. The two Overseas batsmen in form were Pete Wright, 87
not out, and Jim Glover, 43.

The Malta Sports Club arranged friendly matches on Sundays
at the Marsa from June onwards.

A wealth of cricket was provided by three good touring sides
from U.K. The Mendip Acorns visited Malta in May and again in
October, the second visit captained by D.J. Shepherd of Glamorgan.
