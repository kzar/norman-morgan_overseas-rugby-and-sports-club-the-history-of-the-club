                                    PLAYING SEASON

1963/64 was a season of mixed fortunes. Overseas
started on a bright note defeating D.E.R.R. Wanderers by
14 points to 3, but this proved to be the only Saturday
win of the season. Starting in January, five Sunday games
were arranged which made it possible to play Overseas players
otherwise committed on a Saturday (Service Teams) with the
result that a more balanced team could be fielded. This
produced a much higher standard of rugby. Three of the Sunday
games ended in wins.

The highlight of the season was without doubt the tour of
the Combined Services side (Army and R.A.F.) from Tripoli,
N. Africa. This tour was arranged by the Combined Services
(Malta) although Overseas was represented on the Working Sub-
Committee, which made arrangements for accommodation, transport
and entertainment for the touring side, by Mr. C.V. Morris (Hon.
Secretary) and Mr. E. Battye (Hon. Social Secretary). Four
games were arranged for the tourists, namely against the R.N.,
the Army, the R.A.F., and the Overseas Club, all played at
the Stadium, Marsa. For the game against the Overseas on the
