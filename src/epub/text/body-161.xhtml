Steering Committee for the Run Down of the Club

With the 7-year Defence Agreement between Malta and Britain due to end in March, 1979, and the consequent complete withdrawal of all British Forces and U.K. Based Civilians from Malta, there exists the very real possibility that on or before that date the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club will be forced to close down. Besides the obvious problem of lack of numbers, there is the question of whether the Maltese, members and non-members, have sufficient interest to keep the non-Maltese sports of rugby and cricket alive in Malta. As for soccer and hockey, especially with soccer being the Maltese national game, the question is whether there would be the continued need for 'Overseas' XI's. All sections of the Club have already felt the strain of decreasing numbers and lack of fixtures as the British Forces have gradually withdrawn, but the testing time will finally arrive when the last of the British Service Schools, Tal Handaq and Luqa, close in July, 1978. The biggest proportion of our present membership comes from the Service schools, particularly S.C.S. Tal Handaq, and to a lesser extent from the Services. With this in mind, in February, 1977, at the suggestion of the General Secretary, Mr C. Fitches, the Overseas General Committee formed a Steering Committee to consider the future activities of the Club, and ultimately to make recommendations with regard to the distribution and eventual disposal of the Club's funds.

The Steering Committee was formed from members of the General Committee and was made up as follows:

Messrs C. Fox (Chairman), J. Sharp, J. Andrews and B. Leonard
