On the 11th January, 1961, the Bar Manager opened a separate Bar account at Barclays. However, at the end of June, 1961, the Bar and General accounts at Barclays were made into one. For the first season the Bar net profits were shared with the Nomads R.F.C. and for this purpose the Bar account was audited up to the 23rd March, 1961. The net profit to each Club was £19.

On the 15th June, 1961, the Overseas Committee decided that in future the Bar profits would be shared between Overseas and the Joint Services Rugby Sports Fund. The period during which the profits would be shared ran from the 1st October to the 31st March, i.e. the playing season. The Overseas Club would make the Club room available during this period for any Social/Playing functions. Capt. J. Day, army representative on the Overseas General Committee, was the prime mover of this decision and so it must have been the wish of the army authorities that profits be shared in this way.

On the 24th September, 1961, Lt. Col. T.G. Chambers, as Chairman Army Rugby (Malta), wrote to Mr. C.V. Morris, Hon. Secretary Overseas, setting out the conditions required for Overseas to continue to use Marsa Lodge. This communication was referred to as Sigs/426. Lt. Col. Chambers asked that the following condition be recorded in the minutes of the next Overseas A.G.M.

"A formal request must be made annually on behalf of the Overseas Rugby Club (Malta) for the continued use of the Marsa Club House by the Overseas Rugby Club. The Chairman Army Rugby (Malta) will in turn approach the General Officer Commanding and will communicate to the Overseas Rugby Club the General Officer Commanding's decision. The request should be made in September of each year and will cover the period 1st October to 30th September following receipt of the request."

"The first request will be due in September, 1962."

Sigs/426 remained in force until the 3rd November, 1967, when the Overseas were allowed to use Marsa Lodge as an 'Encroachment'. More of that later.

In consequence of the different method of sharing profits the representatives of the three Services were asked to sit on the Overseas Bar Management Committee in lieu of the Nomad R.F.C. representatives. These Service representatives were elected at the A.G.M. They were given authority by the Overseas full Committee to open the Club House on occasions when an elected member of the Bar Committee was not present for Service games.

Each year, as soon as possible after the 31st March, an Extra-ordinary General Meeting was held to present the Bar account for the 6 months ending the 31st March. The Bar and General Accounts were audited for the year on the 30th September. On the occasion of the Bar account for the 6 months ending the 31st March being presented, a cheque for the Joint Services Sports Board's share of the net Bar profits was handed to the Secretary Army R.U. (Malta). The Secretary Army R.U. in turn shared this amount between the three Services.
