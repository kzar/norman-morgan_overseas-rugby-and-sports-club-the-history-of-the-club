Tour to Messina

The Tour to Messina was arranged as a result of an article in 'Rugby World' describing the 'Penguins' tour to Malta in May the previous year. On reading this article the Secretary of Messina University Rugby Club, or to be correct, Centro Universitario Sportivo, Messina, Sicily, wrote to Overseas suggesting we visit Messina, and that they pay a return visit at a later date. The Centro Universitario Sportivo, known as CUS, had initially thought we were a Maltese Club. The Tour was agreed to, and on the 7th December, 1967, a Sub-Committee was formed to make arrangements.

The Tour to Messina took place between the 1st and 5th January, 1968. For the crossing from Malta to Syracuse on board the Citta di Alessandria we had booked an 18 berth dormitory, just about the size of an average cabin, plus a 3 or 4 berth cabin. We had a bus waiting on the quayside to transport us the 93 miles to Messina where we were met at the Central Station by the CUS Secretary, Pino di Lorenzo. The hospitality of the Sicilians was tremendous. As guests in Student House, or Casa dello Studente, each member of our party had an individual bed sitter. Being the Christmas holiday period, all the students and staff were away on holiday, and so we were fed, at CUS expense, at a nearby cafe. At most meals we were joined by the CUS Secretary, or the CUS President, Piero Jacai. For one meal we were joined by the whole Sicilian team. On this occasion, helped by a liberal amount of wine, we finished the meal by singing rugby songs, English and Italian. Neither team understood the other team's songs, but if Italian rugby songs are like English rugby songs, and the 'entente cordiale' was such that it seemed certain they were, then I don't know why we were not thrown out of the cafe. However, everyone, including staff and passers-by enjoyed themselves.

In the evening of the first day we were entertained by Members of the University Rugby Club at their Club House, where we were presented with a Sicilian Carroza and plied with Asti Spumante and cake. The following morning we were given a pleasant reception at the main Messina University by the Rector, Il Magnifico Rettore, Professor Salvatore Pugliatti. All Overseas Members were given a commemorative medallion. The 4th January, being a free day, we went by bus with most of our hosts to Taormina.

The CUS rugby pitch, if one can describe it as such, was within the walls of the old 'Cittadella', which is situated near the harbour. The 'pitch' is best described in the Team Captain's words.
