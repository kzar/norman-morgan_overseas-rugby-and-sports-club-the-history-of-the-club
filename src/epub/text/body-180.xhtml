The Island Sevens, sponsored by the R.A.F. were held at Safi on the 19th March. Overseas entered 2 teams and our 'A' team was unfortunate to be beaten in the final of the Cup Competition by the R.A.F. 'A', 10 points to 8. Tal Handaq won the Plate Competition.

The last game of the season for Overseas was on the 14th May, 1978 when we beat H.M.S. Fearless 20-4.

In what is perhaps the last 'full' season of senior rugby in Malta, I would like to comment on the marvellous relationship between Overseas and the Services in organizing senior rugby on the Island, on an individual basis, and through the Joint Services Rugby Committee. Overseas is represented on the J.S.R.C. by the Team Captain, and its credit to this body that such a full fixture list has been kept, especially in the last two years, with so few resident teams. This co-operation has existed since the Club was formed in 1946, and it has given rugby in Malta a sound base to work from. I have said it is perhaps the last full season, because, in spite of the wish to keep Overseas going as a Rugby Club, I have been told by both Navy and R.A.F. organizers that it will be very difficult, if not impossible, for either Service to put out teams next year. If I have not always given due praise to the different services for their part in the Rugby life of Malta, it is only because of the lack of time and space, and I have had to keep to the history of Overseas.

I have already mentioned the hard work done by members of the Committee, but others who also deserve praise are those, such as Charlie and Doris Borda, who are always there when called on. I certainly did not expect to see Charlie play rugby again after a serious operation two years ago. Doris has been looking after the jerseys all season, and is constantly working for the club. I am also thinking of such good members as Margaret Mcpherson, and Carol Crossfield, who along with Doris Borda have done much work preparing food for social functions at the club, and also cleaning and tidying up before and afterwards. I must include also Dorothy Sharp, Maria Schembri, Margaret Newton and Liz Andrews for the constant support they give the Club. Of the Committee, it is hoped that Jack Sharp, in his retirement, will be available at times to give advice. Unfortunately, Ewan Macpherson, Secretary, leaves Malta in October, 1978, although fortunate, with his experience of rugby and the running of the club, he will be there to tide the Club over the next A.G.M., usually held in September. For the future, a great deal will rest on the shoulders of James Andrews who has done so much for the Club over two seasons, even though he has threatened to hang up his boots, the rugby experience and club knowledge of David Schembri, Marcel Cassar, Charlie Borda, and others, and most important, those who are elected at the Extra- ordinary General Meeting to be held in June, 1978 to form an interim Committee, and perhaps form the next Committee at the A.G.M. I wish Overseas well in the future.
