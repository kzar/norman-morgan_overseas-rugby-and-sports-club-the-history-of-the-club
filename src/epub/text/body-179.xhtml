Recently I spoke with Father Ferro of St. Edward's College.
Besides telling me about the 3 rugby teams at the College, i.e.
under 13s, under 16s and 16+, he also told me something about
rugby in Malta in the past. It was most interesting. Father
Ferro told me he was in the R.M.A. for 30 years, and in 1923
he started playing rugby in Malta for a Service team called
the 'Pirates'. He played for the Pirates for 11 years until
forced to stop by ill health.

The highlight of the 1977/78 season in Malta was the visit
of the touring side from U.K., Moseley R.F.C., between the 23rd-
29th March, 1978. Arrangements had been made for them to play
4 games over the Easter period. The touring party of 39 members,
led by Tour Manager, Andy Petrie, and Moseley Club Secretary,
John Butt, included 32 players from their 3rd, 4th and 5th teams,
with a sprinkling of ex 1st team players. They made their own
arrangements for the tour and stopped at St. Paul's Court,
Bugibba, St. Paul's Bay. Moseley had previously toured Malta
in twice, 1975, and on the present tour they certainly found a
difference in the strength of the local teams, all weakened by
the loss of players. The tourists, proving far too good for
the opposition, won all 4 games. They played impressive rugby
and their handling and passing of the ball in the high winds
was excellent, as indeed was their backing up in all phases of
the game and speed to take advantage of opponents mistakes. I
was fortunate to be present at all 4 games and I was most
impressed with the spirit of the Overseas and Malta XVs,
individual talent, and the fact that, at a time when they were
needed most, there were 6 or 7 Maltese players on the field.
The Malta XV was formed from Overseas and the Services. For
Overseas and the Malta XV the players who stood out were the
talented and speedy back, S. Thomas, David Schembri in the
forwards, and great credit to the Captain, James Andrews, who
played at scrum half, a very unfamiliar position for him. For
Moseley, the star was their scrum half, Brian Powell. Overseas
entertained Moseley at the Malta Sports Club immediately after
the game on Saturday, 25th, followed by liquid refreshment in
Overseas Club House. Here David Schembri proved to be an entertainer
after the style of Charlie Rice, and Overseas proved they
could win, even it was only the gents and ladies boat races.
After the two Service games at Safi the tourists were entertained
in the R.A.F. Club at Safi.
