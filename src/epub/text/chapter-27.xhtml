Steering Committee for the Run-Down of the Club

At the A.G.M. on the 22nd September, 1977, the President of the Club, Mr. T.N. Ricketts, asked all members to support the Club and to make this, our possible final year, one to remember - be it in celebrations or high standards on the field. He also asked members to accept the findings of the Steering Committee and to support them in whatever they might suggest.

The Steering Committee made two recommendations to the new Committee at the A.G.M., both recommendations being discussed at the General Committee Meeting held on the 24th October, 1977.

  1. To donate a suitable trophy to be competed for annually by the local schools and the University.
  2. To provide for the renovation of one of the Marsa changing rooms, with a commemorative plaque in the name of the Overseas Rugby & Sports Club.

On the first recommendation, the President asked for a delay in its discussion until the level of schools participation be known - the Rugby Section was asked to activate discussion when this became known. The second was held in abeyance in view of the fact that one of the changing rooms had already been improved.

In the early part of the 1977/78 season the Steering Committee continued its deliberations into the possibility of the Club surviving in some form after the British presence was withdrawn from Malta by the 31st March, 1979. By January, 1978, no concrete recommendations had been made by the Steering Committee as to the future of the Club. This delay was due in part to the fact that the Minister of Sport, Mr. L. Sant, was showing an interest in sports in general in Malta, and as far as Overseas was concerned, in rugby in particular, and in part to the encouraging results from the Club's efforts to interest the playing of rugby by the University and Colleges in Malta. As opposed to this encouraging interest, the Steering Committee had to weigh up the many problems, to try to decide as to whether it was practical to keep the Club alive after the withdrawal, before putting forward any proposal. The Club would be left in the hands of the few members left behind after the Service schools close in July, 1978, and even more so after March, 1979.

In February, 1978, the matter was 'more or less' taken out of the Steering Committee's hands and returned to the General Committee by the fact that a number of Maltese members of the Rugby Section strongly expressed a wish that the Overseas Club and Rugby Section should continue after the 1977/78 season. The General Committee agreed that all encouragement must be given to trying to keep rugby, and therefore the Club, alive, and at the suggestion of Jack Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, two Maltese members, David Schembri and Marcel Cassar, were co-opted onto the General Committee to take part in any discussion on the future of the Club, particularly reference rugby. Both members were already serving on the Rugby Section Committee, David Schembri as liaison between the Club and the Government Schools, and Marcel Cassar as liaison between the Club and Private Schools, U.S. School, and the Royal University of Malta.

Although the Overseas General and Section Committees wished it otherwise, it was generally accepted, that with the withdrawal taking its toll of playing strength, 1977/78 was the last season for the Soccer and Hockey Sections, and the Cricket Section would close in July, 1978. It is interesting to note that all Committee Members of the Soccer and Hockey Sections, and all but one of the Cricket Section, are schoolmasters at the British Service schools in Malta, and they leave en bloc in July, 1978, when the said schools close down for the last time. Here I must stress, that because of the problems of printing and distribution whilst there are sufficient members still in Malta who might be interested in reading it, the writing of this history must be completed by the beginning of May, and final decisions regarding the sections mentioned in this paragraph must, of necessity, be made by the General Committee after this date.

For many years, first as Overseas Rugby Club, then as Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, we have made continuous efforts to create interest in rugby amongst the Maltese people and therefore, the fact that there should be such a strong wish amongst the Maltese members to keep 'Overseas' alive as a Rugby Club, was most pleasing and rewarding. Although problems had been increasing over the years as the Services gradually withdrew as a general policy, and would increase further when the Service schools close down in July, 1978, the testing point, as to whether the Club can survive, even as a Rugby Club, will come when the Services finally and completely withdraw at the end of March, 1979. The main problems to be overcome will be our own lack of membership and so the ability to put out a XV when required, the lack of opposition, and referees. After March, 1979, Overseas will be the only senior team on the Island, and in 1977/78 the only referees to officiate in senior games at least, have been British Servicemen. At junior level in the Colleges, particularly over two seasons, 1976-78, the interest in rugby has increased, and this must surely be where the answer lies. The Club will also need the blessing of the Minister of Sport in order to continue to use the rugby pitch at the Marsa.

With the knowledge that in July, 1978, almost all members of the General Committee, including the President, would be leaving Malta, the General Committee has decided to call an Extraordinary General Meeting of all interested members on the 8th June, 1978. The purpose of this meeting is to discuss the future composition of the Club and to elect Committee Members to cover the vital interim period prior to the next A.G.M., normally held in September.

Terminal Awards

With so many Members leaving Malta during the present season it was apparent that to present all with the normal 'going away tankard' would be beyond the Club's means. To alleviate the problem the President advised the Sections to bear in mind the criteria, that those members qualifying should have performed good service to the Club, either as players or in the sphere of administration, not just be good players.

Club Ties

A further, and perhaps final order, for Club Ties was placed with Alex Brook Ltd., London. The design chosen was a single Club motif, white on navy blue, incorporating 30 years. A tie for members to value and to remind them of a great Club.

The start of the 1977/78 season, a season which was generally believed to be the last, started in a most unusual way. The Minister of Sport, Mr. L. Sant, in order to promote rugby in Malta, invited a team of ex Italian internationals, S.S. Vecchia Roma, to play games in Malta against the Combined Services, on the 23rd September, and a Maltese Select, on the 25th September. Normally, no rugby is played in Malta until the ground is softened by rain, later in the year as that might be, and the pitches at this time were like concrete. However, two weeks of watering made the pitches playable, although still described as hazardous.

The Maltese Select/S.S. Vecchia Roma game was sponsored by the Minister of Sport, and he invited a Spanish Coach, Senor Gonzales, now resident in Italy, to prepare the Maltese Select players for the great day. The Maltese Select XV, formed by the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, consisting of as many Maltese players as possible, lost a hard but very enjoyable game 18 points to 10. Mr. Sant was present during the whole of this game and was presented to both teams before the kick off. After the game we were honoured by Mr. S. Inglott from the Ministry of Sport visiting Overseas Club House. On the 23rd September, the Combined Services beat S.S. Vecchia Roma by 20 points to 19.

In June, 1977, Overseas sent a letter to the Minister of Sport giving him a brief history of rugby in Malta and its future prospects. In acknowledging this letter the Minister suggested that a meeting be arranged. A similar letter was sent by the President to the Chairman of the Joint Services Sports Board, Colonel Seabrook. In his reply, the Colonel said he recognised the valuable work done by the Club in promoting rugby in Malta. The meeting took place in September, when a representative of the Ministry of Sport met representatives from Overseas and other rugby bodies interested in the game in Malta. Those present included Jack Sharp, James Andrews and Ewan Macpherson, of Overseas Club, and Paul Miller, in charge of R.A.F. rugby, also referees (Malta).

Prior to the start of the season, Mr. J. Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, applied for and obtained permission from the Minister of Sport for Overseas to use the rugby pitch at the Marsa on Wednesday, Saturday and Sunday afternoons. Through the good offices of the Naval Sports Officer, Lt. Walker, the R.N. Representative on the Rugby Section Committee, C.P.O. M. Kirk, looked after the marking on the pitch.

The season started in earnest for Overseas on the 15th October with a game against H.M.S. Fearless, and even then the ground was described as being extremely hard. As in the previous season, the only teams to provide regular 1st team opposition were the R.A.F. and 41 Commando (Army), but perhaps the most noticeable difference was the lowering of the playing standards through lack of playing strength. This was particularly so with 41 Commando, no longer the dominant side of old. ‘B’ team opposition came mainly from the R.A.F. ‘B’ side, with single appearances by the Army ‘B’, St. Edward's College, De La Salle College, and a Services ‘B’ side. For Overseas it was not a great season resultwise, again the problem was lack of players who would commit themselves to playing regularly, although on the days when we had our best side out we looked as good as any team on the Island. It proved to be another frustrating season for Overseas as we fast became deplete of players as members left the Island with no replacements filling vacancies.

What I have written in the previous paragraph is consistent with all problems created by the run-down and imminent withdrawal of the British Forces from Malta, in the sphere of rugby during this season. That is one side of the coin. The other speaks of the hard work, and the constant living of rugby, by Jack Sharp (Chairman), James Andrews (Captain) Ewan Macpherson (Secretary and Vice-Captain), Marcel Cassar ('B' Team Captain) and David Schembri, in not only fielding a team throughout the season, but also in building a side for the future. The season started with the possibility that 1977/78 was to be the final rugby season for Overseas, and it ended with a determination, that in spite of the withdrawal, there was a future for Overseas, at least rugbywise, and for continued rugby in Malta. The main reasons for this optimism were (A) At a time when U.K. membership was decreasing there was a welcome increase in the number of Maltese players of reasonable standard in the Overseas teams. (B) The expressed wish of a number of the Maltese members that the Club and the Rugby Section should carry on in 1978/79. (C) The interest the Minister of Sport, Mr. L. Sant, had shown in promoting senior and junior rugby in Malta. I have spoken in this paragraph only of the work done by the Rugby Section Committee members, but all credit is due to our President, and the General Committee, where Section work is discussed, for the work done to keep the Overseas flag flying rugbywise.

In spite of the interest shown by the Minister of Sport, and the repeated offer by Overseas of coaching assistance, the interest shown by the Maltese Colleges during 1977/78 was disappointing, as only St. Edward's College fielded a team on a regular basis. The University had internal problems, although individual players still retained an interest in the game. At St. Edward's College, under the instruction of Father Ferro, the game was spreading, with about 60 boys in different age groups being coached in the game. De La Salle College also fielded a team. At the Rugby Section A.G.M. David Schembri and Marcel Cassar had been elected to sit on the Rugby Section Committee to liaise with the Government and Private Schools respectively, and both did a great deal to further the game in Malta. Jack Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, had also nominated David Schembri and Marcel Cassar to the Minister of Sport as being able to do much to assist him in his efforts to 'establish' the game of rugby in Malta. In October, 1977, in an effort to interest the Government Secondary Schools, David Schembri talked to the Official in Charge of the Secondary Schools Federation who had indicated a willingness to let rugby be tried out providing Overseas produced 6 months coaching programme in advance, and undertook to provide coaching. In turn the Overseas President wrote to the Federation offering coaching assistance. To end the season, the Minister of Sport intends to promote a Youth Tournament for under 16s in May, 1978.

Recently I spoke with Father Ferro of St. Edward's College. Besides telling me about the 3 rugby teams at the College, i.e. under 13s, under 16s and 16+, he also told me something about rugby in Malta in the past. It was most interesting. Father Ferro told me he was in the R.M.A. for 30 years, and in 1923 he started playing rugby in Malta for a Service team called the 'Pirates'. He played for the Pirates for 11 years until forced to stop by ill health.

The highlight of the 1977/78 season in Malta was the visit of the touring side from U.K., Moseley R.F.C., between the 23rd - 29th March, 1978. Arrangements had been made for them to play 4 games over the Easter period. The touring party of 39 members, led by Tour Manager, Andy Petrie, and Moseley Club Secretary, John Butt, included 32 players from their 3rd, 4th and 5th teams, with a sprinkling of ex 1st team players. They made their own arrangements for the tour and stopped at St. Paul's Court, Bugibba, St. Paul's Bay. Moseley had previously toured Malta in March, 1975, and on the present tour they certainly found a difference in the strength of the local teams, all weakened by the loss of players. The tourists, proving far too good for the opposition, won all 4 games. They played impressive rugby and their handling and passing of the ball in the high winds was excellent, as indeed was their backing up in all phases of the game and speed to take advantage of opponents mistakes. I was fortunate to be present at all 4 games and I was most impressed with the spirit of the Overseas and Malta XVs, individual talent, and the fact that, at a time when they were needed most, there were 6 or 7 Maltese players on the field. The Malta XV was formed from Overseas and the Services. For Overseas and the Malta XV the players who stood out were the talented and speedy back, S. Thomas, David Schembri in the forwards, and great credit to the Captain, James Andrews, who played at scrum half, a very unfamiliar position for him. For Moseley, the star was their scrum half, Brian Powell. Overseas entertained Moseley at the Malta Sports Club immediately after the game on Saturday, 25th, followed by liquid refreshment in Overseas Club House. Here David Schembri proved to be an entertainer after the style of Charlie Rice, and Overseas proved they could win, even if it was only the gents and ladies boat races. After the two Service games at Safi the tourists were entertained in the R.A.F. Club at Safi.

The Island Sevens, sponsored by the R.A.F. were held at Safi on the 19th March. Overseas entered 2 teams and our 'A' team was unfortunate to be beaten in the final of the Cup Competition by the R.A.F. 'A', 10 points to 8. Tal Handaq won the Plate Competition.

The last game of the season for Overseas was on the 14th May, 1978 when we beat H.M.S. Fearless 20-4.

In what is perhaps the last 'full' season of senior rugby in Malta, I would like to comment on the marvellous relationship between Overseas and the Services in organizing senior rugby on the Island, on an individual basis, and through the Joint Services Rugby Committee. Overseas is represented on the J.S.R.C. by the Team Captain, and its credit to this body that such a full fixture list has been kept, especially in the last two years, with so few resident teams. This co-operation has existed since the Club was formed in 1946, and it has given rugby in Malta a sound base to work from. I have said it is perhaps the last full season, because, in spite of the wish to keep Overseas going as a Rugby Club, I have been told by both Navy and R.A.F. organizers that it will be very difficult, if not impossible, for either Service to put out teams next year. If I have not always given due praise to the different services for their part in the Rugby life of Malta, it is only because of the lack of time and space, and I have had to keep to the history of Overseas.

I have already mentioned the hard work done by members of the Committee, but others who also deserve praise are those, such as Charlie and Doris Borda, who are always there when called on. I certainly did not expect to see Charlie play rugby again after a serious operation two years ago. Doris has been looking after the jerseys all season, and is constantly working for the club. I am also thinking of such good members as Margaret Macpherson, and Carol Crossfield, who along with Doris Borda have done much work preparing food for social functions at the club, and also cleaning and tidying up before and afterwards. I must include also Dorothy Sharp, Maria Schembri, Margaret Newton and Liz Andrews for the constant support they give the Club. Of the Committee, it is hoped that Jack Sharp, in his retirement, will be available at times to give advice. Unfortunately, Ewan Macpherson, Secretary, leaves Malta in October, 1978, although fortunate, with his experience of rugby and the running of the club, he will be there to tide the Club over the next A.G.M., usually held in September. For the future, a great deal will rest on the shoulders of James Andrews who has done so much for the Club over two seasons, even though he has threatened to hang up his boots, the rugby experience and club knowledge of David Schembri, Marcel Cassar, Charlie Borda, and others, and most important, those who are elected at the Extraordinary General Meeting to be held in June, 1978 to form an interim Committee, and perhaps form the next Committee at the A.G.M. I wish Overseas well in the future.

In what appears to be the final year in which the Soccer Section will function, their fortunes have been very mixed. In the Major League, the Corinthians did exceptionally well, winning 8 out of 12 games played, whilst in the Minor League, the U.K. Civilians have been able to win only 1 out of 13 games played. This has kindly been put down to the fact that there was too much experience and not enough leg power. The Minor League games were played when the younger players were not available during the week. During this season we were very dependent on the R.A.F. at Safi for a pitch.

As with the other sections, the question being asked is whether the Soccer Section will cease to exist at the end of the 1977/78 season. All the Committee and most of the players return to the U.K. in July, 1978. Even if the few remaining players kept the Soccer Section in being, as a Club policy, there is still the fact that the leagues and cups, as organized by the J.S.F.A. (Malta), and in which the Section's teams are entered, will fold up when the Services withdraw in March, 1979.

In a team which has contained so many good and experienced players, and whose spirit has always matched its skill, it is difficult to pick out any individual for performances on the field. For the last 3 years the team has battled against dwindling numbers, added to the fact that the average age has far exceeded most of their opponents and mainly their skill has given them points. As other experienced players have left, the backbone of the team has remained Jeff Bonner, Trevor Ricketts and Dave Smith. This year Trevor celebrates his 10th year as Chairman of the Section. Jeff Bonner and Dave Smith, Captain and Vice-Captain respectively, for 3 years, have also held the following Section Offices. Jeff Bonner, Social Secretary for 2 years, 1973-75, and Hon. by Jeff Bonner

In all probability this will be the last season for the Soccer Section, and before giving detailed report on 1977/78, I would like to comment briefly on its history.

As far as the Soccer Team is concerned it would be most inappropriate to mention only the Overseas for it has only borne that name since the expansion of the Rugby Club in 1973. Prior to that it played under the name of the United Kingdom Based Civilians Football Club (U.K.B.C.), which was founded about 1963. Unfortunately, we know very little of the early days although reliable sources believe that it was a strong and successful team. In the period 1966-70 the team was drawn from various sources. They included Mike Upton, George Hepplestone, Rex Watson, Charlie Rice and Peter Miller from the University of Malta, Phil Dobson from Dingli Wireless Transmitting Station, John Whitehead, a local businessman and Mike Roberts and Trevor Ricketts representing the teachers who have always figured prominently in the team. In 1968 Trevor Ricketts, then playing on the right-wing was elected Chairman and has held the distinction of remaining in that position since that time.

The period 1970-74 was the most successful in the Club's history with victories in the Inter-Services 6 a-side Competition, the Minor League, the Minor League Cup and consistently high placing in the Major League with the newly formed Corinthians Team. The Captains during this run were Mike Upton, Dennis Locke and Jim Glover and several talented and colourful characters had now appeared in the team. In goal we were served very well by Mike Upton, Ralph Daley and Trevor Ricketts respectively. Defenders included the evergreen Phil Dobson, John Williams, Pete Joerning, Bob Tisdale and Mike Novak - the ultimate deterrent, who was guaranteed to stop anything that moved. With such a strong defence behind them, our midfield players were able to use their considerable talents to the full, among them Dennis Locke and Jim Glover, both skilful and excellent Captains, Eddie Pearson and Charlie Rice. Up front John Whitehead and Gerry Porter operated on the wings and the attack was spearheaded by Peter Wright and Jeff Bonner, who between them put the finishing touches to what was a consistently high standard of football from the entire team. In the best season they scored 68 goals each and Peter Wright who was an outstanding striker with the most lethal left foot in the League, scored over 150 goals in his four seasons with the Club.

In 1974 the structure of the team changed yet again with the departure of many fine players. Other players as always came forward to fill their places, the best of these being Dave Smith, but unfortunately the team was unable to match the previous high standard. With the University no longer providing any players and Dave Cooper the sole survivor from Dingli, the number of players available diminished and in recent years the bulk of the Overseas team has come from the teachers. Among them were David Nield, Paul Goss, Tom Jordan, Brian Charnley, Mike Caseley, Jim Slide, and Mike Newton. Four other players who fully deserve a mention were Derek Ward, our two German players Eric Keifer and Dieter Herrud and Andy Holland who as well as being a very solid defender, also provided a venue for several excellent social functions at the Crystal Hotel where his parents Eddie and Margaret Holland, always entertained us in magnificent fashion.

And now in our final season we have tapped yet another source of players, the De La Rue factory in Malta which provided four very talented Geordies, Peter Carnie, Tommy Cummin, Bob Allsopp and Ronnie Feenen. Unfortunately for the Overseas, as games were largely played on Wednesday, working commitments rarely made it possible for us to field a full strength side, and therefore the team's poor showing in the Minor League was not really indicative of its true ability. The Corinthians however, finished second in the Major League, winning eight out of their 12 games and tribute must be paid to the strength and maturity shown by the Tal Handaq boys who played in this team. Among these were John Russell, who started the season as a defender but eventually proved particularly effective as a striker, Andy Basson a brave and consistently reliable goalkeeper, Andy Madge a skilful midfield and forward, Derek Bonnar who was always a welcome addition to the forward line when not required by Floriana and Stuart McKee who although being the youngest in the team, improved with every game and eventually was generally acknowledged to be one of the best sweepers in the League. Other newcomers to the side were Roy Swift, who although having been seen on Malta cricket pitches for several seasons, unfortunately only made known his considerable footballing talent at this very late stage, Rodney Aird-Mash, Tony Rafferty and Dick Owen. Of the established players, Trevor Ricketts and Paul Goss continued to do good work in their unchallenged roles as Overseas goalkeeper and right back respectively, and David Nield had an excellent season being equally at home as sweeper, midfield or on the left wing. Up front Jeff Bonner, the team Captain since 1975, took his tally of goals to over 300 in eight seasons, while the outstanding player in the team was once again Dave Smith who has dominated the midfield for the Overseas in the four seasons he has been with them.

The Overseas Soccer Section has over the years relied very heavily on the more 'mature' and 'experienced' players and many members of the team had thought that he had 'hung up his boots' for good before arriving in Malta and letting it be known that he 'used to' play football. This is further illustrated in our remarkably consistent average which in 1973 was 33 and in 1978 had crept up to 34, unusually high figures for a football team to say the least. Nevertheless, this could well be a factor that has made the soccer team such an enjoyable one to play for, and one which has had considerable success during its 15 years existence. It is particularly pleasant then to see on this last season the Corinthians providing such an excellent farewell performance.

In spite of the loss of a number of players, and a depleted Service League, Overseas continued to field a side. Even so, the gradual withdrawal of the Services from Malta was beginning to bite, and it was difficult to obtain fixtures. The Services League now contained only 4 teams, i.e. Overseas, The Navy (combined with the Commandos), the R.A.F., and the Army. To try to counteract this, the Overseas Hockey Section Secretary, Mr. David Walker, wrote to the Hockey Association Malta in an attempt to enter an Overseas team in the Malta Hockey League, but our application was rejected. The reason given was that the Association could not waive their rule that there could not be more than two non-Maltese players in any team.

As the season started there was continued difficulty in arranging matches in the Services League, also friendlies outside the league. In spite of this, a number of friendlies were played, including games against the Malta club 'Young Stars' and a local club from Qormi. The Young Stars, Malta League Champions in 1976/77, mainly Maltese teachers, defeated Overseas twice. Our relations with the Maltese clubs were very cordial.

Overseas took part in a 6 a-side competition organized by Luxol, a Maltese club which is sponsored by Pepsi-Cola, and succeeded in reaching the quarter final. The Tal Handaq XI won the Plate. During this competition, the game in which Overseas defeated Valliere (this year's Malta League leaders) appeared on Malta T.V. Overseas also took part in a 6 a-side Knockout Competition, organized by the White Hart Hockey Club, on Sunday, 2nd April. This was the first time we had been invited to take part. The competition took place at Corradino Recreational Sports Ground.

The Overseas XI was much changed from the previous seasons, and it was not always possible to field a full strength side. When it was possible, Overseas were a match for any Service side (although results rarely reflected this), and for many Maltese sides too. Even so, without 4 schoolboys from Tal Handaq we would not have been able to field a team, for besides those players who left in July, 1977, Charles Fitches and Gerry Davey were unable to play for medical reasons. We had hoped to persuade some Maltese players to join us, but with our exclusion from the Malta League there was no attraction for them, and so, to the end, we remained a U.K. Based Civilian/Tal Handaq side.

Three Overseas players, Dave Walker, John Whitehead, and Steve Oliver, were selected to play for a Combined XI (Navy, R.A.F. and Overseas) to play against the Malta XI on Sunday, 5th March, 1978. This was no mean achievement for Steve Oliver, who at 17 years of age, had already played for Overseas for 2 years, and had developed into a skilful player, winning the Club award as the 'Outstanding Player of 1977/78'. He was also Captain of the Tal Handaq XI.

In what is the final year of the Hockey Section it is almost impossible to say too much in praise of the small band of men who have formed the Hockey Section Committee, particularly Peter Marchant and Dave Walker, who, since 1975/76 have shouldered most of the offices of the section between them. Also, prior to 1975/76, the same must be said of Dave Taylor and Dave Ditcham. Always fighting against loss of players and paucity of fixtures, and at the same time improving their standard on the field.

In the summer of 1978, all the present Hockey Section Committee, and almost all Section players, will leave Malta. I am informed by Dave Walker, that the last function arranged for the Hockey Section is a Dinner Dance at the Arches, Mellieha, on the 28th April, 1978. This will be the last social occasion and will signal the end of the Overseas Hockey Section.

My Thanks to Dave Walker for details from his Section reports, for vetting and bringing up to date work done, and to Trevor Ricketts and Committee, for access to Committee minutes, in writing the last 3 years of Hockey Section history.

Hon. Social Secretary M. Newton
Team Captain G. Davey

With the 1978 cricketing season for Overseas not yet underway, and with the necessity to finish this history in order to get it to print, I can give no details of the Club’s cricketing activities for this season. This is doubly unfortunate as it is generally believed that the Overseas Cricket Section will close in July, 1978, at the end of the League programme. This is extremely sad as an Overseas Cricket XI has been part of the Malta cricket life since 1965, and has given pleasure to many, players and spectators alike. It only remains for me to wish the Cricket Section a successful and enjoyable year’s cricket.

Extraordinary General Meeting of the Club

Held at 21, Flagstone Wharf, on Thursday, 8th June, 1978

Agenda - Future of the Club

At this meeting, it was agreed by 25 votes to 1 that the Club should continue under the same name and with the same objectives. The meeting recognised that the main emphasis of the Club activities would revolve around the Rugby Section. This was especially so, as the Forces School Teachers would be leaving the Island next month. The Overseas Cricket, Soccer and Hockey Sections were supported almost entirely by these teachers and therefore, had no chance of continuity on the Island.

The meeting re-iterated that the Rugby Section, on the other hand, is well supported by quite a number of Maltese with Messrs D. Schembri, C. Borda, W. Ellul-Bonnici, N. Caruana, S. Mangion, T. Agius-Ferrante, M. Cassar and A. Darmanin forming the nucleus. Also, St. Edward's and De La Salle are progressing rugbywise under the guidance of Father Ferro and Mr. John Taylor and if further encouragement is given, the University and Polytechnic should provide teams. There is, therefore, no reason why rugby should not continue on the Island with the main drive coming from 'Overseas'.

At the E.G.M. an interim Committee for the 1977/78 season had to be elected owing to the departure from Malta of Messrs Ricketts (President); Latham (Membership Secretary); Crossfield (Bar Manager); and Newton (Social Secretary).

It was recognised by the meeting that positions held by Messrs G. Davey, J. Bonner, D. Walker, P. Marchant, T. Jordan, D. Smith, B. Leonard, M. Plummer and M. Caseley would not be refilled.

After a vote of thanks was accorded to the above (and in particular to Trevor Ricketts for his long, invaluable service) for the great work they had done for the Club and the time which they had devoted to the furtherance of their particular sport or sports, the following elections were made:

President C.J.M. Andrews
Social Secretary, Membership Secretary D. Schembri
Bar Manager C. Borda

The positions held by Secretary and Treasurer did not have to be altered as Messrs Macpherson and Andrews were remaining until the end of the season.

Mr. Cyril Fox would continue to provide his wise counsel as Vice-President and Chairman of the Special Steering Committee.

Finally, Mr. Jack Sharp would, the meeting hoped, continue (for as long as he remained in Malta) to be Chairman of the Rugby Section, which after all would be the backbone of the Club.

The meeting closed confident that "A true sporting spirit will continue to be fostered on the Island of Malta by Overseas".
