Evans XV from S. Wales on their third tour. During their short tour, 25th to 31st March, 1970, they played against Overseas and also against the Combined Services. Once again they were accommodated in Lintorn Barracks, by kind permission of the Army (Malta) authorities. There is no record of the names of players and officials included in the party, although one must assume that they were not quite as strong as in their previous two tours as Overseas beat them 18 - 15. The Tour was a great success.

The other touring teams, Army (Cyprus) who remained unbeaten during their short tour, R.A.F. (Cyprus), and the R.A.F. Training College, Cranwell, (Sleaford, Lincs.) were arranged by the Services.

Touring Teams
Army (Cyprus) 21st to 28th November, 1969
R.A.F. Cranwell 29th December 1969, to 2nd January, 1970
R.A.F. (Cyprus) Approximately, 21st February, 1970
John Evans XV 26th to 31st March, 1970

Overseas entered the Inter Service 7's on the 14th March, 1970. No record remains of the results.

Several players who had been Overseas stalwarts for a number of years left Malta at the end of the season. These included John Why; Peter Henshall; Eddie McClure and Adrian Wirth. The Cup for the 'Most Improved Player' of the year was won by Mr. M. Farman.
