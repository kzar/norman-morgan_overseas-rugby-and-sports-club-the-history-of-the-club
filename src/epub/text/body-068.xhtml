However, we will keep to the 1966 A.G.M. for the present.

Vivian Morris, after 6½ years as Hon. Secretary, felt the time had come to hand over to a successor. Vivian joined the Overseas in 1959 and in the following seven years, besides the Office of Hon. Secretary, held the Offices of Hon. Fixture Secretary, 1959/60 and 1965/66; Hon. Team Secretary, 1960-62; Caretaker Bar Manager during the close season 1962; and served on the Club House/Bar Committee, 1959/60; 1961/62 and 1964/65. He rendered the Club invaluable service. Vivian was succeeded by Mr. E.W. Platt, who, unfortunately, was due to leave before the next A.G.M. For this reason an Assistant Hon. Secretary was elected at the A.G.M. 1966.

Ralph McGillivray had been with the Club for 5 years and his record was also quite impressive. Ralph an Overseas player for 3 years became Team Captain for the 1964/62 season. He served on the Social Committee, 1962/63; the Bar Committee, 1963/64; and Bar Manager, 1964-66. Ralph was also a first class referee.

Playing Season

The Team Captain, Brian Jackson, in his end of season report, said that 1966/67 was another successful season - most games which we lost were against combinations. The highlights of the season were the two games against H.M.S. Hermes, the Inter Unit 7's Championship, and the end of season tour by the Penguins. The only players that Brian mentioned were David Agar and Philip Parker for the number of points they scored, and Jack Handford for leading the team when he (Brian) was injured. Mention should also be made of Ted Platt, who, at the age of 43, played in practically every game during the two seasons 1964-66. Ted also Captained the side on the odd occasion. Two pupils of the SCS Tal Handaq, Brian Jackson and Ken Lawrence, are also worthy of mention. Brian played for Overseas for 4 seasons (1963-67) at scrum half, he also Captained the School XV for 2 seasons. Brian is no relation of Brian Jackson, Team Captain. Ken Lawrence played for Overseas for 3 seasons (1964-67) at centre and stand off. Ken was Vice-Captain of the School XV and Student Representative on Overseas General Committee. Both were selected for the full Malta XV for the match against the Penguins. The Cup for the Most Improved Player of the Year was won by John Hitt.

Overseas entered two teams in the Inter Unit Seven-a-side Rugby Championship played at the Marsa on Saturday, 25th February, 1967. Overseas 'A' lost to RAF Hawks 'A' in the final, 14 points to 5.
