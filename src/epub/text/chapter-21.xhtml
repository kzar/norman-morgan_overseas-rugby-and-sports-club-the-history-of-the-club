1972/73

Reformation of Overseas R.U.F.C.

As mentioned previously, a Steering Meeting of members who had been elected at the A.G.M. 1971, was held at the Malta United Film Corporation Offices, Zachary Street, Valletta, on the 21st September, 1972. Present were: Messrs J. Christie; T. Ricketts; J. Sharp; R. Daley; M. Lewis; D. Taylor; F. Kitson and J. Handford. This was the initial meeting to determine the possibility of the rebirth of the Club. Mr. Christie was able to inform members of this Committee that he and Mr. Sharp had visited Dr. B. Camilleri, the Malta Government Minister in Charge of the Marsa Grounds, and permission had been given for Overseas to use a pitch at the Marsa, complete with 2 changing rooms and bar facilities after the game. From this it will be appreciated that the situation as regards Club House, grounds and changing rooms had changed completely. Prior to the withdrawal, the Marsa Sports Grounds area, including Marsa Lodge, came under the jurisdiction of the British Army Authorities. Now, however, the whole area came under direct control of the Malta Sports Board, a department of the Malta Government. At this Steering Committee Meeting it was decided to invite the Service Representatives to a 'discussion' on Rugby on the Island, also to hold an Inaugural Meeting for all ex-members and potential new members.

The informal Meeting of Overseas Committee and Service Representatives took place at the M.U.F.C. offices on the 5th October, 1972. The Inaugural Meeting took place at the Malta Sports Club, previously known as the United Services Sports Club, on the 19th October. At the Inaugural Meeting it was formally decided that the Club should be reformed under the same constitution as that in force prior to the enforced disbandment. It was also decided that all Officials elected at the 1971 A.G.M. should continue in Office until the next A.G.M.

The Rules of the Club, as at the time of the withdrawal, were adopted. Following the adoption of the Rules, Rule 13 was amended to raise the Membership Fees to £M1 for Playing Members and Social Members, and to 50cents for Student Members. Also, Rule 2 was amended to change the word 'Rugger' to 'Rugby'.

It must be pointed out at this stage that as from the 16th May, 1972, the currency in Malta went decimal. Cents and mils replaced the old shillings and pence.

Playing Season

The season started off quietly from the playing point of view, with problems of playing strength and servicing of the pitch. By January we had a good reserve strength and the playing standard had improved. Finally, at the end of the season, the Team Captain, Mr. N. Armstrong, was able to report that we had the best standard on the Island. In his own words, "all in all it had been a tremendous and extremely successful season."

The team strength was built round the Captain, playing at hooker, and Jim Glover, playing at stand-off. Jim Glover, ex Oxford University Team Captain, Cornwall county player and Barbarian, could dictate play and looked as good as his impressive record. Ably assisted by Charlie Rice, Brendan Cradden and Christopher Medlicott, Overseas played an open game.

The problems of servicing the pitch were gradually overcome. Prior to the withdrawal we had relied on the Services to look after the pitch, now we found that the problem was ours. As a Civilian Club, through the efforts of Jack Sharp and Bill Christie, the President of the Malta Sports Board, Dr. B. Camilleri, had given us permission to use the pitches at the Marsa, but we were expected to look after them ourselves. At the beginning of the season, the groundsman employed by the M.S.B., Mr. Salvu Busuttil, had employed outside labour to prepare the pitch. Overseas Committee appointed Mr. F. Kitson to act as liaison between the groundsman and the Club. Through the good offices of Lt. Cdr. J. Harrell and Lt. J. Lunn we were able to use the Navy marker and creosote. Mr. D. Taylor took over the task of looking after the pitch.

The highlight of the season was the defeat of H.M.S. ARK Royal. An Overseas '7' was beaten in the final of the Inter Service Sevens Tournament on the 8th April, 1973.

During the season the old problem was discussed yet again, whether to play our games on Saturdays or Sundays. Whereas Saturday games were better from the spectator point of view, we could field a stronger team on a Sunday. It was decided to accept fixtures whenever available.

The Cup for the 'Most Improved Player of the Year' was awarded to Peter Joerning.

Club House – 21, Flagstone Wharf, Marsa

As has already been mentioned, Marsa Lodge, our old Club House, now came under direct control of the Malta Sports Board. Consequently, the Club rooms and Bar were lost to the Overseas R.U.F.C. Although both the Malta Sports Board and the Malta Sports Club were approached for temporary facilities, an immediate search was made for a permanent Club House.

Thanks to the Team Captain, Mr. N. Armstrong, we were not long without a home. Mr. Armstrong, the General Manager of Andrews Feeds Malta Limited, obtained permission from his Directors for Overseas to use premises belonging to the firm at 21, Flagstone Wharf, Marsa. The premises consisted of 2 adjoining rooms above a warehouse, the main drawback being that it was situated approximately three quarters of a mile from the playing field.

Thanks again to the directors of Andrews Feeds and to Mr. Armstrong, a bar was built in the inner room and a loan was forthcoming to buy a stock of beer. Tables and chairs were obtained from the Services. The grand opening was on Sunday, 17th December, 1972, after the game at the Marsa versus H.M.S. Fife.

Mr. Armstrong, as Bar/Club House Manager, immediately registered the Club with the Malta Police and applied for a licence. This was no easy matter. First, a number of additions to the Club Rules were necessary, all appertaining to Membership. In the hopes of speeding up the issue of a licence, in spite of the fact that the existing Club Rules stated that Rules could only be amended at an A.G.M., the Committee adopted the additional rules on the 8th March, 1973. The additional rules, 16 to 18, were accepted by the Members at the A.G.M., 1973. Also, during the 1972/3 season, the Malta Police checked past and present Membership lists.

At the beginning of the 1973/74 season, members were asked by the Police Authorities to report to the Police Station in their area, and confirm that they were bonafide members of the Overseas R.U.F.C. This proved impracticable. The Police Officials at these Police Stations knew little or nothing about the situation, also the Police had difficulty in contacting members. In February, 1974, members were asked to sign a form declaring that they were fully paid-up members and finally, in April, 1974 the Police issued the Club with a licence.

From the outset, the building and all items within were insured by Andrews Feeds. In April, 1974, an incident in which water was left running within the Club, and which seeped through into the warehouse below, involved insurance claims which Andrews Feeds dealt with. After this incident, Mr. D. Taylor, Overseas Treasurer, inquired into the necessity of Overseas taking out a separate insurance, only to be told that a blanket insurance was held by Andrews Feeds Malta Ltd.

When Overseas R.U.F.C. became Overseas Rugby and Sports Club in September, 1973, Mr. Armstrong believed we should look into the possibility of purchasing our own premises. Because of the political situation the Committee were against purchasing a property, and in November, 1973, Mr. Armstrong was asked to approach his Company with the view to a long-term rental, depending on Police permission. Fortunately, or unfortunately, the Directors of the Company were against this, but, they had no objection to some form of redecoration under Mr. Armstrong's control. However, before this, was carried out another problem arose. Mr. Armstrong reported that there was a possibility of the area in front of the Club House being turned into a dry-dock with consequential difficulties in approaching the Club House entrance. Once again Mr. Sharp came to the rescue. In an interview with Mr. L. Sant, Minister of Public Buildings & Works, Mr. Sharp was informed that the dry-docks were only in the planning stage and that it would be some time before the scheme affected us. With this assurance, the work went ahead. On the 22nd November, 1973, a Sub-Committee was formed, comprising of Mr. C. Fox (Chairman); Mr. Armstrong and Mr. R. Ward, to deal with redecoration. Mr. Ward, Hockey Section Captain, advised on possible designs. On the 22nd February, 1974, with the expert advice of the firm's architect workmen from Andrews Feeds and the M.U.F.C. widened the opening between the two rooms by means of an archway. A number of Club members volunteered their assistance. The alteration and redecoration were completed by the 21st March, 1974.

During December, 1974, the Club House was again re-decorated, this time under a Sub-Committee comprising of Messrs. N. Armstrong, J. Masuwale, M. Crossfield and B. Jones. The General Committee authorized that £50 be spent on soft furnishings and decoration. In January, 1975, curtains, tables and chairs were purchased, and at long last the Club House was complete. Again a number of members volunteered their assistance.

Niall Armstrong left Malta in October, 1975. It is thanks to Niall's unceasing effort that the Club, in the first place, received permission from the Directors of Andrews Feeds to use the premises, and later received permission to alter them structurally. It is also thanks to Niall and Jack Sharp for overcoming the problems of obtaining the Police Licence and carrying out alterations.

Finally, at the beginning of the 1975/76 season, the Bar/ Club House Manager, Mr. M. Crossfield, and the Social Secretary, Mr. J. Hughes with the help of other members, gave the Club House another face lift.

Social Events 1972/73

Except for the Annual Dinner Dance, held at the New Villa Rosa, St. George's Bay, on the 5th May, 1973, the only social events of the season were the film evenings at the Club House.

The film evenings starting on the 8th February proved a success and a good source of revenue at the bar. Once again all the equipment and films were provided by Jack Sharp.

The Annual Dinner Dance, although financially a failure, was an enjoyable event, in pleasant surroundings.

The Club House closed during the summer months.
