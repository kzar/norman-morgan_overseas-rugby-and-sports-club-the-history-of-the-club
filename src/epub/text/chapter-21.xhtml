Barrie Jones

Just prior to Christmas, 1975, a tragic motor accident resulted in the death of Mr. Barrie Jones, a very popular Social Member. This was felt deeply by the members closely associated with Barrie and in response to the Club’s appeal to the members, also with the assistance of the three Services Junior Schools, the net sum of £100 Sterling was sent to Mrs. Jones, then in U.K. A Sub-Committee, under the Hon. General Secretary, was formed to deal with the collection.

Overseas Club Plaque

In March, 1974, Mr. M. Van der Eijk, a rugby playing member of the Club, made 27 plaques of solid plaster, a number of which were backed with wood. The design consisted of a white Maltese Cross on a blue background. Above the cross were the letters O.S.R.F.C. At the end of the 1973/74 playing season a number of leaving members were presented with one of these plaques.

Whilst researching for this history, I was informed by both Mr. Ian Chapman (Hon. Secretary 1954⁠–⁠56) and Mr. Mike Phillips (Hon. Treasurer 1953⁠–⁠56) of the blazer badge which was produced for Overseas R.U.F.C. in 1954/55. Mr. Chapman forwarded his badge to me and in October, 1975, it was placed before the General Committee to discuss whether the basic design could be used for a Club badge or plaque. It was felt that a badge would be unmarketable, but a beautiful plaque, a replica of the original design, was made by a contact of Mr. P. Tyler. Two plaques were made for the Club at a price of £M4 each, the first one being paid for by Mr. Tyler himself and presented to the Club. The plaque itself was made of wood and the inserts of the “design” and “Overseas R.S.C.” were made of plaster. The intention was to present a plaque when it was deemed necessary to a visiting team and such a presentation was made to the Yugoslav touring team “Energoinvest” on the 21st March, 1976. Also, in March, 1976, Mr. J. Sharp and Mr. J. Hughes produced sample pennants, the latter from 6 girls at S.C.S. Tal Handaq. Authority was given by the General Committee for Section representatives to ask Mr. Hughes to commission pennants as need arose. In actual fact, with a replica of the sample pennant as produced by the schoolgirls being unavailable, a pennant was purchased from Sports Dealer, Emmanuel Said, Britannia Street, Valletta, embossed with the Club Badge as on the Car Sticker, and presented to Energoinvest, together with the aforementioned plaque, and a number of Club ties (3 were mentioned in minutes prior to the presentation, although I believe 6 were given).

Officials Leaving Malta - 1975/76

Officials leaving Malta during this season included our President, Mr. D. J. Taylor. Dave left Malta in July, 1976, after serving the Club for 6 years in many capacities. His departure was a big loss to the Club and appreciation of this fact was shown by his being elected an Hon. Life Member, details of which are given on a following page. Also included amongst those leaving, with full details under separate headings, were Mr. N. Armstrong and Mr. J. Handford.

Another hard worker for Overseas, and therefore whose departure in April, 1976, was a loss to the Club, was Mr. G. Porter. Gerry took an active part in the Soccer Section (Hon. Treasurer 1974- 76, Hon. Social Secretary 1975/76), the Cricket Section (Hon. Social Secretary 1974/75), and also the Hockey Section.

Finally amongst the leavers I must include myself. I left Malta in July, 1976. On previous occasions when I left Malta I knew I would be returning, but this time it was almost certainly final. With Overseas Club being so much part of my life since 1948, it was a sad occasion. My greatest hope was that Overseas would continue to exist as a Club, in one form or another, after the British presence was finally withdrawn in March, 1979.

Niall Armstrong

Niall left Malta in October, 1975, and his departure was a loss to rugby in general in Malta and to Overseas in particular. For 3 years he had given a great deal of his time and energy to rugby in Malta, both as a player and as an organizer. As Overseas XV Team Captain he represented Overseas on the Joint Services Rugby Committee and was therefore also our Fixture Secretary. He was an automatic choice as hooker, latterly as Team Captain, of the Malta XV. He was a great Team Captain and Club man, and very popular.

Overseas will always be grateful to Niall for coming to the rescue when we were without a Club House in 1972. As General Manager of Andrews Feeds Limited, Malta, he obtained permission from his Directors for Overseas to use their premises at 21, Flagstone Wharf, Marsa, and to fit out same as a Club House.

Jack Handford

In November, 1975, Mr. Jack Handford, a Vice-President and one of the Club’s longest serving Members, left Malta. Jack left quietly and no official farewell was given. When the General Committee was informed Jack was already away. The omission was partly remedied by the President, Mr. D. J. Taylor, sending a letter from the Committee and a gift from the Club of an engraved paper knife. Jack’s club record is quite impressive. He joined the Club in 1962 and when he left in 1975 he could claim the club record of being the longest continuously serving Committeeman, 1963 to 1975. He was a playing member of the Rugby Section for 10 years, from 1962 to 1972, during which time he played 185 games for the Club out of a possible total of 225. This is allowing for the fact that he broke his leg in December, 1965, and could play only 8 games out of 21 in that year, also for the fact that I cannot confirm the number of games he played in 1966/67 and 1971/72. List of games played is produced below. Jack was a hard uncompromising player. He was Team Captain for 3 years, 1963⁠–⁠1966, Vice-Captain for 3 years, 1966⁠–⁠1969, and finally taking over as Vice-Captain for a 4th year when Eddy McClure was injured in November/December of the 1969/70 season. He was a Vice-President for 7 years, 1969⁠–⁠1976. Jack took part as a player (vice-Captain) in the Rugby tour to Messina in January, 1968, and was in Committee during the temporary disbandment and reformation of the Club in 1972.

Jack also played for the Cricket Section from its inception in 1965 to approximately 1972.

From the outset John Hodgeon said he would be a non-playing Captain and this left the problem of Captaincy on the field, and also the vacant position of Vice-Captain, to be settled. Niall Armstrong willingly captained the team on the field until he left Malta in October. At this point Brian Charnley tentatively agreed to take over from Niall, and James Andrews agreed to be Vice-Captain. The position was further complicated when John Hodgeon left Malta on the 21st January, 1976, but then Brian Charnley agreed to take over the Captaincy on and off the field. Mr. J. Sharp was elected as the 3rd Member of the Selection Committee, with the other two Members being Mr. J. Hodgeon, later Mr. B. Charnley (Captain), and Mr. J. Andrews (Vice-Captain). It was decided that as the number of Student Members had fallen in the previous season there was no requirement for Student Member of Junior Member Representatives. In fact no Student Members joined the Club in 1975/76. Mr. Hodgeon, later Mr. Charnley, represented Overseas on the Joint Services Rugby Committee.

Through the good offices of Mr. Jack Sharp, Overseas was again given permission by the President of the National Sports Board, Dr. Benny Camilleri, to use the pitch at the Marsa. With the pitch having been watered throughout the summer by the National Sports Board it was in good condition at the start of the 1975/76 season, and no seeding had been required. Throughout the season the pitch was looked after by the Naval Sports Officer at St. Angelo, Lt. B. Traynor, also P.T.I. D. Cousins, with Mr. J. Andrews acting as link between the Sports Officer and the Rugby Section Committee. In January, 1976, following the departure of John Hodgeon, Lt. Traynor was asked to sit on the Rugby Section Committee as R.N. Representative.

The season started with 3 practice games. These were against H.M.S. Intrepid on the 13th September, and against “G” Coy. 41 Cdo. on the 28th September and the 5th October. The Team on the Island throughout 1975/76 was undoubtedly 41 Commando, they proved unbeatable. This made the standard of all other teams, including Overseas, poor in comparison, and made for some heavy defeats, especially at “B” team level. At 1st team level there were only 4 regular local teams, namely, Overseas, 41 Cdo., R.A.F. Luqa and Alma Battery, and at “B” team level the only regular local teams were Overseas, 41 Cdo. and R.A.F. Luqa. Other teams who managed to put out a team on the odd occasion at “B” team level were 203 Sqdn. (R.A.F.), Mtarfa, St. Edwards College and S.C.S. Tal Handaq. This meant that players, particularly in our “B” team, who met 41 Cdo. six times during the season, began to think, “Oh, not again!”. However, notwithstanding this situation, the season was exceedingly enjoyable rugbywise. The first four games of the season for Overseas were against visiting naval ships, all wins, and the final game of the season for Overseas was against the touring team Energoinvest R.C. from Makarska, Yugoslavia. Good rugby was also provided by two touring sides from S. Wales Deri R.F.C. and Abercarn R.F.C., although Overseas did not have a fixture against them. The tours by the two S. Wales sides were organized by the J.S.R.C. Overseas was represented in the Malta XV against Deri by B. Charnley, D. Schembri, and W. Wood, and in the Malta XV against Abercarn by B. Charnley. Overseas entered two teams in the Inter Service 7’s on the 28th March, 1976, with Overseas “A” being beaten in the semi final by 41 Cdo., the eventual winners of the competition. At the outset of the season Mr. J. Hodgeon, Tours Secretary, attempted to arrange a tour to Messina to play Messina Rugby Clan. This failed through lack of support.

The highlight of the 1975/76 season was the visit to Malta by Energoinvest Rugby Club from Makarska, near Split, Yugoslavia, between the 19th and 26th March, 1976. The initial approach to our Club for this tour, by Mr. Marko Protega, General Secretary of the Yugoslav Rugby Federation (Ragbi Savez Yugoslavije), was made at Federation level, after finding our Club address in the R.F.U. Twickenham handbook. From this point, all arrangements for the tour were made between Overseas General Secretary, Mr. T. N. Ricketts, and Mr. Marko Protega, acting as Tour Manager for Energoinvest R.C. The touring party of 25 travelled by Yugoslav Airlines (JAT) and stopped, at their own expense, at the Villa Rosa, St. George’s Bay. With kind permission of Capt. L. Pace, B.E.A. Manager at Sliema, they were met with due ceremony on the airport tarmac by our President, Mr. D. J. Taylor, accompanied by Mr. Ricketts and Mr. J. Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman. They were an extremely talented and sporting side and this resulted in two very good games, the first on Sunday, 21st March, versus Overseas, and the second on Wednesday, 24th March, versus a Malta Select XV, both on our regular pitch at the Marsa. We had hoped to play the two games on the Marsa Stadium pitch which at this time was mainly a soccer pitch. Permission was received from the National Sports Board, and by courtesy of the R.A.F., the Naval Sports Officer, Lt. B. Traynor, had actually had the rugby posts transported from Hal Far, but due to last minute disagreement by the Maltese soccer fraternity, we were not able to install them. Our own posts had proved too rotten to move.

The game against Overseas was very exciting and looked upon by Energoinvest as a “Friendly International” (Prijateljska Medunarodna). Overseas, well led by their Captain, Brian Charnley, played well and fully contributed to a fast and open game. Overseas were beaten by a fitter side and superior threequarter play.

At the beginning of the season Overseas yet again attempted to interest the Maltese Colleges in rugby. Mr. J. Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, first approached the National Sports Board and then wrote direct to the Rectors of St. Edward’s, De La Salle and St. Aloysius College. Mr. Sharp pointed out that we would like to see the game of rugby established in Malta before the British presence was withdrawn in 1979. This produced immediate response from St. Edward’s College and by agreement between the College, the N.S.B., and S.C.S. Tal Handaq, pupils from the College trained with pupils from Tal Handaq during the latters games periods on Friday afternoons at the Marsa. The pupils were instructed by Trevor Ricketts and Dave Taylor, sports masters at Tal Handaq, with Dave being in charge of coaching the boys from the College. By the end of January some of the pupils from St. Edward’s had improved sufficiently to play for Overseas “B” team and indeed on the 14th February, the College fielded a team to play Tal Handaq. Also in February, St. Edward’s put out a side against “G” Company (41 Cdo.) and, although losing 24 - 0, had played well. On the 14th March three pupils from St. Edward’s played for Overseas “B”. In February, De La Salle College also started training, too late to take part in games, but sufficiently improved to give hope that they could field a XV the following season. No interest was shown by St. Aloysius College.

In November, Brother Hilary was approached to find out if the Maltese viewers would be interested in rugby on his sports programme on Malta T.V.. In reply Brother Hilary offered to film a game for T.V.. The match chosen “by Overseas, in conjunction with the J.S.R.C., was the Malta Select practice game on the 14th February, Probables versus Possibles. The film was shown on Malta T.V. on Friday, 15th March, 1976, with Marcel Cassar, ‘B’ Team Captain, commenting on the game in Maltese. Marcel also gave the viewers a brief history of Overseas Rugby Club. Whilst making the film care was taken to shoot one or two carefully planned highlights involving David (Schembri) and Marcel. With David and Marcel being local boys this involved the Maltese with Overseas and the game more and also gave a better impression of what rugby is all about.

In addition to the direct approaches made by Overseas, David Schembri and another local sportsman cum reporter, writing under the Nom de Plume “Atticus”, attempted to reach the Maltese public by writing separate articles in the Times of Malta. David, a lieutenant in the Malta Armed Forces, winner of the Overseas Club’s “Most Improved Player of the Year” Cup in 1974/75, wrote an article (16th October,1975) under the heading “Rugby is a Fascinating Game”, “Atticus”, himself an all-round sportsman, in his weekly article (sportspage) headed “By the Way - Sport” wrote twice (February 4th and 11th) on rugby in general in Malta, going back over the years, and on Overseas Rugby Club in particular. Both writers informed the public that Overseas would be pleased to teach rugby to anyone interested.

In November, once again, members experienced difficulty in entering the Marsa whilst horse racing was in progress on a Sunday afternoon - police were stopping cars at the main gate. It was decided to have cards reprinted and issued to members, similar to those issued at the beginning of the 1974/75 season. This did not suffice, and those Overseas members who were not also members of the Malta Sports Club, i.e., unable to show a M.S.C. car sticker, had to obtain a N.S.B. car sticker. Jack Sharp was able to get a limited amount of N.S.B. stickers from the N.S.B. Secretary.

During the season a problem arose over the makeup of Overseas 1st team, and which, because of the small number of teams on the Island, affected rugby in Malta in general. A special meeting of all rugby players was called on the 29th January, 1976. presided over by Mr. J. Sharp, Overseas Rugby Section Chairman. The crux of the matter was that Overseas, as a Civilian Club, was fortunate to be able to accept as members, and so select its teams from, all interested players, Civilian and from Service Units. This included 41 Cdo. personnel, when they were not committed to their own unit team, most of whom were good enough to walk into any team. Many 41 Cdo. chose to play for Overseas when possible, often to the detriment of less able players who had no other team, and so at times Overseas looked very much like 41 Cdo. itself. Overseas players, that is, players who owed allegiance only to Overseas, were adversely affected, also the opposition started remarking on similarity of teams. The meeting was handled with great tact and good sense by Jack Sharp and Overseas Team Captain, Brian Charnley, to the general satisfaction of all concerned. 41 Cdo. were friends of all gathered and no one wished to offend. The rugby community in Malta is of necessity a very close unit, and as Brian Charnley said on addressing the meeting “Overseas” was unique, it was more than a Club, the team was made up of Civilians, 41 Cdo., Battery, Navy and R.A.F. From this point Overseas recreated its own identity and rugby was more enjoyable for all concerned. This was a chance for Brian, also James Andrews, Team Vice-Captain, to pep up Overseas players on lack of fitness, asking them to let the Captain know they wished for a game, promptness on the field etc. - The meeting was a good airing of views and a success. At the end of the season Jack Sharp stressed that in view of the difference of opinion encountered this season, we should have Service Representatives on the Rugby Section Committee next year. The same practice was adopted when we had our Club House at Masra Lodge.

Consequent to the above, the second half of the season was more enjoyable and according to the Captain, our standard of play generally improved. He described the season as reasonably successful after a disappointing start. Brian did not comment on the play of any individual, but there is no doubt that Brian himself earned his place playing at scrum half for the Malta XV. Dick McArdle and Brian were our most consistent players. Credit is due to Marcel Cassar who captained the “B” Team under great odds, always short of players. He gave a great deal of his time and energy looking for players during the week, and he was a tower of strength on the field. No player was considered to have earned the “Most Improved Player of the Year” Cup. The “Young Player of the Year” Cup was presented to Peter Traynor.

Throughout the 1975/76 season the Soccer Section had difficulty in raising a team of comparable standard with previous seasons. This was particularly so in the Minor League and there were fears that we might be relegated to Division II at the end of this season. Fortunately, these fears were not realised.

The main problem was trying to replace the experienced players who left Malta during the summer months and this proved almost impossible. This was aggravated by the occasional clash of Soccer Section and Rugby Section fixtures on Saturdays. The fact that one or two members who played both codes might choose to play rugby was a serious matter when the Soccer Section had so few players to choose from. This led to the Section Secretary, Mr. J. Bonner, approaching the General Committee for priority to be given to Soccer on Saturdays. After the start of the season, Jeff Bonner reported using schoolboys in the Major League, although the Minor League XI was made up of all Overseas players. In January, 1976, the League Management Committee queried the constitution of our membership with reference to one of our players, although this was cleared up to our satisfaction at a League Management Committee Meeting in the February. Our point that our constitution allowed a certain amount of freedom of choice of players was agreed to, provided we informed the Management Committee of any players we used.

In April, 1976, the Soccer Section lost another good player and hard working Committee man in Gerry Porter. Gerry had been Treasurer for 2 years, 1974⁠–⁠76, and in his final year also took on the task of Social Secretary.

The season opened on the 24th April with the 6 a-side Competition. Overseas entered 4 teams including a team of boys from Tal Handaq as Overseas “C”. Our strongest team, Overseas “O”, succeeded in reaching the semi final when we were beaten by 41 Cdo., the eventual winners.

The final of the Government Tourist Board Trophy for 1975, between the Malta Sports Club and 41 Commando, which had, through force of circumstances, to be left over until May, 1976, was won by the Malta Sports Club.

The Single Wicket and the Double Wicket Competitions were held on the 3/4 July and the 14/15 July respectively. The Double Wicket was won by Ray Berrington and his son. The B.F.B.S. Trophy was held on the 27th June.

Overseas 2nd XI, under the Captaincy of Jim Robinson, played 3 matches, winning against the Malta Sports Club and Tal Handaq, and losing to the Navy.

Throughout the summer Malta enjoyed good and exciting cricket from touring sides from U.K. In April we had the Speedbirds, a cricket organization of British Airways, and at the end of May we had a team from Panteg, Glamorganshire, who chose Malta for their centenary celebrations. Then, at the end of August, the Old Boys of the Royal Grammar School, Worcester, under the name of the Old Vigornians, visited us. The end of the season was more like a cricket festival. Starting late September we had the Blighters from Cheshire and Lancashire, followed in quick succession by the Mendip Acorns from the West Country, 2 - 10 October, Bill Frindall’s XI, 10 - 17 October, and finally the Forty Club, 18 October. The touring sides played against individual Civilian and Services sides, Combined Civilian XIs, Combined Services XIs, and also against the full Malta XIs. Overseas played the Old Vigornians on September 2nd and Mendip Acorns on October 2nd. The Forty Club included Fred Trueman, and the Mendip Acorns included Don Shepherd, amongst their players.

The season produced a veritable feast of cricket at the Marsa from April to October in which Overseas members played a full part. For Overseas, in spite of some good wins in the league, our success was limited until late in the season when we reached the final of the 1976 Government Tourist Board Trophy for the first time. The final was held on the 23rd October and here we came off second best to the M.S.C. Overseas Team Captain, Mike Newton, in describing our improvement in play, said Overseas had found a real gem in Ken Dobell, his last few knocks being 117, 33, 73 not out, 65 not out, and 35 not out, plus Trevor batting like a man inspired and the steady batting of Gerry Davey, so our fortune with the bat had taken a turn very much for the better. We could look forward to next season with very much more confidence.

Social Functions

Throughout the 1975/76 season we had a very full calendar of events. It was a very successful year. All Club and Rugby Section Social Functions were excellently organized by John Hughes, General and Rugby Section Social Secretary, backed by the hard work of Martin Crossfield, Bar Manager, and James Andrews, General Treasurer, who kept the bar stocked. James, successor to Niall Armstrong as General Manager of Andrews Feeds, was responsible to his firm for the continued use of their premises at Flagstone Wharf by the Club.

Again we must thank Jack Sharp for the film shows throughout the year, whether at the fortnightly club event on the first and third Thursday of each month, or on the many other occasions, including Committee evenings. They were very much appreciated by all who enjoyed Jack’s hospitality.

One never likes to see members leaving Malta, but the official functions given to Club Officials prior to their departure were a great excuse for all members to get together. This season they were excellently organized by John Hughes and Trevor Ricketts, General Secretary, and were approached with a great sense of humour. The events were organized under the guise of a “quiet drink” and the recipients were greatly surprised to be led into a “noisy” social function arranged for their benefit. They were a success socially and enjoyed by members, especially by the person so honoured. The first occasion was on the 4th October, when a Farewell Cold Buffet and Dance was organized for Niall and Carol Armstrong at Tigne Beach Club, Tigne. At the end of the season, on the 3rd July, the President, Dave Taylor, and his wife Marjorie, were also given a Farewell Cold Buffet Dance at Tigne Beach Club, and finally, on the 8th July Amy and I were honoured by a Farewell Dinner at the Crystal Hotel, Bugibba. Other enjoyable and successful Farewell Parties were arranged privately, which all added to the social scene and camaraderie.

Great effort and no little expense was put into entertaining Energoinvest, the visiting team from Yugoslavia. The Disco & Buffet organized for them at Marsa Lodge on the 24th March did not come up to expectations, mainly through lack of support, although the expense was considered acceptable in terms of the goodwill created between Overseas and Energoinvest. The final cost to the Club of hosting the visitors was £M65.81. There was a definite language barrier as only their Tour Manager, Marko Protega, spoke fairly good English, but they enjoyed themselves at all functions arranged for them. They were particularly interested in a rugby film showing games played by the British Lions on one of their tours. They took their rugby very seriously indeed and drank very little intoxicating liquor on this tour. They were also entertained by Jack Sharp at the M.U.F.C. Valletta. Whilst still on the subject of the tourists, I must add to what I have already written about the language difficulty. Whilst Marko was the only one who could converse fully in English, one or two of the others spoke some English, whilst none of the hosts could speak any of the three major languages used in Yugoslavia.

The Annual Dinner Dance, successfully organized by John Hughes was held at the Old Villa Rosa, St. George’s Bay, St. Julians, on the 3rd April. We were a little cramped for space and numbers had to be kept down to 65 doubles. Actually, approximately 48 couples attended. The only official guest invited, although unable to attend, was the President of the National Sports Board, Dr. Benny Camilleri. Each Section of the Club also organized their own Section Dinner to which all members were welcome. Other functions held included a Stagnight (9th October), a Cheese & Wine Party (13th November), a Games Evening (27th November) and a Children’s Christmas Party (18th December).
