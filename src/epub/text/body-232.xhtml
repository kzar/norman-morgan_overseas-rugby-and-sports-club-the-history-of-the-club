Long Serving Members

Cyril Fox

Cyril Fox joined Overseas in 1972. In his first year as a member, and in all years between 1974 and 1982, Cyril served the Club in the capacity of Vice-President. In 1973/74, the year Overseas became a Rugby and Sports Club, he was elected President. Although intensely interested in both rugby and cricket, watching from the touchline or boundary on a Saturday and/or Sunday afternoon, his forte was to work for the Club as a whole, and not trying to pick the winning team on a Selection Committee. This was particularly so over the last 4 years of the Club's existence, when, as one of a Committee, he first fought to save the Club from closure, and, when this was a fait accompli, he helped to settle the Club's affairs with equal dedication. Always willing to take on extra Committee work, usually through difficult times, all members came to rely on his sound judgement. I personally have never seen Cyril ruffled or lose his calm and always he was interested in people, and had the time and courtesy to speak to everyone. Twice during the last 3 years he stepped into the breach to act as President/Chairman, and his last Office was to Chair the final Committee Meeting of the Club.

Jack Sharp (Hon. Life Member)

In the History of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club (p. 152) I gave a full account of the many ways in which Jack served the Club up to May, 1978. At that time Jack was due to retire and his future movements unknown, at least to the writer. Fortunately for Overseas, he remained with M.U.o.F.C., later called M.K.R.S., after May, 1978, and was deeply involved in the Club right up to its closure.

I repeat what I wrote in the History. Although I have listed below many of the Offices undertaken by Jack, it is impossible to name the many diverse ways he has helped the Club. It is impossible to overestimate his contact with the Maltese Ministry, particularly the Minister of Sport, on the Club's behalf; the many times he has acted as host to Overseas Committees and Members; his direct contact with the Colleges appertaining to rugby participation; and not least for providing all the necessities for Film Evenings at the Club House. This last mentioned service did much to boost the morale of members towards the end when times were difficult, and a Club House was of vital necessity for keeping members together.
