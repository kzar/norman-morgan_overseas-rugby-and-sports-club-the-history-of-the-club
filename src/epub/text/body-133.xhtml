One of the initial problems facing the Club, in giving notice of the first Annual General Meetings of the General Committee and of the different sections, was that the majority of Members were interested in the General Committee A.G.M. and also one or more section A.G.M.s. This particularly concerned Members of the Rugby Section, the largest faction of the Club. The different notices would be addressed in many cases to the same individual Members. This could mean as many as four notices going out, and also there was the difficulty in trying to fit the meetings of the Rugby, Soccer and Hockey Sections in before the main A.G.M. This is accentuated by the fact that a big percentage of Members, including Committee Members, are Schoolmasters and are away from the Island during the summer months, returning early September.

The Club rules as formulated at the 1973 A.G.M. did not specifically state that each Section must hold its own A.G.M., hence the already mentioned change to Rule 6. No separate notice for the Rugby Section A.G.M. of 1974 was sent out, but advantage was taken of the word 'Rugby' in the name of the Club, i.e. anyone interested in Rugby would attend the main Club A.G.M., after having been informed of such. In fact, all Members attending the main A.G.M. in 1974, and who were interested in Rugby, were asked to attend the Rugby Section A.G.M. immediately following. For the following year (1975) this was resolved by the General Secretary sending out one notice to all Members giving dates of the different Annual General Meetings. The same information was broadcast over the B.F.B.S. and the local radio, also inserted in the local press and the Dolphin & Eagle.

'OVERSEAS' FUNDS, UNITED KINGDOM AND MALTA

During this season it was increasingly felt by Overseas Members, in view of the belief that Overseas might cease to exist once the British presence in Malta ended in March, 1979, that the Club funds, now in the U.K. in Cdr. M. Pearey's name, should be utilised for the benefit of the Club at the present time. Consequently, following decisions made in Committee, the Hon. General Treasurer Mr. D. Taylor, wrote to Cdr. Pearey asking him to make arrangements to transfer the Club monies, then in his name, to a bank assessible to and under control of the Hon. General Treasurer. Accordingly, Cdr. Pearey made arrangements and following resolutions passed in the General Committee on the 24th March, 1975, an account was opened with Lloyds Bank, Cox's and King's Branch, Pall Mall, London, in the name of Overseas Rugby and Sports Club (Malta) with a starting balance of £231.37. The account was immediately made use of to purchase training films from the R.F.U. Twickenham (£36.30) and for Cricket balls (£34.20).

As previously stated in this history under year 1959, Overseas first opened an account in Malta with Barclays Bank on the 23rd March, 1959. This account was closed and transferred to the National Bank of Malta in approximately 1964 until it was transferred back to Barclays Bank International in December, 1972. The account was held with Barclays Bank International, Sliema, until this bank ceased to operate as such in Malta on the 30th September, 1975. On the 1st October, 1975, the banking business of Barclays Bank International in Malta and Gozo, and the account of Overseas, was transferred to Mid-Med Bank Limited.
