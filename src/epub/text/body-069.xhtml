The Penguins, as guests of the Overseas Club, toured Malta from the 5th to the 15th May, 1967, and proved to be very popular socially besides producing excellent rugby. I think that even Ben Bush would have learned some new songs. The Penguins played five matches in eleven days, won four, lost one and scored 119 points to 31. The R.A.F. were the only side to beat the Penguins. The last game, against a Malta XV produced the best rugby of the tour, with speedy passing and some excellent backing-up.

The main burden of arranging the Penguin's tour was borne by Ted Platt, Overseas Hon. Secretary, Ted in turn stressed that without the considerable help of the Army, in the person of Major J. Bird, and the Air Force, represented by Fl. Lt. C. O'Regan, the visit would not have been the success it was.

Penguin players come from a fairly wide area of the southern half of England. On the occasion of their trip to Malta the party consisted of 17 players and officials led by their Fixture and Team Secretary, Tony Mason.
