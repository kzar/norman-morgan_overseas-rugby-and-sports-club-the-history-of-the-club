1973/74

'Overseas' Becomes a Rugby and Sports Club

Annual General Meeting, 21, Flagstone Wharf, Marsa, 20th September, 1973.

At this meeting members were asked to vote on perhaps the most controversial issue in the history of the Club. A motion was put forward to change the rules of the Club, a motion which was to change the structure of the Club completely. It was, in effect, to change the name of the Club from Overseas Rugby Union Football Club to Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, for the affairs of the Club to be run by a General Committee, and for the Club to embrace not only rugby and cricket as sections of the Club, but any other sporting factions who applied to become part of the Club and could satisfy the General Committee as to their viability.

The resolution was put forward by Mr. T. N. Ricketts and seconded by Mr. C. Rice. In Mr. Ricketts' own words, "The amalgamation of the other sports clubs with the Club would not take away the name of Overseas R.U.F.C., but would try to bring under one roof all finances for the betterment of sport in Malta in general. Many people were paying out two or three lots of fees in order to belong to these clubs. The financial position of the Club would be improved. The bar would be open more therefore increasing the bar profits. Social functions would improve and the name of Overseas would be perpetuated by many Clubs. The identity of the Rugby section would not be lost as it would bring all sportsmen together."

The basic idea behind the proposal was good. On an Island as small as Malta, a number of men were playing 2 or 3 different sports over each weekend, particularly during the winter season, and also, as has been stated, paying subscriptions to each sport. The same men were mostly from the comparatively small band of U.K. Based Civilians and Service Units, and so they not only met on the field but at many social functions as well. Very naturally, a number of rugby 'Die-hards' who had put a great deal into the Club, some over a number of years, had no wish to see, nor could they see the need of, other sports being amalgamated in the Club. Also, one must not forget that the Club funds had been hard won by these same rugby players at functions and over the bar. There was a genuine fear that these funds could be used to subsidize other sports.

Mr. Ricketts answered all questions put to him, as regards the intentions behind the resolution, with sufficient clearness to ease most members' minds. Most points raised were covered in the rules as proposed in the resolution. He said that up to that time all Clubs (Soccer and Hockey) had been self supporting and therefore, after inclusion within our umbrella, all sections would again be expected to be self supporting. The initial Committee would consider each Section's financial needs at the start of each season. Any grant given to a section at the start of a season must be repaid. Mr. Ricketts also said that there was nothing in the rules against lady members, a ladies team could join under the same conditions.

At this point I would mention that Club membership has always been open to lady members, although, in general, they have not been encouraged. My wife, Amy, is very proud of having been the first Lady Member of the Club, at least to be issued with a Membership Card, in 1958. Also, as previously mentioned in this history, Mrs. M. Rushworth and Mrs. M. Polkinghorne were Committee Members in years 1958-60, and Mrs. Rushworth was elected Hon. Social Secretary at the A.G.M. 1959.

The proposed rules were based on the existing rules, plus the rules previously mentioned, as required by the Malta Police Licensing Authorities, appertaining to Membership. Amendments had been made to the resolution, prior to being put to the members, particularly as regards keeping the word 'Rugby' in the name of the Club, and the following motions were put to the Members.

Motion I

That the Club Rules be changed to the following:

Rules of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Malta, G.C.

  1. That the name of the Club be: THE OVERSEAS RUGBY AND SPORTS CLUB, MALTA, G.C.

  2. That the object of the Club is to foster a true sporting spirit on the Island of Malta by providing facilities for:

    1. All Service and Civilian players who are unable to play for any other team, i.e. Members of Units unable to field a side.

    2. All Service and Civilian players, who, although committed to play for a Unit side during the week, wish to play for the Club on any other occasion.

    3. Non-playing sports enthusiasts who may support the Club and join in the social life.

  3. That the Management of the Club shall be entrusted to a General Committee of such officers as shall be elected at the Annual General Meeting. Liaison with the Representatives of the Services shall be effected by means of the Joint Services Representatives in each sport.

  4. That the General Committee of the Club shall consist of:

    • President

    • Vice-Presidents

    • Hon. General Secretary

    • Hon. General Treasurer

    • Bar Manager

    • Social Secretary

    • Secretary for each Section

    • Chairman for each Section

    The Officers to hold office for one year and to be elected by members at an Annual General Meeting.

  5. That the General Committee shall have powers to fill any vacancies which may arise in their number during the course of the year, have full power to co-opt as may be found expedient, and delegate any of its duties to such other Committees as may be necessary.

  6. Each Section shall be governed by its own Committee which shall consist of: Chairman; Hon. Secretary; Hon. Treasurer; Captain, and Vice-Captain. The Officers to hold office for one year and be elected by members at the Annual General Meeting. Each Section Committee shall be fully accountable to the General Committee for its management and its Chairman and Hon. Secretary shall be Officers of the General Committee.

  7. A quorum for the General Committee shall consist of one third of the Committee.

  8. The Annual General Meeting to be held in September and the financial year to end on the 31st August.

  9. Members to be given 14 days notice of a General Meeting. Items for inclusion on the Agenda to reach the Hon. General Secretary not later than seven days prior to the meeting. Extra-Ordinary General Meetings may be called by ten or more members or by a majority vote of the General Committee.

  10. The Club cannot accept liability for any loss, accident or injury which may occur on the field of play or on the Club premises.

  11. That the sanction of two thirds of the number of members present and voting at the Annual General Meeting or Extra-Ordinary General Meeting is required for the passing of any resolutions to alter the rules.
    Any other resolutions shall be passed by simple majority.

  12. Membership Fees:

    1. Playing Member - Annual Subscription £M1.

    2. Social Member - £M1.

    3. Student Member - 50 cents

    a, b, c. have full privileges.

  13. That these rules be placed on the Club Notice Board, and a copy be forwarded to each member.

  14. That in the event of the Club winding-up, the Committee in office at that time should be given authority to use any surplus funds in the furtherance of the name of Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Malta, G.C.

  15. New members must be proposed and seconded by existing members and elected by the General Committee.

  16. Membership will cease if

    1. A member gives notice in writing to the Committee to cancel his membership

    2. A member fails to pay his annual subscription by 31st December and the Committee removes his name from the list of members.

    3. A member is guilty of misconduct and in the opinion of the Committee his membership should be cancelled.

  17. Members may admit guests to the Club premises. The names and addresses of those guests must be recorded in the visitors' book and members are responsible for the conduct of their guests.

  18. No entrance fee is payable for admission to Club Premises either for members or their guests.

  19. The Club premises may be open to members and their guests on any evening from 4.30 p.m. to 1.00 a.m. providing a Committee Member be present.

  20. No beer, wine or spirits will be sold to any member or guests under 16 years of age.

Motion II

Should Motion I be defeated, then rules 15 to 20 in the said Motion be accepted as additional rules for the Overseas Rugby Union Football Club.

The result of the voting was not unanimous, 18 to 5 in favour of Motion I. However, suffice it to say, from this point in its history, the Club became a 'Sports Club', with all the expected teething troubles. Initially, Rugby and Cricket were the only Sections, and one must keep in mind that the Annual General Meeting, 1973, started off purely as that of the Overseas R.U.F.C. This meant, that at this A.G.M., not only must a Rugby Section Committee be elected for the year, but also a General Committee for the year. In succeeding years there would be separate Annual General Meetings for the General Committee and for each of the Sections. Vice-Presidents would be elected at the General Committee A.G.M., and as confirmed by Mr. Ricketts prior to the vote being taken, they would be working members of the Club and not a source of income. It was to be considered an honour.

Applications for New Sections

At the first General Meeting held on the 18th October, 1973, the U.K. Civilian Soccer Club and the Overseas Hockey Team applied to become, and were accepted, as Sections of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club. Although the 'Overseas' Hockey Team was so called prior to amalgamation with Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, they were two entirely separate Units. The President, Mr. C. Fox, confirmed that each Section should hold its own monthly meetings and report back to the General Committee at each meeting.

Membership Secretary

Also at this first General Committee Meeting, in view of the enlarged membership, it was decided that there was need of a Membership Secretary. Mr. N. C. Morgan was duly elected. The task had previously and normally been undertaken by the Hon. Secretary. This was a position that would have to be legalised at the next A.G.M., and so initially the Membership Secretary was 'co-opted' to help the General Secretary.

Hon. Social Secretary, Rugby Section

On the 12th November, 1973, the Rugby Section decided in committee that a Social Secretary was required, even though a General Social Secretary had been elected at the A.G.M. to serve all Sections. Mr. P. Joerning was elected as Hon. Social Secretary of the Rugby Section.

Rugby Section 1973/74

Against all pessimistic views, Rugby on the Island proved to be in a very healthy state, and Overseas had an excellent season. This was at a time when the resident teams had declined in number to 5, i.e. Overseas, R.A.F.; 40 Cdo. Gp. R.M.; 234 Signal Sqdn. and Kirkee Battery. Each of the 5 fielded an A and B team also 203 Sqdn. (R.A.F.) on one occasion fielded a B team. Overseas must thank the Team Captain, Niall Armstrong, who gave a great deal of his time to Rugby in Malta, not only for Overseas, both organising and playing. Also, during this season, we had a very keen and talented junior side. Other factors contributing to a good rugby season were the number of visiting naval ships, who could usually field an A and/or a B side, and the touring sides organised by the Services. The season started with 1st and B team practice games on the 20th and 21st October, 1973.

Whilst on the subject, the following list will show how the number of resident teams in Malta, who had provided regular opposition to Overseas XVs, had declined year by year due to Service station closures. Forts R.F.C. disappeared as a Club after the 1964/65 season, after which the Navy Shore Establishments continued to put out a team up to the end of 1966/67 as Fort St. Angelo. I believe Hawks R.F.C. was first formed as an R.A.F. small units team. It ceased to exist in name after 1973/74, although it probably carried on as R.A.F. 'B' Team. 234 Signal Squadron ceased as a team after 1974/75, although the unit provided players for an Mtarfa XV in 1975/76. I have not included the main Army opposition as this depended on the regiment serving in Malta at any particular time and this opposition is still provided by 41 Royal Marine Commando (1977/78).

Once again the National Sports Board, Malta, gave Overseas Rugby Section permission to use pitches, known of old as numbers 2 and 6, at the Marsa Playing fields. These rugby pitches were for the use of Overseas Rugby Team and their opponents. For use of these pitches we must thank Mr. C. Cauchi, who gave us permission on behalf of the National Sports Board, and also our Section Chairman, Mr. J. Sharp, who negotiated on the Club's behalf. We had to look after and mark the pitches ourselves, with the use of equipment loaned by the Services, and this was ably done by Dave Taylor, Dave Snaith, and Jim Masuwale. Number 2 pitch proved a little dangerous at times when the local Archery Club were practising. This problem was solved when they kindly agreed to change the direction of flight of arrows!

At the start of the season Col. W. E. B. Dansie agreed to be the rugby Press Relations Officer. Both Col. Dansie and Mr. Sharp gave the local papers details of games and results.

At the Annual General Meeting, Mr. D. McKenzie, the Student Representative, had informed the Committee that 20 players were keen to play for the B side. They were mostly pupils from Tal Handaq School. During the Season this B side became known as the 'Colts' or 'Junior Side'. Their ages varied from 14 to 19, with the odd stalwart to add strength, and one must appreciate that the only opponents available at this time were adult teams. They were organized and trained by Marcus Halliday, Dave Taylor, and Jim Masuwale, and very ably led by their Team Captain, Don McKenzie, and Vice-Captain, Clay Owens. Don's leadership on and off the field was excellent for such a young person. A number of the Juniors played for the Senior side including D. McKenzie, C. Owens, C. Laurie and J. Callens. On the 12th November, 1973, a 'B' Team Selection Committee was formed comprising D. McKenzie, D. Taylor, J. Masuwale and T. Agius Ferrante.

Fixtures were arranged on a monthly basis by the Joint Services Rugby Committee (J.S.R.C.). Previously teams had arranged their own fixtures, but with the small number of resident teams on the Island, the J.S.R.C. assured an even distribution of games. The duties of the J.S.R.C. also included discipline, tours, selection of Malta XVs and entertaining of touring sides. The J.S.R.C. had been in existence in Malta for a number of years in one form or another, and one of its main functions had been to control the use of the Service Pitches. The J.S.R.C. withheld permission to play on Service Pitches, now only at Safi and Hal Far, and no games were arranged to take place at the Marsa, now under control of the Maltese National Sports Board, until the winter seasonal rain started assuring continuing growth of grass. Overseas was represented on the J.S.R.C. by the 1st Team Captain Mr. N. Armstrong, and by the Junior Team Representative, Mr. M. Halliday.

During the season two problems arose concerning the Junior team, both of which were resolved to the benefit of Club and players. For the first time the Club had a large number of junior members, 25 in number, one or two as young as 14, and obviously, where functions were concerned, this age group could not mix with adult members. Most of the Juniors were also members of a club called the Junior International Sports Club (J.I.S.C.), and there was talk of the J.I.S.C. forming its own rugby team the following year. With the obvious talent amongst junior members, it was in the interest of Overseas to keep them with the Club. From decisions made in Committee, separate functions were arranged for the Juniors, including film evenings, and they were further represented on the Rugby Section Committee, by Mr. J. Masuwale, Junior Team Representative, and Mr. C. Owens, 'B' Team Vice-Captain.

During the season, as Secretary of the Rugby Section, I corresponded with Mr. L. Elliott, Secretary of Scarborough R.U.F.C. Colts, making arrangements for the Scarborough Colts to visit Malta during the following New Year. It was hoped to arrange games for them against our Colts and the Exiles. Unfortunately, Scarborough Colts had to start rebuilding their side at the start of the 1974/75 season.

The annual fixtures, Tal Handaq School versus the Exiles, took place over the Christmas Holidays. Although it was planned in Committee otherwise, the games were organized by the youths themselves and were extremely successful. Both games were won by the Exiles, the first by 27 points to 19, and the second by 11 points to 10.

Four touring sides were arranged by the Services; three were Welsh, namely, Ynysddu R.F.C., Hirwaun R.F.C. and Cardiff H.S.O.B., and the English team Canterbury R.F.C. The four parties were accommodated at Salina Bay Hotel at their own expense. Arrangements were made for each of the visiting teams to be hosted by a different home team. Unfortunately, the team which Overseas were asked to host and play against, Hirwaun R.F.C., cancelled their tour. This meant that Overseas had no fixture against any of the touring sides. Overseas was represented in the Malta XVs to play the tourists by Messrs. J. Glover; B. Cradden; N. Armstrong; D. Taylor; P. Joerning; C. Medlicott; C. Rice; K. Mathers; J. Masuwale; M. Pearce; and C. Owens.

In his end of season report the Team Captain said that our standard had fallen slightly from the previous season. He also said that the Colts XV was a joy to watch, they had produced some excellent players. This was confirmed by the Rugby Section Secretary. The Colts were a credit to Marcus Halliday, Dave Taylor and 'Gentleman' Jim Masuwale, who organized and trained them. Cricket Section 1974

The Cricket Section of Overseas was formed in 1965 and details of its inception have already been given. In September, 1973, the Cricket Section had just finished a very successful season. Mr. T. N. Peter Wright had been a regular source of runs, ably backed by Ralph Daley (Captain) and Trevor Ricketts. Peter Wright and Ralph Daley had finished the season with batting averages of over 40. On the bowling side Rob Ward, Mike Caseley and Dave Taylor had the best analysis.

For the history of the Soccer Section, and for details of the 1973/74 season, I am indebted to Mr. T. Ricketts. Also to Mr. D. W. Pennick, Joint Services Public Relations Officer, Malta, for allowing me to look through back copies of the Dolphin & Eagle.

At the time of applying for membership of Overseas, the Soccer Section was already a thriving Club, in fact, they started the 1973/74 season with the nucleus of the very successful side of 1972/73. The Club was formed in 1968, although a 'Team' had been entered in the league prior to this. At the time of amalgamation the Section had teams playing in the Joint Services Football Association Major and Minor Leagues. When first entered in the Major League, the side had a combination of Civilian and Army (Service) elements and they played under the name of Corinthians. After a year the Civilian element became predominant and took over. The side entered in the Minor League was purely Civilian and played under the name of U.K. Civilians. The Soccer Section's home ground, prior to the withdrawal, had been at the Marsa, the pitch in front of Marsa Lodge now used by Maltese Industrial Firm's Teams. After the withdrawal Corradino became the home ground, by kind permission of the Navy Authorities. The Soccer Section play in red jerseys.

On becoming a Section of Overseas the intention was, if possible, to play under the name of 'Overseas', although naturally, season plans were too far advanced at the time of amalgamation, to make any changes for 1973/74. For the 1974/75 season, the team, when playing in the Minor League, changed the name from U.K. Civilians to Overseas. Unfortunately, when playing in the Major League, because of the original entry terms, part Civilian and part Service, and Overseas Rugby and Sports Club being purely a Civilian Club, the name had to stay as Corinthians.

Football leagues and cups, as organized by the Joint Services Football Association (Malta), and in which the Overseas Soccer Section had an interest in the 1973/74 season (and still do), were as follows:

  1. Major League

  2. Minor League. For the 1973/74 season the Minor League was split into two with promotion and relegation at the end of the season, four up and four down. The two teams at the top of each division to play each other to decide overall winners of the Minor Unit League. Overseas, playing as U.K. Civilians, were placed in Division II, even though they had won the Minor League the year before.

  3. Minor Units Knock-Out Cup

Overseas Representative on the Joint Services Football Association for 1973/74 was Mr. T. Ricketts.

The 1973/74 season started off with a Major League game on the 15th September - Overseas, as Corinthians, played the R.A.F. at Corradino.

1973/74 proved to be the most successful season in the Soccer Section's history. It finished the season 2nd to 41 Commando in the Major League, Champion of Division II in the Minor League, and winner of the Joint Services Knock-Out Cup. Only 8 games were lost out of a total of 37.

The highlights of the season were two superb games in the Major League against 41 Cdo. Both times the Commandos managed to overcome the Corinthians by the odd goal, firstly 3 - 2 and then 4 - 3. Corinthians finished the league with 14 points from 10 games.

In the Minor League U.K. Civilians swept all before them and a succession of superb wins left us at the top of the table with 28 points from 16 games, 6 points in front of our nearest rivals '2 Troop'. Prior to the two games lost in this league during 1973/74, the U.K. Civilians had not lost for 4 years. U.K. Civilians completed a double in the Minor League when they won the Knock-Out Cup, beating St. Angelo in the final by 5 goals to 1.

Throughout the 1973/74 season Overseas was basically dependent on 16 players for both leagues, and this fact alone makes their achievements even more remarkable.

During the month of January, 1974, we were down to 12 players. Of the 21 players who appeared for Overseas during the season, 10 played 30 or more games out of the maximum of 37. In his end of season report, the Soccer Section Secretary, whilst commenting on, and because of, the tremendous team spirit, said it would be wrong to single out individual players. He did mention, however, that in keeping with the spirit of the side, Mike Newton played 15 times and was substitute for 10, Malcolm Panter played 10 times and was substitute for 18, also that two of the side scored over 100 goals between them out of 136 scored by the team.

Dennis Locke had been Team Captain for the last two seasons 1972-74, and in this task he had been ably assisted by Vice-Captain Phil Dobson, known to one and all as 'Dobbie'. Jeff Bonner did an excellent job in organizing numerous social functions.

Hockey Section 1973/74

As previously stated, the Overseas Hockey Team became a Section of Overseas Rugby and Sports Club on the 18th October, 1973. The Hockey representative, Mr. D. Taylor, spoke in support of the application for membership.

Officials of the Hockey Section for 1973/74:

Chairman Mr. D. Taylor
Hon. Secretary Mr. D. Ditcham
Team Captain Mr. R. Ward

For details of the history of the Hockey Section, and results for 1973/74, I am indebted to Mr. D. Taylor and Mr. D. Ditcham. I also found useful information in the Dolphin & Eagle.

Mr. Ditcham informed me that when he first arrived in Malta in September, 1971, there were several teachers at Tal Handaq, including Mr. Taylor and Mr. Ward, who had previously entered the Inter Service 6 a-side Hockey Tournament each year. Mr. Ditcham joined their ranks. As the majority of the players were already members of what was then the Overseas R.U.F.C., it was a natural development to call the team 'Overseas'.

Following the British Forces withdrawal and subsequent return, the Navy P.T. Department in Malta organized the Joint Services Hockey League. Teams were entered by St. Angelo, 41 Cdo., R.A.F. Luqa, 234 Signal Squadron and R.N. Hospital Mtarfa. Overseas entered the League at the start of the 1973/74 season. League fixtures for 1973/74 ran from October to February and from the 8 games played Overseas derived 4 points - a long way behind the Champions, 41 Cdo., who remained unbeaten.

During this season Overseas also played a number of friendlies against H.M.S. Hermes, H.M.S. Lynx, H.M.S. Hampshire and the local team 'Young Stars'. In this inaugural season Overseas Hockey Team played a total of 14 matches, scoring 17 goals and conceding 34. Overseas lost 3 - 2 to H.M.S. Hermes on Easter Monday.

With permission from the Navy, Overseas home ground was at Corradino. Overseas colours, white shirts and black shorts.

Although the hard core of Overseas players came from S.C.S., Tal Handaq, anyone was eligible to become a member of the Club and so play for the team. For the 1973/74 season the team was invariably made up from R. Ward (Captain), D. Ditcham, D. Taylor, H. Ritchie, Mave Turner, P. Joerning, P. Marchant, M. Lewis, J. Whitehead, F. Frendo, T. Ricketts, P. Wright, J. Bonner, and J. Glover. The only lady in the side, Mave Turner, was an exceptionally good player.

It is worth pointing out that of the names mentioned, at least four, namely, J. Glover, T. Ricketts, D. Taylor and P. Joerning, played, or had played for the 1st teams of each of the four sections, Rugby, Cricket, Soccer and Hockey. P. Wright played for the 1st teams of the Soccer, Cricket and Hockey Sections. Even from the few names mentioned, one can see the sound argument of those advocating joining the four sections under one roof.

In the Joint Services 6 a-side Competition held on Sunday, 3rd March, 1974, a total of 15 teams competed, and these were split into 3 leagues. Overseas 'A' qualified for the Knock-Out final rounds by leading their league. They were eliminated 1 - 0 by H.M.S. St. Angelo, the eventual winners, in the quarter final.

Social Events 1973/74

The amalgamation of the four Sections injected new life into the social activities of the Club. Not only were functions organized by the Hon. General Social Secretary, Mr. P. Joerning, but also additional functions were organized by each of the Section Hon. Social Secretaries.

The Film Evenings at the Club House continued as the main social events and were well supported. From February these evenings were arranged on the 2nd and 4th Thursdays of each month. Once again we must thank Jack Sharp for the supply of films and for arranging for Mr. Maurice Baldacchino to operate the equipment. Also, once it became accepted that there was a clash of interests between the Colts and some of the Senior Members, separate films and functions were arranged for the Junior Section.

An attempt was made to enter an Overseas team in the Bowling League. A dozen members turned out for a trial get together at the Malta Bowling Club on the 9th March. Further practices were arranged with the view of finding out if there was enough interest. A good time was had by all, but the idea came to nought.

As has already been mentioned in the Rugby Section report of 1973/74, we had been asked by the J.S.R.C. to host the touring team, Hirwaun R.F.C. Unfortunately, the Club made extensive preparations for their visit, including a disco at Marsa Lodge on the 24th March, before learning of the cancellation of this tour. This left a void when no social functions were held.

The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the Preluna Hotel Skyroom on the 30th March, 1974, and this event proved a tremendous success, with 120 paying guests attending. As Mr. Joerning said in his end of season report, it was an ideal occasion to honour the 14 members, of all sections, who were leaving the Island. Besides the official guests, Mr. & Mrs. Colin Cauchi, the ladies of the 5 Colts who had played for the 1st team attended. The young ladies were the personal guests of Mr. J. Sharp, Chairman of the Rugby Section. Commenting in Committee, Mr. Sharp said that this dance was the best he had attended in his 15 years association with the Club. All credit was due to Peter Joerning for the organization and success of the dance.

Dinner Dances were also organized by the Football Section at the Crystal Hotel, Bugibba, on the 19th January, and by the Cricket Section at the Marsa Sports Club, on the 27th April. Both dances were a great success and most enjoyable.

A special farewell dinner was held at the Lancer Hotel, Salina, in honour of Charlie and Jacky Rice when they left Malta in June. Charlie had been a popular member of the Club for 7 years and could only be described as a great character. Besides being a star on the Rugby and Football field, Charlie had the odd game of Cricket & Hockey for Overseas.

For the final event of the season Peter Joerning organized a barbecue during the summer at Armier. Peter deserves a great deal of credit for the tireless effort, always with a touch of quiet humour, he put into arranging social activities during the season. In his end of season report the President said 'His [Peter's] organization this year was most efficient as evidenced by the very successful dinner dance the Club held at the Preluna Hotel'.
