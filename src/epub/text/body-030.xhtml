On the 5th January, 1959, two representatives of the Forts, Jack Gibbons, Secretary, and Hugh Kay, Treasurer, proposed that the two Clubs, Overseas and Forts, should be merged as one socially, using Fort's Club House in Argotti Street, Floriana, registering as perhaps the Malta Rugby Club, whilst keeping the playing sides entirely separate. The idea being to improve both Clubs socially and financially. At an Overseas Committee Meeting held on the 15th January, 1959, this proposal was discussed from both the short and long term views. It was unanimously agreed to remain as a separate unit socially as well as a team and to continue to use our own Club.

Through the good offices of Major Scott Lowe 3members of the Overseas, Messrs Polkinghorne, Morgan and Swain, attended a Combined Services Rugby Football Referee's Course which took place between the 5 - 7 November, 1958, at 69 AEC, Beacon House, St. Andrews. The course was entirely theoretical and no practical refereeing was undertaken. Inclemental weather at the time of the course stopped the practical part. After the theory exam, held on the 7th November, at Beacon House, we were able to referee Service games on the island.

In the words of the Team Captain, Mr N.J. Swain, 1958/59 was an enjoyable season for the Overseas. Jim Swain was a great Club man and Team Captain. What he did not know about the blind side of the referee was not worth knowing. Leading scorers were Brian Hodgins with 73 points, including 15 tries, and Geoff Rushworth with 55 points, including 13 tries. A point worth noting is that both men were wing threequarters. The season was very short due to dry grounds.

CLUB HOUSE - GERRY'S BAR

From the beginning of the 1958/59 season a look out had been kept for likely premises for a Club House. On the 28th September, 1958, the Treasurer of the Forts, Sub. Lt. Hugh Kay, informed Overseas that there was a room vacant adjoining and belonging to Gerry's Bar, at the corner of Manoel Street and Gzira Road, Gzira (Behind Marshall Court). The room had originally been a small café and was fitted out with a small bar. It had a main entrance into Manoel Street and a connecting door into the main bar. A preliminary meeting to discuss details took place on the 20th October, 1958, between Gerry, the Proprietor, and Messrs N.C. Morgan, H.W. Polkinghorne, General Committee, and Mr & Mrs G. Rushworth, Social Committee. A Committee Meeting was held at Gerry's Bar on the 24th November 1958, to discuss the matter in Committee and enable the full Committee to look at the premises. It was decided to hire the room as a ClubHouse on a temporary basis as from the 1st December, 1958. The Club House was small, but a start. We did not register as a Club, Gerry's Bar was already licensed.
