Investigations had also been going on into a more economical
tour to Messina and in answer to initial enquiries by Mr.
Ewan MacPherson, Rugby Section Secretary, the Centro Universitario
Sportivo (CUS), Messina, invited Overseas to tour. This offer
was accepted. After a great deal of further correspondence,
telephone calls and telegrams on the part of Jack Sharp, Ewan
Macpherson and James Andrews (Vice-Captain), the tour was
finally arranged for the 13-17 April, 1977. Unfortunately,
after all their efforts, the tour had to be cancelled on the
11th April due to lack of support from Overseas members. At
the time of cancellation only 13 players were available and
this comprised 8 Overseas members, including Trevor Ricketts
hoping to make his second tour to Messina, and 5 guests from
41 Commando (3) and the R.A.F. (2). This had been looked upon
as the last possible chance for an Overseas XV to make a tour.
The money to enable the tour to be made, a total of £475, had
been raised by a series of raffles, mostly at film nights, profit
from a disco in November, and from tickets, cabaret and raffle
at the Rugby Section Dinner Dance. Added to this the Steering
Committee (on run down of Club) had recommended to the General
Committee that a grant of £475 be made towards the expenses of
the proposed tour. At the suggestion of Mr. Sharp, 6 volunteers
for the tour who had been inconvenienced to a significant extent
by the cancellation were given complimentary tickets for the
Club's Annual Dinner Dance, at a cost of £130, also tankards
and ties were bought for those leaving (£10.95). The remainder
of the money raised was donated to the Cricket League Tour to
Corfu. Earlier in the season two U.K. Teams, the West Midlands
Police and Merthyr R.F.C., contacted Overseas requesting they
tour Malta possibly at Easter. These contacts came to nothing.
Fortunately, H.M.S. Hermes were visiting Malta at Easter, 1977
and enjoyable games were played against the Hermes and the
Glamorgan on the 9th and 10th April, also H.M. Yacht Britannia
on the 24th April.

During the summer of 1977 the Team Captain, Brian Charnley,
and Bill Wood left Malta, and so the Overseas XV lost two of its
most consistant players and leading try scorers of the two seasons,
1975-77, added to which Brian had been one of the recognised
goal kickers. Both had represented Overseas in Combined XV's
and Bill played for the Navy (Malta) and usually in any Combined
Service XV's in Malta. Brian, who had been in charge of school-
boy rugby coaching and other requirements of the College rugby
sections during 1976/77 would also be missed in future school
rugby development. As Team Captain he had represented Overseas
on the J.S.R.C. and for his second and final year he had been a
Vice-President of the Club. Both men hard to replace.
