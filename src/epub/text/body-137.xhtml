In the 1974/75 season Tal Handaq did not have the playing strength to provide opposition for the Exiles during the Christmas holidays. Two games were arranged, Exiles versus an Overseas 'B' Team, for Saturday, 28th December, and Wednesday, 1st January. The first ended in a 10 - 10 draw and the second with an easy win for the Exiles 26 - 0. Major Ian Robinson, OIC 234 Signal Squadron, his son an Exile, together with Dave Taylor for Overseas, organized the series.

At the Annual Dinner Dance on the 19th April, 1975, the 'Most Improved Player' cup was presented to David Schembri, and the 'Young Player of the Year' Cup to Tini Agius-Ferrante. It was great satisfaction to the Club that the awards were won by Maltese players. It was also a pleasure that David, a regular 1st team player, was selected to play on occasions for the Malta XV.

The Marsa was proving to be a popular area for the Maltese people on a Sunday afternoon. The main attraction was of course the horse racing, but many made use of the playing area where the rugger pitches are situated, some I am pleased to say even stopping to watch what is a 'foreign' game to them. The National Sports Board started to control the use of the area. At the beginning of November, 1974, Overseas Members were issued with permit cards by the N.S.B. allowing them to use the 'Marsa Sports Club Rugger Pitches' and so the Marsa Car parking area by Marsa Lodge. The permits were signed by Mr. C. Cauchi, for the N.S.B., and Mr. T.N. Ricketts, Overseas General Secretary. For the 1974/75 season no serious effort was made to control cars entering the Marsa playing area from the Marsa end and these permits were never asked for on entry.
