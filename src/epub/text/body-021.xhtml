Together at Montgomery House, Floriana, on the 19th October, 1956, in order to form plans with a view to calling an A.G.M. No record remains of this get together, and possibly the Suez crisis forced its cancellation.

Although Overseas was still a civilian Club in name at the beginning of the 1956/57 season, it was virtually a service side and there was no Club life. There was no Club House and no Committee. There was no A.G.M. to appeal to civilians, and information and enrolment forms were distributed through service channels. In fairness to Jim Osbourne, there were no other civilian members to bring into the team, and the objects and functions of the Club, as promulgated to members and units, prove that it was still a Civilian Club and not just a Service team. At the start of the 1957/58 season another civilian, Jim Swain, joined the Club. The following team sheet shows the variation in the name of the Club, make up of the team and units in which players served.
