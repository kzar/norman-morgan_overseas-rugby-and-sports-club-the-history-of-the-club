Commenced on the 3rd December in the gymnasium of Tal Handaq School, by kind permission of the School Authorities. The Club rugby training films were now held by Marcel Cassar, who acted as Librarian. The films were now coming into their own as different bodies were interested in using them, including the R.A.F., and the new teams of the University and the Colleges.

Again, through the good offices of Jack Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, the Secretary of the National Sports Board, Mr. Colin Cachia, gave Overseas permission to use the rugby pitch at the Marsa on Saturdays and Sundays for Club games, and on Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays during the week for schoolboy training. Later in the season certain restrictions were imposed on the use of the pitch on training days, and on the odd occasion, such as the weekend of the 11th/12th December, we were unable to use the pitch due to local soccer being played there. During the season the Services did not need to use the Marsa for their home games. 41 Commando, with a Club House at R.A.F. Safi, now had their own pitch at Hal Far, and the R.A.F. still had their own pitch at Safi. The Colleges, University and Tal Handaq all used the one pitch at the Marsa with their games being played Monday to Friday. Through the good offices of the Naval Sports Officer, Lt. Dixon, and the R.N. Representative on the Rugby Section Committee, C.P.O. M. Kirk, the Overseas Club looked after the marking of the Marsa pitch and general care of the posts etc. During this season the Overseas Club had every reason to be grateful to the Representatives of the 3 Services who sat on the Rugby Section Committee. Not only for work done for the Club, but also for advice in Committee, and for the continued spirit of co-ordination of Overseas and Service Rugby.

The most important aspect of the season was the continued interest and improvement in the game shown by St. Edward's College and De la Salle College, and the starting of the game at the Royal University of Malta. The Colleges and S.C.S. Tal Handaq had joint training sessions during the week at the Marsa under Mr. Ricketts and Mr. Charnley. The University players were well coached by Marcel Cassar and not only provided 'B' team opposition themselves, but were keen enough to play for Overseas 'B' team on the occasion, also to join in Overseas Training. The first game played by the University (R.U.M.) team was on the 25th November versus Overseas 'B' at the Marsa and this was great credit to Marcel. Mr. Charnley undertook to deal with coaching and other requirements of the various College Rugby sections. In November he informed the Rugby Section Committee that with 4 Colleges/School/R.U.M. in Malta there was the basis of a Junior League, and by January he reported that Tal Handaq, De la Salle and St. Edward's were now playing each other fairly regularly. The R.U.M. players proved too big for schoolboy rugby. The only disappointment was that St. Aloysius College, although interested, did not field a team, in spite of Mr. Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, approaching the College suggesting schoolboy coaching. In March, Mr. Charnley ran an inter-College/School competition with St. Edward's, De la Salle and Tal Handaq taking part, and this proved a great success. At the time of the competition the R.U.M. were unable to field a side. Mr. Charnley said that the objectives in promoting a school's competition had been achieved.
