As shown above, Members who volunteered to do Bar Duties were named 'Bar Helpers', instead of, as previously Bar Committee Members. However, as described on Page 30 under 'Club House - Marsa Lodge', their duties were quite onerous without Committee work.

Up to the A.G.M. (1966/67) the financial year ended on the 30th September. At the A.G.M. it was proposed and carried, that because of the difficulty in organising the A.G.M. early enough in October, the financial year should end on the 31st March.

During this period the Club Accounts were audited by Turquand, Youngs and Co., Accountants of 10, South Street, Valletta. Four members who were on the staff of Turquand, Youngs and Co., and to whom the Club was most grateful, were Messrs. N. Johnson; J.L. Duncan; J.D. Green and P. Bennie. The first three named were Vice-Presidents of the Club between 1963 and 1969. Mr. Bennie was Hon. Treasurer of the Club 1965/67 although Mr. Green took over as acting Hon. Treasurer during the 1966/67 season. Mr. Green was elected Hon. Life Member of the Club at the A.G.M. of 1969/70. I believe there are other members of the staff of the same firm of Auditors who did sterling work for Overseas but unfortunately I do not know their names.

At the end of the 1965/66 season three very staunch supporters of the Club resigned from Office.

Jack Sharp had been President for four years and had decided not to stand for re election as he considered it time to make way for some other person. Jack, at this point of time (1966/67), besides being Vice-President, 1961/62, President, 1962-66, and serving on the Bar Committee, 1963-65, had helped the Club in many ways behind the scenes. One of the more obvious ways in which he helped the Club was to provide all the necessities to hold Film Evenings. It is very difficult to talk of Jack in the past tense, as he is still, at the time of writing this history (1975/76), one of the mainstays of the Club. However, we will keep to the 1966 A.G.M. for the present.

Vivian Morris, after 6½ years as Hon. Secretary, felt the time had come to hand over to a successor. Vivian joined the Overseas in 1959 and in the following seven years, besides the Office of Hon. Secretary, held the Offices of Hon. Fixture Secretary, 1959/60 and 1965/66; Hon. Team Secretary, 1960-62; Caretaker Bar Manager during the close season 1962; and served on the Club House/Bar Committee, 1959/60; 1961/62 and 1964/65. He rendered the Club invaluable service. Vivian was succeeded by Mr. E.W. Platt, who, unfortunately, was due to leave before the next A.G.M. For this reason an Assistant Hon. Secretary was elected at the A.G.M. 1966.

Ralph McGillivray had been with the Club for 5 years and his record was also quite impressive. Ralph an Overseas player for 3 years became Team Captain for the 1961/62 season. He served on the Social Committee, 1962/63; the Bar Committee, 1963/64; and Bar Manager, 1964-66. Ralph was also a first class referee.

Playing Season

The Team Captain, Brian Jackson, in his end of season report, said that 1966/67 was another successful season - most games which we lost were against combinations. The highlights of the season were the two games against H.M.S. Hermes, the Inter Unit 7's Championship, and the end of season tour by the Penguins. The only players that Brian mentioned were David Agar and Philip Parker for the number of points they scored, and Jack Handford for leading the team when he (Brian) was injured. Mention should also be made of Ted Platt, who, at the age of 43, played in practically every game during the two seasons 1964-66. Ted also Captained the side on the odd occasion. Two pupils of the SCS Tal Handaq, Brian Jackson and Ken Lawrence, are also worthy of mention. Brian played for Overseas for 4 seasons (1963-67) at scrum half, he also Captained the School XV for 2 seasons. Brian is no relation of Brian Jackson, Team Captain. Ken Lawrence played for Overseas for 3 seasons (1964-67) at centre and stand off. Ken was Vice-Captain of the School XV and Student Representative on Overseas General Committee. Both were selected for the full Malta XV for the match against the Penguins. The Cup for the Most Improved Player of the Year was won by John Hitt.

Overseas entered two teams in the Inter Unit Seven-a-side Rugby Championship played at the Marsa on Saturday, 25th February, 1967. Overseas 'A' lost to R.A.F. Hawks 'A' in the final, 14 points to 5. Interesting details of the 7's issued by the Secetary of the Combined Services Malta Sports Board were as follows:

  2. Total number of games played: 24

  3. Duration of tournament (playing time): 4 hours

The Penguins, as guests of the Overseas Club, toured Malta from the 5th to the 15th May, 1967, and proved to be very popular socially besides producing excellent rugby. I think that even Ben Bush would have learned some new songs. The Penguins played five matches in eleven days, won four, lost one and scored 113 points to 31. The R.A.F. were the only side to beat the Penguins. The last game, against a Malta XV produced the best rugby of the tour, with speedy passing and some excellent backing-up.

A humorous situation developed during this season at the Marsa. At the beginning of the season the Overseas Hon. Secretary wrote to the St. John Ambulance Association asking for a qualified member to attend our games every Saturday afternoon. The St. John Ambulance member or members duly attended complete with all the necessary first aid equipment. What the member could not understand was, why, with all their training and experience, they were no match for the magic sponge. They ran onto the field as soon as they saw a body lying on the ground writhing in agony, only to find, that by the time they reached the spot, the body was back in the scrum. I say humorous, there is always a touch of humour about the magic sponge. This is not forgetting the gratitude the Club felt for the St. John Ambulance Association and the members, male and female, who so kindly turned out giving up their own time.

Once again, during the 1966/67 season, the Club tried to interest Maltese youth in the game of Rugby. A letter was written to the Hon. Secretary, Malta Secondary Schools Association asking him to circulate to all schools within his jurisdiction informing them that the Overseas Rugby Club would like to encourage the Maltese to play and asking anyone interested to get in touch with the Overseas Hon. Secretary. Unfortunately, there was no response.

Social Events 1966/67

David Agar, Hon. Social Secretary, very quietly and very efficiently organised a highly successful social programme during the 1966/67 season. David left Malta at short notice during the summer of 1967 leaving no record of events. I do not think that the Club President, Mr. Peter Ratcliffe, will mind me quoting from his address.

"Our Social life, 1966/67, was left in the very capable hands of David Agar. Everyone will agree I know that David did a splendid job in organising our Film evenings - for which of course, we are permanently grateful to Jack Sharp, as well as for his continued interest and support. David also organised an excellent Cocktail Party at the Naval Base Officer's Club on the 30th January, 1967, when we acted as hosts to the other clubs, another cockers for the Penguins, for which Mr. and Mrs. Bill Woodley kindly lent us their splendid residence in Dockyard Terrace, several Hot-pots, including one when the Penguins were here, and finally the Annual Dinner Dance which went with a zip and at which I set a personal record in remaining almost sober for a change and not losing my specs. A splendid job by David."

The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the United Services Sports Club on Saturday, 29th April, 1967. Once again Hitty was Master of Ceremonies.

Brian Jackson took on the double role of Club Captain and Bar Manager and did the two jobs with a maximum of efficiency and a minimum of flap. One must also mention George Jackson, another character of this era. It was a little difficult to carry on a conversation with George as he had a strange dialect from north of the border and was slightly deaf. Nevertheless, nothing was too much trouble for George and Dorothy Jackson to do for the Club. Their son, Brian, as a pupil at SCS Tal Handaq, has already been mentioned under Playing Season. Finally, David Agar and Bill Woodley, by their efforts on the Social side, helped to make the Penguins Tour a success. The Club House remained open during the close season 1967.
