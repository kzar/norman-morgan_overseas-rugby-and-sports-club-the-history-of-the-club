It is difficult to be precise as to when we moved to Ta' Qali. I can confirm that we played at Luqa on the 27th November, 1949 against H.M.S. Falcon. On the 10th December, 1949, we played at Ta' Qali against the Med. Fleet. Also during the latter half of the 1948/49 season and the first half of the 1949/50 season we played once or twice at the Marsa, whether this was our home pitch, or the only pitch available at the time, I cannot be certain. During this period our away games against H.M.S. Falcon, the Fleet Air Arm Team, were played at Hal Far. At this time the runway was still in use and we occasionally had to take down the goal posts during a game when aircraft landed.

The use of the pitch at Ta' Qali started after Commander R.V. Brockman (now Vice-Admiral (Ret'd)) had scoured the island for a suitable piece of grass on which to play rugger. The area at Ta' Qali was decided on although it was hardly suitable as there were thousands of tiny shells. These were gradually cleared by the efforts of working parties, organised by Commander Martin Price R.N., the Commanding Officer at Ta' Qali, and the players.

New pitches were laid at Ta' Qali in 1952. It is recorded in the Times of Malta, 22nd November, 1952, that four full sized rugby football grounds had been constructed at R.N. Air Station (H.M.S. Goldfinch), Ta' Qali, to be shared with the R.A.F. and Army. By 1958/59 there were 6 full sized grounds at Ta' Qali allocated to different services. Two of these grounds were well laid near the perimeter road and were kept for Inter Service games and other special matches. The other four pitches were rougher, especially through continued use, but certainly an improvement on Luqa. The Australian Air Force was stationed at Ta' Qali from June, 1953 to approximately 1955, after which it was handed over to the R.A.F.

Overseas used Ta' Qali as their home ground from 1950 to 1960. From 1960 to the present date, our home ground has been at the Marsa. This final move was made when Marsa Lodge became our Club House. The rugby playing area at the Marsa was renovated in 1960 and the four pitches were known as Army 1 and 2 and Navy 1 and 2. Previously, the whole of the Marsa rugby playing area had been completely re-laid in late 1956.

Before the start of each rugby season Overseas received permission to use the pitches from the proper authority. This had been given by the Commanding Officer of the R.A.F. Station, Ta' Qali, and with the move to the Marsa, up to 1971/72, from the General Officer Commanding via the Secretary Army Rugby Football (Malta). In 1972 the whole of the Marsa playing area was taken over by the Maltese Government, and from this point we have played rugby at the Marsa by kind permission of the President of the Malta Sports Board.

During the early years, at least up to 1950, when the grounds were too hard to play rugby, the Overseas rugby team on the odd occasion played hockey against the rugby opponent of the day. On the odd occasion we played against civilian clubs. Imagine 11 rugby players, only one or two with knowledge of hockey, wielding sticks. We never won but we scared the living daylights out of the opposition!
