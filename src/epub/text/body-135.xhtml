Mr. N. Armstrong, Team Captain, described 1974/75 as a very successful season, as a Club we had a high percentage of players selected for the Malta XVs against touring sides. Again we ran two teams with most of the 1st and 'B' Team games taking place on Sundays and Saturdays respectively. Field training for the season, on the traditional Thursday evenings, started on the 24th September continuing until the evenings darkened earlier. Fitness training then took over, under the instruction of John Hodgeon, in Tal Handaq Gymnasium. The first games were at Marsa on the 12th October, 'B' Team vs H.M.S. Ark Royal Colts, and on the 13th October, 1st team vs H.M.S. Hampshire. Both comfortable wins for Overseas. Throughout the season 1st team opposition proved difficult to obtain, the already difficult situation of shortage of local teams being worsened by the fact that 41 Commando Unit was temporarily serving in Cyprus. Of the 15 games played by the 1st team only 4 were against local opposition, and this included playing R.A.F. 'A' 3 times. The other local opposition, being: (79 Kitklee) Battery. 'B' Team opposition was easier to find, 7 out of 16 games being against local opposition. For the first time, at least since Overseas started entering the competition in 1961/62, and excepting for 1971/72 during the withdrawal, the Inter Service 7's were not held. This was mainly because the army rugby was disorganized due to 41 Cdo serving in Cyprus.

The 'B' team, ably lead by Tim Agius-Ferrante, again gave a good account of itself, but how the make up of the team had changed! During the previous season (1973/74) the Club had 25 Student Members, mostly from S.C.S. Tal Handaq, who, strengthened by a number of older and more experienced players, had formed the 'B' team. In 1974/75 only one Student Member remained, most of the other had returned to the U.K., and the 'B' team took on a different complex. The main strength came from the staff of Tal Handaq and local U.K. Servicemen, but to the great pleasure of the Overseas Committee, a number of Maltese, one or two quite experienced, showed an interest in playing and learning the game. A great deal of credit for this must go to the 'B' team Captain, Tim Agius-Ferrante, himself an ex Head Boy of Tal Handaq, to Marcel Cassar and David Schembri, also to the comradeship which was built.
