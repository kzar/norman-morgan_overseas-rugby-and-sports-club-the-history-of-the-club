A number of the Juniors played for the Senior side including D. McKenzie, C. Owens, C. Laurie and J. Callens. On the 12th November, 1973, a B Team Selection Committee was formed comprising D. McKenzie, D. Taylor, J. Masuwale and T. Agius Farrante.
Fixtures were arranged on a monthly basis by the Joint Services Rugby Committee (JSRC). Previously teams had arranged their own fixtures, but with the small number of resident teams on the Island, the JSRC assured an even distribution of games. The duties of the JSRC also included discipline, tours, selection of Malta XVs and entertaining of touring sides. The JSRC had been in existence in Malta for a number of years in one form or another, and one of its main functions had been to control the use of the Service Pitches. The JSRC withheld permission to play on Service Pitches, now only at Safi and Hal Far, and no games were arranged to take place at the Marsa, now under control of the Maltese National Sports Board, until the winter seasonal rain started assuring continuing growth of grass. Overseas was represented on the JSRC by the 1st Team Captain Mr. N. Armstrong, and by the Junior Team Representative, Mr. M. Halliday.
During the season two problems arose concerning the Junior team, both of which were resolved to the benefit of Club and players. For the first time the Club had a large number of junior members, 25 in number, one or two as young as 14, and obviously, where functions were concerned, this age group could not mix with adult members. Most of the Juniors were also members of a club called the Junior International Sports Club (JISC), and there was talk of the JISC forming its own rugby team the following year. With the obvious talent amongst junior members, it was in the interest of Overseas to keep them with the Club. From decisions made in Committee, separate functions were arranged for the Juniors, including film evenings, and they were further represented on the Rugby Section Committee, by Mr. J. Masuwale, Junior Team Representative, and Mr. C. Owens, 'B' Team Vice-Captain.
During the season, as Secretary of the Rugby Section, I corresponded with Mr. L. Elliott, Secretary of Scarborough R.U.F.C. Colts, making arrangements for the Scarborough Colts to visit Malta during the following New Year. It was hoped to arrange games for them against our Colts and the Exiles. Unfortunately, Scarborough Colts had to start rebuilding their side at the start of the 1974/75 season.
The annual fixtures, Tal Handaq School versus the Exiles, took place over the Christmas Holidays. Although it was planned in Committee otherwise, the games were organized by the youths themselves and were extremely successful. Both games were won by the Exiles, the first by 27 points to 19, and the second by 11 points to 10.
Four touring sides were arranged by the Services; three were Welsh, namely, Ynysdu R.F.C., Hirwaun R.F.C. and Cardiff H.S.O.B., and the English team Canterbury R.F.C. The four parties were accommodated at Salina Bay Hotel at their own expense. Arrangements were made for each of the visiting teams to be hosted by a different home team.
