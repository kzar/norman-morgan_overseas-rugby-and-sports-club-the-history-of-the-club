At the Annual General Meeting the President, Mr. J. Andrews, recorded with regret the closing of the Cricket, Hockey and Soccer Sections, and noted that the Club name remained Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, thus leaving the possibility for further expansion in the future. In fact, to the end, there was no further expansion. As has been stated, the Calypso Diving Club did not become a section of Overseas although their representative sat on Overseas Committee and their members were Full Members of Overseas Club. With the closing of the sections it was accepted by members attending the A.G.M. that the Club should revert to its original form, i.e., no separate Cricket, Hockey and Soccer Sections, and that the President should thus chair all meetings. This was reverting Overseas to the form it took before the A.G.M. of the 20th September, 1973, when it was changed from a pure Rugby Club to a Rugby and Sports Club. This meant a change in the Club Rules. In making these changes it was also taken into consideration that the British Services would be leaving Malta at the end of March, 1979, and thus, for the first time since the Club was founded, the need for the Services to be worded into the rules no longer existed. It was decided to up-date the normal Full Membership qualifications, since the Service and U.K. Based Civilian qualifications were hardly valid any longer. The subscriptions were raised from £M1-00 to £M1-50.

I reproduce in full the Rules as formed at the A.G.M., 1978, and except for the fact that the word 'Malta' had to be taken out of the name of the Club, they were the Rules in use at the closure.

Rules of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Malta, G.C. - September, 1978

  1. That the name of the Club be: THE OVERSEAS RUGBY AND SPORTS CLUB, MALTA.
  2. That the object of the Club is to foster a true sporting spirit on the island of Malta by providing facilities for:
    1. All players who wish to play for the Club.
    2. Non-playing sports enthusiasts who may support the Club and join in the social life.
    3. Student members, who are still in full time education, who wish to become playing members of the Club.
  3. That the management of the Club shall be entrusted to a General Committee of such officers as shall be elected at the Annual General Meeting. Liaison with the representatives of the Services shall be effected by means of the Joint Services Representatives in each sport.
  4. That the General Committee of the Club shall consist of:
    • President
    • Vice-Presidents
    • Hon. General Secretary
    • Hon. General Treasurer
    • Bar Manager
    • Social Secretary
    The officers to hold office for one year and to be elected by members at an Annual General Meeting.
  5. That the General Committee shall have powers to fill any vacancies which may arise in their number during the course of the year, have full power to co-opt as may be found expedient, and to delegate any of its duties to such other committees as may be necessary.
  6. A quorum for the General Committee shall consist of one third of the Committee.
  7. The Annual General Meeting to be held in September and the financial year to end on the 31st August.
  8. Members to be given 14 days' notice of a General Meeting. Items for inclusion on the agenda to reach the Hon. General Secretary not later than seven days prior to the meeting. Extraordinary General Meetings may be called by ten or more members or by a majority vote of the General Committee.
  9. The Club cannot accept liability for any loss, accident or injury which may occur on the field of play or on the Club premises.
  10. That the sanction of two thirds of the number of members present and voting at the Annual General Meeting or Extraordinary General Meeting is required for the passing of any resolutions to alter the rules.
  11. Membership fees:
    1. Playing member: Annual subscription: £M1 . 50c
    2. Social member: Annual subscription: £M1 . 50c
    3. Student member: Annual subscription: 50c
    4. Temporary membership: 10 cents per match
    a and b only have full voting rights.
  12. That these rules be placed on the Club notice board, and a copy forwarded to each member.
  13. That, in the event of the Club winding up, the Committee in office at that time should be given authority to use any surplus funds in the furtherance of the name of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Malta, G.C.
  14. New members must be proposed and seconded by existing members and elected by the General Committee.
  15. Membership will cease if:
    1. A member gives notice in writing to the committee to cancel his membership.
    2. A member fails to pay his annual subscription by 31st December and the committee removes his name from the list of members.
    3. A member is guilty of misconduct and in the opinion of the committee his membership should be cancelled.
  16. Members may admit guests to the club premises. The names and addresses of those guests must be recorded in the visitors' book and members are responsible for the conduct of their guests.
  17. No entrance fee is payable for admission to the club premises either for members or their guests.
  18. The club premises may be open to members and their guests on any evening from 4.30 p.m. to 1.00 a.m., providing a committee member be present.
  19. No beer, wine or spirits will be sold to any member or guest under 16 years of age.

Deletion of 'Malta' from Club Name

In November, 1978, the Maltese Government passed legislation which forbade the use of the word 'Malta' by clubs, publications, etc, without government permission. This was discussed in committee on the 2nd October, 1978, and it was decided to ask legal advice as to whether the word 'Malta' in the Club's name clashed with this legislation. On the 16th October, 1978, after receiving advice from Andrews Feeds' solicitor against use of the word, Overseas Committee decided that the rules of the Club be altered so that the name of the Club no longer contained the word 'Malta'. No approach was made to the Prime Minister's office to ask to be an exception.

Rugby Section 1978/79

Playing Season 1978/79

In order to give as much background to the problems facing Overseas, and indeed all rugby enthusiasts, Maltese and British, in their attempt to keep the Club alive and the game played in Malta, I will give as much detail as possible of the different factors involved.

At the start of the playing season, including Overseas, there were still three major rugby sides. In order to have better fixtures and to give more people the chance to play rugby the Servicemen made two teams. Salerno Company 41 Cdo, as a unit, formed one of the Service teams, whilst the other was called Army Elements and consisted of R.A.F., Army and Royal Marines. It would appear that these Service teams were not as well defined as the season progressed and the time came near for the Service withdrawal.

The number of teams was increased by the visit to Malta of H.M. Ships Britannia, Alacrity, Ark Royal and Fearless in October/November, and London in March for the British withdrawal. With the initial increase in teams the season started off very well with plenty of rugby being played. Together with the promise of schoolboys' participation, the future looked promising.

The Overseas Committee spared no effort in not only making 1978/79 a successful season, but also in looking to the future by attempting to build up an Overseas team which would of necessity have to comprise mostly Maltese players. Also, everything possible was done in the effort to keep up interest in the schools, colleges and the Maltese Services, with the Overseas Team Captain acting as Liaison Officer in this field.

On the 1st October Overseas placed an advert in the Sunday Times (of Malta) which said 'RUGBY; ANYBODY WISHING TO PLAY OR WHO IS INTERESTED TO LEARN PLEASE CONTACT T. AGIUS FERRANTE'. Over 30 local men turned up, and the hope was to maintain this interest, although training players from scratch proved a slow job. A number of these recruits were from the Polytechnic. On the 10th January, 1979, it was reported that Overseas, St. Edwards, Army Elements, Salerno and some R.A.F. players enabled a game to be played at Marsa most Saturdays. On the same date it was reported that 'recently 12 Maltese played for Overseas'. Throughout the season, admittedly with the help of Service players, and the 'B' Team being boosted by a few 1st Team players, Overseas was able to field two teams.

Every effort was made by Overseas to promote interest in the University, the Colleges of St. Edwards, De la Salle and St. Aloysius, and the Polytechnic. An approach was made to any school which showed interest, together with an offer of coaching. The Colleges and schools were represented on Overseas Committee. Unfortunately, the only college to successfully and continuously field a team was St. Edwards under the guidance of Father Ferro. Throughout the 1978/79 season, in spite of interest shown, De la Salle and St. Aloysius did not field a team. St. Edwards gave reason to believe that interest would continue to be shown in rugby in Malta, and they were rewarded by a number of victories over Overseas 'B' Team. The schoolboys team called the Minestrones, comprised of schoolboys being educated abroad, once again proved an attraction and a hope for the future. The Minestrones played over the Christmas and Easter holidays. A number of schoolboys were keen to play for Overseas and were given every encouragement.

The Armed Forces of Malta played no active part in the 1978/79 season, although in May, 1979, Marcel Cassar, Army Rugby Representative, reported that he had been trying for some time to organise a Services (Malta) rugby team. Marcel was asked by Overseas to represent the Armed Forces on Overseas Committee but he was unable to accept.

Two competent referees, Paul Miller (Head of referees in Malta) and Bob Eaton, both in the R.A.F., officiated Overseas' games until they left Malta early January, 1979. Both were willing to give coaching and lectures in schools and to those amongst the Maltese who were interested, also to run a referees course. Derek Holt from the Royal Marines offered his services in assistant coaching. When Bob Eaton left in January, Overseas Team Captain, David Schembri, stressed the need for referees. As the Service referees left Malta, the schools representative from St. Edwards, Dave Howell, offered to referee at least school matches. Fortunately, for the 1979/80 season, two competent referees joined Overseas, namely Dave Gribbon (May, 1979) and Jim Young (January, 1980). Jim Young was also a qualified coach. For the 1979/80 season the Armed Forces of Malta also provided a referee.

Back to the 1978/79 season. Permission having been given to Overseas by the Maltese authorities to use the pitch at Marsa on Thursdays for training, and Saturdays and Sundays for games, training started in September. No indoor training took place throughout the season although the Club approached both the Ministry of Sport and the Ministry of Education for use of one of the ex-British Service gymnasiums. The first game of the season, in spite of the ground being rock hard, was played on the 14th October against H.M.S. Britannia. After this game, during the social gathering of the teams in the Overseas Club House, the Cup for the 'Most Improved Player for 1977/78' was presented to W. Ellul-Bonnici. Though it meant a great deal of work for Overseas Committee, no outstanding problems arose during this season, and except for a period in January and February there were games most Saturdays or Sundays. The last game that an Overseas XV was ever to play against a British Service XV was on Saturday, 24th March, 1979, versus H.M.S. London. A memorable but sad occasion. After the British Services left Malta at the end of March, the rugby season came to a close with 2 games versus the Minestrones and St. Edwards College.

Besides the games against H.M. Ships, the highlights of the season were the visit of the touring side, Tallywain Rugby Club, from Gwent, the 7-a-side Tournament, and undoubtedly the tour by 'Seas to Sicily.

From all reports, the 7-a-side Tournament sponsored by Farsons, and organized by Overseas, was a success, even though rain and wind spoilt it from the spectators’ point of view. The schoolboys put up an excellent show though unfortunately they were picked to play against the Royal Marines 'A' Team, winners of the competition. Most information is to be gained from a newspaper report, and I quote:

"A 7-a-side tournament was held at the Marsa on Saturday, December 23, at which six teams competed for prizes presented by Simonds Farsons Cisk Ltd. It was encouraging for the future of Rugby on the island to see two good school sides (one from St. Edwards and one made up of boys schooling in U.K.) and also quite a number of Maltese players.

It was most unfortunate that a strong wind and a heavy rain spoilt the enjoyment of the spectators for the second half of the tournament. Nevertheless some entertaining fast Rugby was played in difficult conditions by the two Commando sides, the schools' teams and the Overseas.

Due to the weather conditions the presentation of the trophies, a silver salver and cups to the winners, and silver coasters to the runners-up, was postponed until Boxing Day, when Mr. A. Tabone, Marketing Manager of Simonds Farsons, made the presentations at the Overseas Club. Mr. Dave Schembri, Captain of the Overseas, presented Mr. Tabone with an Overseas Club tie."

From correspondence and personal contact hopes were high that 1978/79 season would exceed all others in the number of touring sides to visit Malta. Overseas Committee spared no effort or trouble in trying to attract the potential tourists. At one time or another Overseas Committeemen were in touch with no less than nine clubs who showed interest in visiting Malta, viz. Moseley R.F.C., SS Vecchia Roma, Sun Life Assurance Company's U.K. team called Wimbledon Rugby Club, British Airways Rugby Club called Treferest, Bedwas R.F.C., Tallywain Rugby Club, Selkirk R.F.C., John Evans XV, and Sicilflora Rugby Club from Milazzo. However, in spite of the hours of Committee work and correspondence, the records show that only Tallywain R.F.C. visited Malta in the 1978/79 season. For one reason or another all the others dropped out.

Tallywain Rugby Club, from Gwent, Wales, arranged to tour Malta between the 28th November and the 5th December. Unfortunately, their flight was delayed three days at Luton, and consequently they could only fit in the one game against Overseas on Sunday, 3rd December. Tallywain played good rugby, beating Overseas 23-10, although the home side was not disgraced. Jack Sharp (who has worn a trench alongside the rugby pitch at the Marsa running the line for Overseas), stated his delight that 'Seas played so well, scoring two tries in the 2nd half. Rugby aside, the tour was not the success hoped for. The opinions expressed were that the visitors were all great singers, but not the most social crowd, and rather "standoffish". However, the main thing is that Overseas appreciated the good game of rugby.

The Overseas tour of Sicily, between Wednesday, 28th February, and Sunday, 4th March, 1979, was a great success and certainly provided a boost to rugby in Malta. As the correspondent in the Times (of Malta) wrote, after playing various combinations of 41 Cdo R.M. and Overseas against each other for the past two months, it gave the low spirited players on the island a new lease of life.

There were problems, such as accommodation and the type of pitches, but they were all taken with a great sense of humour, and a grand time was had by all the touring party. If space allowed, I would quote Dave Schembri's report in full, it is a masterpiece of fact and humour. A good report, already commented on, was written in the Times by 'a correspondent' whose name was unknown to me, but I am told he was one of the Commandos.

There were problems, as expected under the circumstances, of getting sufficient players to travel, but on the final count 16 players made up the touring party, 11 Servicemen and 5 civilians. They were ably led (perhaps astray according to Dave's own report) by Dave Schembri (Captain) and Derek Holt (Vice-Captain). It was a great effort by Dave Schembri, Derek Holt, Charlie Borda and Francis Roberts, and of course the whole Overseas Committee.

The party travelled to Catania by the ferry 'Malta Express' and from there by train to Milazzo, which was to be their base whilst on tour, and where they were to meet their first opponents, Sicilflora Rugby Club. Signore Santino Cordraro, who was the main liaison man between the two clubs, had met them at Catania. The team was divided into groups and accommodated in three different houses, including one belonging to Signore Franco DeBartollo, the Milazzo President.

The first game, against Sicilflora, was played on the afternoon of Thursday, 1st March, on a pitch, if one can call it that, consisting of grit and volcanic ash with large stones showing through. On first seeing this pitch, the players must have been as horrified as were the players of the previous Overseas team to visit Sicily in January, 1968 (Page 67 of The History of the Club). The posts consisted of metal T.V. poles. The opposition proved to be a weak side, with little knowledge of the game, and it was one-sided in 'Seas' favour. Overseas won the game 54 points to nil.

The tourists had two days for sightseeing and the main feature seemed to be a trip by hydrofoil to the volcanic island of Lipari, a distance of 10/15 miles. A worthwhile trip as the place was very beautiful.

The second match was against Clan Rugby Club of Messina at 0830 hours on Sunday, 4th March. This early start was caused by the problem of the Club having to share a pitch with a local football side who refused to let them use it on the Saturday afternoon as originally planned. Overseas had to be at Catania at 1200 hours the same day to catch the return ferry to Malta, and so, by the time the posts had been installed, there was only time for 'halves' of 25 and 20 minutes. It proved altogether a different match, although played on the same type of gritty surface, for Clan Rugby Club were a bigger and better organised side than Sicilflora. For the most part the game was fairly even but the home side eventually won 16-6.

Sicilflora 0 Overseas 54
Clan 16 Overseas 6

Club House and Social Activities

The year 1978/79 proved to be the last full rugby playing season before the closure, and consequently to be the last year with a fairly full social calendar. The initial decision to close the Club was taken in April, 1980, and the Club House was given up in June, 1980. During May, 1980, the last social event organised at Committee level was held and so I will write of the Club's social life up to this point.

To the end, the Annual Dinner Dance was the important social event of the year, and the last three, all at different venues, followed the normal successful pattern bringing all rugby minded people on the Island together socially. The 1978 Annual Dinner Dance, venue Old Villa Rosa (85 attended), 20th May, organized by Michael Newton, was not quite the last social function to be held before the retiring Committee Members and U.K. Based Teachers left Malta. The cricket "Farewell to Overseas" dance was held at the Marsa Sports Club on the 8th July, 1978, and the occasion was used to say farewell to all Overseas leavers. For the 1979 Annual Dinner Dance a very enjoyable evening was organized by Charles and Doris Borda, venue St. Thomas Tower, Marsascala, 17th March. The occasion was used to present a plaque to referee Paul Miller in appreciation of his services to the Club. Finally, the last Annual Dinner Dance to be held, again organized by Charles and Doris Borda, venue Capua Court Hotel (51 attended), 2nd May, 1980, was the last social event to be organized at Committee level.

The three other social occasions in 1978/79 were the functions held to say farewell to Ewen & Margaret Macpherson and James & Liz Andrews, and the Christmas 'Island Rugby Fancy Dress' dance held at St. George's Social Centre, 29th December, 1978. The latter was run as an Overseas event with profits going to both Overseas and Calypso Clubs. The farewell to Ewen & Margaret was in the form of a dinner at the Astoria Hotel, on Saturday, 14th October, 1978, given by both Overseas and the Calypso Clubs. A farewell film and a farewell dinner were given in honour of James and Liz Andrews in May, 1979. The film show was given by Jack Sharp at the offices of KRS in Valletta, and the dinner was at the Astoria Hotel. As a token of appreciation the Committee presented James with a Maltese Silver Dghaisa and Liz with a Mdina Glass Tray. Guests of honour at the Dinner were Dave Gribbon, successor to James at Andrews Feeds, and Mr. & Mrs. Charles Pace. At this time, although leaving Malta at the end of May, James was still President of the Club.

Every effort was made to make use of the Club House during both the winter rugby season and during the summer when the Calypso Club was more active. The Bar was run efficiently by Charles and Doris Borda, helped at times by a roster of volunteers. Up to Christmas 1978, Charles reported a successful and active season, both with the Rugby Section and with Calypso Sub-aqua Club Members. From then on use of the bar declined, except for film nights, which were well attended and most successful, and latterly with the BSAC lecture nights. For 1979/80 Charles reported that barwise it was not a good year and the bulk of sales was thanks to Calypso members. Early in the 1978/79 season two games nights were held in the Club House. The first, organized by the Social Secretary, Dave Schembri, with the R.A.F., was poorly attended by 'Sea members. The second, organized by the Calypso Club, was a success.

Thanks to Jack Sharp and Charles Pace, films were provided by the M.U.F.C., later known as K.R.S., when the Maltese Government banned the commercial use of the word 'Malta, for film nights held weekly at the Club House during the winter months of 1978/79 and 1979/80. The same 'team' were instrumental in getting a pay box telephone installed in the Club. Later, after this type of phone proved too expensive for its limited use, an ordinary private telephone was installed in its stead. Costs for the latter type of phone were met by Andrews Feeds, in return Andrews were allowed to use it should the need arise. During 1978/79 structural alterations were carried out inside the Club House, both to provide more space and for better projection of films, with costs being met by Andrews Feeds. The labour was carried out by contractor Mr. M. Agius, Club members and Andrews Feeds.

The last minuted social event to be held in the Club House by Overseas, prior to relinquishing it, was a Social Evening on the 8th February, 1980, when Overseas hosted important people with whom the Club had had contacts. The list of guests for this occasion included such people as the Minister of Sport, the Directors of Andrews Feeds and the Directors of Simonds Farsons Cisk. Arrangements for this function were made by a sub-Committee consisting of Tim Agius-Ferrante, Charles Borda, and Charles Mock.

For a number of years, due to the extension of Dockyard planning, there had been the threat of 20 Flagstone Wharf being demolished. In April, 1979, came a further threat when James Andrews, President of Overseas, but also the General Manager of Andrews Feeds, owners of the premises, informed Overseas Committee that the continued availability of the premises as a Club might be in the balance owing to a re-organization to be made by the Mill. James left Malta in May, 1979, returning to Belfast. In July, 1979, when Jack Sharp spoke in Committee concerning the possible disposal of the Andrews property at Flagstone Wharf, he paid tribute to James Andrews for his efforts, both in Malta and in Belfast, to ensure that it did not happen to the detriment of Overseas. Thanks to the efforts of both James Andrews, and his successor at Andrews Feeds, Dave Gribbon, Overseas retained use of Flagstone Wharf up to June, 1980, when the rundown to the Club Closure had started, and need for a Club House for Overseas no longer existed. The Calypso Diving Club had by this time been offered the use of the Metropole Cinema as a Club House. The furniture from Overseas Club House at Flagstone Wharf was given to the Calypso Club, ostensibly on loan.

Departures from Malta

Inevitably, with the large numbers of Overseas players and committeemen, including Calypso Club members, leaving Malta in the months leading up to the final British Forces withdrawal in March, 1979, many left without being mentioned in dispatches. Besides Ewan Macpherson and James Andrews, the longer serving members of the Club, those leaving included Francis Roberts, Secretary, Derek Holt, rugby player and Calypso Representative, and Paul Miller, Referee's Representative. Also, in January, 1979, the President mentioned that Mal Hunter, Bob Eaton (Referee), Bob Lee and Steve Groom had regrettably left Malta. All these had given a lot of service to the Club, both as officials and players.

Ewan Macpherson

After 3½ years with Overseas, as a very keen rugby player and official, Ewan left Malta at the end of October, 1978. Ewan was also a member of the Calypso Diving Club, and as he said himself, he had a foot in both camps. The service given by Ewan to Overseas is best described by James Andrews in Correspondence to me. I quote:

"Ewan, through his efforts, by example on the field during matches, and in preparation and coaching in training, did more to improve 'Overseas' rugby (Particularly forward play) in their last few years, than anyone. His contribution on the social side, linking Calypso Club with Overseas, was vital also."

Membership

The last recorded Membership for 1977/78 was on the 18th April, 1978, before the Calypso Diving Club members increased numbers. This came to 103. For 1978/79, with the departure of the British Services at the end of March, 1979, it had been expected that Membership would drop drastically. Fortunately, as David Schembri, Membership Secretary, was able to report at the A.G.M. on the 27th September, 1979, the loss of members from Service personnel was made good by Calypso members joining Overseas. On this date, the Calypso Section made up 75% of the overall membership which stood at 92. The last ever recorded Membership of Overseas Rugby and Sports Club before the Closure, when the total stood at 70 paid-up Members, was on the 18th February, 1980.

Malta Cavaliers

On returning to U.K. late summer 1978, Trevor Ricketts, Life Member of Overseas, was determined to keep the ex-members of Overseas together, and in some way keep the memory of the Club alive. At the Annual General Meeting, 27th September, 1979, the Chairman, Cyril Fox, reported that Trevor had revived the old Overseas Cricket XI as the Malta Cavaliers. The Cavaliers meet for approximately one week each year in late summer when a short Tour is undertaken. The Tour in 1980 was made to Hampshire where they played 4 games. During this tour, on behalf of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Cyril Fox presented the Cavaliers with a cheque for £50 from the Overseas funds.
