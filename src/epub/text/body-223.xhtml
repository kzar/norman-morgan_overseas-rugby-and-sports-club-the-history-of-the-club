Annual General Meeting, Club House, 21 Flagstone Wharf, Marsa,
27th September, 1979.

Present 17 Members.

The A.G.M. opened with Caretaker President, Mr. C. Fox, acting on behalf of the
President, Mr. J. Andrews, absent in U.K.

At this A.G.M. it was decided that the privilege of being President should be
extended to one of our Maltese Members who has given service to the Club, and
that as far as possible the activities of the Club should be run by the young
Maltese Members.

PLAYING SEASON

The rugby season of 1979/80 in fact turned out to be the last season in which
an Overseas XV took to the field. Inevitably it proved, after the British Forces
left Malta, in spite of valiant efforts to keep the game going, there were too
many insurmountable problems for rugby to continue in Malta. The first crack
appeared in Committee on the 31st January, 1980, and so, first, I will complete
the history to this point. From the 31st January decisions made in Committee
gradually planned the Club's Winding-up and then Closure, and in spite of these
decisions, a rearguard action was fought by the few rugby enthusiasts to save the
game in Malta.

In April, 1979, after the British Forces pulled out, Overseas Secretary,
George Attard, reported that within the Club there was a strong determination to
carry on, although the immediate problem was the almost complete lack of
referees. This problem was fortunately remedied. In May, 1979, the President,
Mr. J. Andrews, said it was very difficult to put one completely in the picture
as circumstances were changing so quickly. One minute one felt pessimistic
about the future of rugby in Malta, and the next, one could be very optimistic.
In June, 1979, George Attard again reported that the members were looking to the
future.
