Playing Season

1963/64 was a season of mixed fortunes. Overseas started on a bright note defeating D.E.R.R. Wanderers by 14 points to 3, but this proved to be the only Saturday win of the season. Starting in January, five Sunday games were arranged which made it possible to play Overseas players otherwise committed on a Saturday (Service Teams) with the result that a more balanced team could be fielded. This produced a much higher standard of rugby. Three of the Sunday games ended in wins.

The highlight of the season was without doubt the tour of the Combined Services side (Army and R.A.F.) from Tripoli, N. Africa. This tour was arranged by the Combined Services (Malta) although Overseas was represented on the Working Sub-Committee, which made arrangements for accommodation, transport and entertainment for the touring side, by Mr. C. V. Morris (Hon. Secretary) and Mr. E. Battye (Hon. Social Secretary). Four games were arranged for the tourists, namely against the R.N., the Army, the R.A.F., and the Overseas Club, all played at the Stadium, Marsa. For the game against the Overseas on the 14th December, 1963, we were honoured by the attendance of His Excellency the Governor, Sir Maurice Dorman, accompanied by the British Ambassador to Italy. The Governor was presented to both teams. Although the Overseas came out losers, the teams were closely matched, and the game proved to be one of the best of the season for Overseas.

Overseas entered one team in the Inter Service Unit 7's at the Marsa on the 15th February, 1964. The team, namely, Handford, Randall, O'Brien, Mac, Spiers, Henry and Brown, was knocked out by Luqa, 5 points to 3 in the first round.

The Team Captain, Jack Handford, spoke highly of a number of schoolboys (Tal Handaq) who played for Overseas during the season, namely, Geoff Randall, wing, Ian O'Brien, centre; Brian Jackson (only 15 years of age) scrum half; and Tex Brown, forward, who won the 'Sam Ward Cup' for the most improved player of the season. This was the first year that the Sam Ward Cup was presented. Other players mentioned were Paul Flack, stand-off half, in his fifth and final year as an Overseas player, who proved to be one of the mainstays of the side, Charles Burton, wing forward, R. Sussex and Army, Peter Henshall, prop, a good all round player with vast experience, Harry Barstow, R. Sussex and Army, centre, Keith Donavon, D.E.R.R. Wanderers, scrum half, Derek Henry, prop, and John Why (Vice-Captain), lock. In summing up, Jack Handford said that as regards match results it could not be said that 1963/64 season was a good one for Overseas, but from the point of view of promoting rugby on the Island the Club played a large part. And this is one of the main objects of the Overseas Club.

Social Events

In the months before Christmas most of the efforts of the Social Committee went into making a social success of the Malta tour by the Combined Services, Tripoli. The overall planning was done by the Working Sub-Committee but the special task for Overseas was to hold a Social Evening for the Other Ranks at our Club House, Marsa Lodge, on the 12th December, 1963. This was a success, helped by the fact that 6 crates of free beer were consumed during the evening. During the same evening an Official Cocktail Party was held at the Officers Mess Castille (Army H.Q., Auberge de Castille, Valletta). Overseas was represented at this function by Jack Sharp (President), Vivian Morris, (Hon. Secretary), Jack Handford (Team Captain) and Frank Kitson (Committee Member).

From the beginning of the season Mr. J. Osman took over from Mr. E. Battye as Social Secretary, Praise for the success of Social activities must go to both for work done whilst they were in office.

A Tramps Ball held at the Club House on the 6th March, 1964 provided a first class evening. The band of the Royal Sussex Regiment attended.

The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the United Services Sports Club, Marsa, on the 15th May, 1964. Financially, the Club lost £34 on the evening but, bearing in mind that a subsidy of 2/6d was paid on 125 tickets and 14 tickets at 17/6d each were donated to Club Guests, the loss was not out of proportion to the enjoyment and goodwill created on behalf of the Club.

The Club House was closed for the summer months from 30th April to the 3rd September, 1964.
