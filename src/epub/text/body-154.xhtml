Overseas players, that is, players who owed allegiance only to Overseas, were adversely affected, also the opposition started remarking on similarity of teams. The meeting was handled with great tact and good sense by Jack Sharp and Overseas Team Captain, Brian Charnley, to the general satisfaction of all concerned. 41 Cdo were friends of all gathered and no one wished to offend. The rugby community in Malta is of necessity a very close unit, and as Brian Charnley said on addressing the meeting ‘Overseas' was unique, it was more than a Club, the team was made up of Civilians, 41 Cdo, Battery, Navy and R.A.F. From this point Overseas recreated its own identity and rugby was more enjoyable for all concerned. This was a chance for Brian, also James Andrews, Team Vice-Captain, to pep up Overseas players on lack of fitness, asking them to let the Captain know they wished for a game, promptness on the field etc. - The meeting was a good airing of views and a success. At the end of the season Jack Sharp stressed that in view of the difference of opinion encountered this season, we should have Service Representatives on the Rugby Section Committee next year. The same practice was adopted when we had our Club House at Nasra Lodge.

Consequent to the above, the second half of the season was more enjoyable and according to the Captain, our standard of play generally improved. He described the season as reasonably successful after a disappointing start. Brian did not comment on the play of any individual, but there is no doubt that Brian himself earned his place playing at scrum half for the Malta XV. Dick McArdle and Brian were our most consistent players. Credit is due to Marcel Cassar who captained the 'B' Team under great odds, always short of players. He gave a great deal of his time and energy looking for players during the week, and he was a tower of strength on the field. No player was considered to have earned the 'Most Improved Player of the Year' Cup. The 'Young Player of the Year' Cup was presented to Peter Traynor.
