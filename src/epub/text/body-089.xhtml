The Team Captain in his end of season report mentioned
only one player. He described Derek Kiddle, from a Captain's
point of view, as the ideal player, never missing a training
session or a game, always on good time, and always going hard.
The President, when particularly referring to people leaving,
mentioned B. Jackson, a grand stalwart of all sports and of
the Overseas Club, Peter Miller, whose forceful runs down the
touchline thrilled all, and Derek Kiddle, who tackled all he did
with the utmost vigour, whether on the field or in the Club. John
Symes won the Cup for the 'Most Improved Player' of the year.

For the fourth successive year John Evans brought a touring
party to Malta from South Wales, on this occasion from the 8th
to 14th April, 1971. This time, due to the rundown of the
Services, the Army (Malta) authorities did not give permission
for them to be accommodated in the Services Transit Accommodation
Lintorn Barracks, Floriana. The party of 20 booked themselves
into flats. John Evans XV played against Overseas on Saturday,
10th April, and against the Combined Services on Monday, 12th
April. No record remains of the names of players and officials
in the party, or of the results and scores. Only because the
teams which Overseas lost to during the season were named, do
I know that Overseas were victors in the Overseas/John Evans
XV game. In spite of this, John Evans spoke of it as being an
excellent tour. This was the last tour that John Evans' party
made to Malta. During their four tours they made a great
contribution to rugby on the Island, they played magnificent
entertaining rugby, and Overseas has much to thank them for,
particularly John Evans.

The Army (Cyprus) under Major P. Winchcombe, R.E.M.E.,
toured Malta in December, 1970 playing against Overseas on
5th December. The RAF (Cyprus) toured Malta in January, 1971,
playing against Overseas on the 30th January. Overseas lost
to the Army 17 points to 0, and the R.A.F. 30 points to 0.

The Tours Secretary, Eddie McClure, also carried out
correspondence with Clifton RUFC, and the Secretary or Manager
of the Paris Team, Didier Borsenaz, brother of Phillipe,
former Overseas Player. Both these teams expressed a wish
to tour Malta at Easter, 1972, and it was proposed by Overseas
Committee to hold a Rugby Festival over the period covered
by their intended visits. John Evans also expressed a wish to
take part in the Festival. It was further proposed that a
Committee, consisting of two Overseas Members, T.N. Ricketts,
and W. Lewis, and three other Members, one from each of the
Services, be formed to prepare the Festival. Major R. Hurley,
Chairman Army RU(Malta), agreed that this was an excellent
proposal and stated that he would suggest at the following
Committee Meeting of the Inter Service Rugby Union (Malta)
that it be accepted. This was not to be. With the with-
drawal of the British Forces in January, 1972, and the consequent
temporary disbandment of Overseas RUFC, no rugby was played on
the Island during the second half of season 1971/72.
