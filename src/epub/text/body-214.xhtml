At the Annual General Meeting the President, Mr. J. Andrews, recorded with regret the closing of the Cricket, Hockey and Soccer Sections, and noted that the Club name remained Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, thus leaving the possibility for further expansion in the future. In fact, to the end, there was no further expansion. As has been stated, the Calypso Diving Club did not become a section of Overseas although their representative sat on Overseas Committee and their members were Full Members of Overseas Club. With the closing of the sections it was accepted by members attending the A.G.M. that the Club should revert to its original form, i.e., no separate Cricket, Hockey and Soccer Sections, and that the President should thus chair all meetings. This was reverting Overseas to the form it took before the A.G.M. of the 20th September, 1973, when it was changed from a pure Rugby Club to a Rugby and Sports Club. This meant a change in the Club Rules. In making these changes it was also taken into consideration that the British Services would be leaving Malta at the end of March, 1979, and thus, for the first time since the Club was founded, the need for the Services to be worded into the rules no longer existed. It was decided to up-date the normal Full Membership qualifications, since the Service and UK Based Civilian qualifications were hardly valid any longer. The subscriptions were raised from £1M-00 to £1M-50.

I reproduce in full the Rules as formed at the A.G.M., 1978, and except for the fact that the word 'Malta' had to be taken out of the name of the Club, they were the Rules in use at the closure.
