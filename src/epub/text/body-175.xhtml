Jack Sharp

Our President, Trevor Ricketts, aptly described Jack Sharp as the father figure of the Club. How true. Jack has taken such an active part in running the Club for so long that it is difficult to imagine the Club being without his always available advice and 'know-how'. No one has done more for the Club than Jack. For the Club's sake, it is unfortunate that Jack is retiring in June this year, and although not certain of his movements, will perhaps find it impossible to take on a permanent office in what is surely the most difficult year in the Club's history. Having said this, all his many friends wish him and his wife Dorothy all happiness and good health in the future, and thank them both for what they have readily given to the Club over the years, and perhaps more so for personal friendship and kindness.

Although I have listed below many of the offices undertaken by Jack, it is impossible to name the many diverse ways he has helped the Club. It is impossible to overestimate his contact with the Maltese Ministry, particularly the Minister of Sport, on the Club's behalf, for the very many times he has acted as host to Overseas Committees, Members and other functions, his direct contact with the Colleges appertaining to rugby participation, and not least for providing all the necessities for Film Evenings at the Club House.
