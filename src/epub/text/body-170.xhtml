Throughout the season 8 talented cricketing sides toured Malta, for the most part old friends, starting with the Speedbirds C.C. in May. The other visitors were Mendip Acorns in June, followed closely by newcomers the Sheffield Cricket Lovers from Yorkshire. At the end of August came Old Vigornians from Worcester and in September the Nottingham Select and the Blighters. In October the season was brought to a close by the Mendip Acorns again, the Forty Club and Bill Frindall's XI. All the touring sides played Combined Service and/or Combined Civilian teams, and each touring side played different individual teams. For the Cricket Festival in October, we had no less than 3 Overseas players selected for the Malta squad, plus two other members who were playing under another flag. Overseas played against Nottingham Select on the 24th September, and we were the only side to register a win over them. We also played against Mendip Acorns on the 15th October, and in this game we were narrowly beaten. Both these touring sides contained county players.

Sat 24 Sep vs Nottingham Select                          Won Nottingham 237-7, Overseas 241-4: Davey 119

Sat 15 Oct vs Mendip Acorns                         Lost Overseas 194, Mendip Acorns 195-3

Overseas 2nd XI played 4 matches, their opponents being the Navy (twice), Malta Sports Club 2nd XI, and Tal Handaq.

The Malta Cricket League tour to Corfu, 22nd August to 2nd September, was a great success, and in spite of some very patriotic umpiring, the tourists managed to win 2 out of 5 games played. The touring party included 5 Overseas members, viz, Trevor Ricketts, Mike Newton, Dave Smith, Gerry Davey, and Brian Leonard. I am told that some of the tactics employed to ensure a home win were quite amusing though apt to spoil the game from a cricket point of view. However, the fact that the tour was enjoyable is borne out by the fact that the Overseas President, Trevor Ricketts, has threatened to retire in Corfu.

As in all sections of 'Seas' team spirit remained high throughout what was possibly the penultimate season for the Cricket Section, indeed, under the Captaincy and example of Gerry Davey, and the Vice-Captaincy of Dave Walker, the spirit and standard have never been higher. With all players available the Overseas XI of 1977 had a very powerful look.

Going back to 1973, the year Overseas became a Sports Club, in mentioning those members who have given a great deal to Overseas Cricket Section, on and off the field, to the fore would have to come Trevor Ricketts and Mike Newton. Trevor, as ever, the driving force behind Seas, and a prolific scorer of runs, was Chairman and Vice-Captain in 1973, and Captain for two years, 1974-75. Mike Newton, Secretary for 3 years, 1974-75 and 1977, Captain 1976 and Vice-Captain 1975. One member who has missed watching very few Overseas matches over many years is Jack Sharp. Jack, whose encouragement is always welcome, was elected on the Selection Committee for 1977.
