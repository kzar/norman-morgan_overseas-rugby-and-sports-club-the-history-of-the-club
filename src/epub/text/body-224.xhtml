In May, 1979, the Kuwait Rugby Team had contacted Mr. Charles Bezzina, the Minister of Sport, showing interest in a tour to Malta in November, 1979. With the visit of the Kuwait Club, Overseas hoped to encourage other teams to tour in Malta and so organize a Tournament. The Minister of Sport agreed to help, to provide free transport and accommodation at reduced prices, as long as Overseas saw to the overall organization. The President expressed his belief that, knowing the enthusiasm of Tim Agius-Ferrante, George Attard, David Schembri and Charles Borg, there would be good rugby during the 1979/80 season.

Early in the 1979/80 season Overseas players made use of the old R.A.F. gym at Luqa for indoor training. With permission from the Minister of Sport, Overseas again used the Marsa pitch. The rugby season started for Overseas on the 20th October with a game against the Armed Forces of Malta (No. 3 Battery) which Overseas won by 14 points to nil. Unfortunately, after this game, due to changes within the Force itself, the Army could not again field a team. Then the main blow fell. On the 25th October, 1979, torrential rain fell on Malta, flooding the Marsa pitch and covering it with four or five inches of mud. There was hope that with the use of a spiked roller the pitch could be saved, but even so, at the time it was not available for matches. The Marsa grounds were expected to be out of use for a number of months. Through the kindness of Colonel Spiteri, Commanding Officer of the Armed Forces of Malta, the Safi pitch was made available for Overseas games. Overseas then played two games against St. Edwards College, losing both, the first by 16 points to nil, and even having to borrow players from St. Edwards to field a team in the second. The final game played by an Overseas XV was over the Christmas (1979) period when they beat a Minestrone team by 30 points to 10.

The Kuwait Rugby Club was unable to tour Malta and so the November Tournament had to be cancelled. Of the other potential teams, De la Salle College did not play any game against Overseas, and there was no further word of Senglea forming a team. Early in the season, a serious injury to one of the De la Salle players caused the College to ban rugby. From this it can be seen that the main problem for Overseas was lack of opposition, and, as a consequence, diminishing interest among the players. It was also found that Safi was remote, there were transport difficulties, players failed to turn out, and matches had to be cancelled at short notice. One must also remember that, on top of the problems of fielding a team, the future of Overseas Club House, the focal point for rugby on the Island, hung in the balance.

The season ended on the 19th April, 1980, when a 7-a-side Tournament, won by St. Edwards College, was held at Safi. 5 teams competed, Overseas (2), St. Edwards (2), and the Minestrones.
