NOTICE INSERTED IN NEWSPAPER, 1954

The following notice was inserted in the Times of Malta on the 15th October, 1954:-

OVERSEAS R.F.C.

The Overseas Rugby Club in Malta exists mainly for the benefit of U.K. based Service and Civilian members of units, ships, etc. unable to form a XV of their own.

Matches are played weekly, Saturday or Sunday, usually at Ta' Qali.

Anyone interested is invited to contact Mr J.H. Chapman, Tel. No/ Dockyard 7276 for details, as it is hoped to start play in the very near future.

OBJECTS AND MEMBERSHIP, 1956

The following information was given to all members and prospective members at the beginning of the 1956/57 season:-

The object of the Club is to help foster the true 'Rugger' spirit on the island by providing facilities for:-

(a) All Service and Civilian Rugger players who are unable to play for any other Rugger team, i.e. Members of Units unable to field a side.

(b) All Service and Civilian Rugger players, who, although committed to play for a unit during the week, wish to play 'Club' rugger over the weekend.

(c) Non-playing rugger enthusiasts who may support the club and join in its social life.

It is appreciated of course, that in the case of (b) above, the Service units have first call on their players.

The Club colours are (a) navy blue and white hoops (b) red and white hoops.

Membership was divided into three categories:-

(a) Full Member

Annual subscription of 30/- : no match fees, This type of member whose subscriptions were fully paid on leaving the Club received an inscribed tankard as follows:-

1 season's membership    ½ pint tankard

2 or more season's membership 1 pint tankard

(b) Associate Member

No Annual subscription - match fee 1/- per game

(c) Non-playing Member

Annual subscription 10/-

Presenting a tankard seemed a novel way of getting members to pay subscriptions, although it would appear that the attraction was not big enough (or some members did not drink). There is a record of 8 members not paying subs for season 1955/56,
