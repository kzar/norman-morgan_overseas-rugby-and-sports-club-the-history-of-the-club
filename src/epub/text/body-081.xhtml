The 7-a-sides were a disgrace. Probably fielding the strongest seven in the competition on paper, both our sides were defeated in the first round. In the Plate the 'A' team could only attain runners up, while the 'B' team once again went out in the first round.

With regard to the players, probably our biggest headache was in replacing 'the Boot' Jackson who, through a knee injury, only made 9 appearances. However, we were fortunate in having a long haired, bearded, at that time, University lecturer who showed us that he could kick. He is, of course, Charlie Rice and he finished up the season with 121 points. Peter Miller was leading try scorer once again.

The pre season training at the Marsa and at Tal Handaq started off on the 17th September, 1968. The main feature of the season was the number of sides touring Malta, including our friends Tripoli (Libya) Nomads and John Evans XV. Investigations made into the possibility of Overseas touring Naples, Sicily or N. Africa came to nought. Unfortunately, we had to turn down an offer from Rugby Reggio to take part in a tournament because the dates were unsuitable for Overseas.

John Evans' party consisted of 19 officials and players and included 8 members who had toured Malta the previous year. Once again the army kindly accommodated them in Lintorn Barracks, Floriana, at a very moderate cost, and once again arrangements were made by Capt. Mike Adams. Their Manager was, of course, John Evans, ex Overseas player, with Assistant Manager, P.R.V. Sinden, ex Durham Medicals. The team was Captained by W.G.D. Morgan. Derek Morgan had led Monmouthshire to the final of the Welsh Counties Championship during the season just ending. Following is a list of the tourists as printed in the Malta News.
