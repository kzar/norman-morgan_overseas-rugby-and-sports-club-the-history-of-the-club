PLAYING SEASON

In spite of the political clouds on the horizon, the 1971/72 season started off normally. Training started on the 30th September with circuit training in the gymnasium at Tel Handaq School under the supervision of Trev Ricketts.

From the start there was a shortage of playing members, and although the Team Captain, Dave Taylor, believed that we had a reasonably competent side, there was, on the occasion, difficulty in raising a side. Lack of interest in training did not help. The two combined led to a number of defeats in the short lived season. It was not until the 18th December that the Captain said in Committee that "Last Saturday's win" had shown a strong change in fortunes. This must have referred to a game in which Overseas beat RAF 1st side 78 points to nil. Dave Taylor scored 4 tries. In all 14 tries were scored, 11 of which were converted by Charlie Rice. Unfortunately, there is not a complete list of games played and results. I believe approximately 6 games were played before the withdrawal.

The points value of a try was increased this season from 3 to 4. No rugby took place in Malta during the second half of the season. This put paid to the plans ahead for the Rugby Festival which was to have taken place during Easter period 1972.
