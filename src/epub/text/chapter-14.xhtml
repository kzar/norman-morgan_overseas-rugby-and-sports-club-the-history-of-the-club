At a meeting held on the 4th October, 1965, it had been noted that under the rules of the Club, the Vice-Presidents, as nominees and not elected members, were not entitled to attend meetings of the General Committee. This point was raised at the A.G.M. and Messrs. N. Johnson; G.G. Jackson; J.P. Ratcliffe; L. Duncan and J.D. Green were duly elected, all to remain as Vice-Presidents until such time as they retire from Office or left Malta.

The Rules of the Club were amended to include the following as Rule 7:

"That there be a Selection Committee of three the composition of which shall be decided at the Annual General Meeting and shall include the Captain, Vice-Captain and one other."

This meant that the Vice-Captain be elected at the A.G.M. The composition of the Selection Committee for 1965/66 was Messrs. J. Handford (Captain); P. Henshall (Vice-Captain); and P. Ratcliffe.

From 1965 to 1971, inclusive, details of Overseas R.U.F.C. were inserted in the Sport Section of The Malta Year Book. These details usually included the President's and Secretary's names and addresses, the fact we were affiliated to the R.F.U. and the previous season's playing record.

Playing Season

1965/66 was another exceptionally good season from the playing point of view. Overseas set off with a new set of jerseys and play to match. The 5 pitches at the Marsa were all available, i.e. Playable, from the 1st November, and the Joint Services (Malta) Rugby Committee agreed that they be allocated by the Fleet Recreation Officer. The first match for Overseas was on the 10th November versus H.M.S. Rhyl.

Once again the Team Captain’s end of season report has been lost. After spending a number of hours in the Royal Malta Library, Valletta, looking for information and results in copies of the Malta News and Times of Malta, I compiled an incomplete list of fixtures and results. Fortunately, Jack Handford had kept his old fixture card, complete with results, and this he has recently handed to me. Jack has also produced an interesting list of player’s individual appearances over four years. This list I will reproduce at the end of the 1965/ 66 'Playing Season' report.

As in the previous season, according to the press coverage in the Malta News by their Rugby Correspondent ‘Remig Sacco’, the outstanding player for Overseas was Brian Jackson (senior). I have no complete analysis of player’s averages, but I have been informed by a very reliable source, Jacko himself, that the total number of points scored by Jacko during 1965/66 was 143. As mentioned previously in an item about insurance, the Team Captain, Jack Handford, broke his leg in December, 1965, and whilst Jack was out of action the Team was Captained most capably by Peter Henshall.

An interesting table in the Malta News (9th December, 1965) gave a round up of Joint Services Inter Unit Rugby, ‘All played on a friendly basis’. The table includes all teams based in Malta during the early part of season 1965/66, indeed, only the Sussex Regt. was not a permanent part of Malta Rugby at that time. For the purpose of comparison 'Overseas', the only Civilian Rugby Club in Malta, was included as a Service Unit. It is a pity that a similar table was not included at the end of the season. We would have topped the table.

Highlights of the season, After 6 years Overseas managed to beat Forts (Fort St. Angelo), 6 points to nil, in the traditional Boxing Day match at the Marsa. For the first time Overseas were winners of the ‘Plate’ in the Inter Service 7’s (sometimes termed the Inter Unit 7’s) on the 19th February, 1966. Overseas 'B' to be exact. Overseas also received an invitation to enter the Navy 7’s although no record remains as to whether we entered same.

Two Touring sides visited Malta in the second half of the season. Overseas played a British Army Team from Benghazi, N. Africa, on the 30th January, 1966, losing 5 - 8. Benghazi Bedwins kept an unbeaten record during their tour. For the last game of the season, on the 12th March, 1966 Overseas played a touring side (R.A.F.) from Cyprus, losing 6 - 9.

One of the mainstays of the Overseas pack, Peter Cantello, left Malta in March, 1966. During his 2 years' service in Malta, Peter had played for the Royal Navy, captaining a winning side in 1964/65, also for Overseas and St. Angelo. Previously he had played for the R.N. (U.K.) and Hampshire County.

Adrian Wirth, one of the few Maltese players to have played for Overseas up to 1973, won the annual award of the Sam Ward Cup for the most improved player of season 1965/66. It was an exceedingly well earned award, both for the improvement in play and very courageous defence. He had absolutely no fear.

Social Events

The main recorded Social Events of the playing season were the monthly Film Evenings at Marsa Lodge. These functions proved popular and financially successful, once again thanks to Jack Sharp.

A Christmas Party for the children of Overseas Club Members was held on the 30th December, 1965, at Marsa Lodge. This was organised by Janet Daley, wife of the Social Secretary, and a Ladies Committee formed for the purpose. The Overseas Committee were of the opinion that the venture was an unqualified success. The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the United Services Sports Club, Marsa, on Friday, 25th March, 1966. V. Valente and his band attended. John Hitt (Hitty) was Master of Ceremonies. This was voted another memorable evening. There was a loss of £33. 22 guests were invited.

The Club House remained open during the close season under a Summer Committee.
