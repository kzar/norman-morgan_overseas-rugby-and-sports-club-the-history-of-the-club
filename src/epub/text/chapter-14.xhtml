In accordance with the fact that Overseas no longer made payments from the Bar profits to the three Services, Rule 9 was amended as follows, leaving out any reference to the Services and stating only the month in which the A.G.M. is to be held and the date of the financial year ending.

"Rule 9. The Annual General Meeting to be held in September and the financial year to end on the 31st August."

Playing Season

The playing season of 1968/69 was a success. The Team Captain, Roy Ravenscroft, left Malta before the end of the season and the report was given by the Vice-Captain, Jack Handford. This makes interesting reading and I quote.

"The number of points scored '477' is a Club record, but balanced against this the number of points scored against '242' is also a record." (This does not include the game against the Army (Cyprus) on the 19th January, 1969, which Overseas won 11 points to 6.)

The R.A.F. side, known as the Hawks, were once again our bogey team and they beat us on 3 occasions. Other defeats were recorded against John Evans XV and R.A.F. College Cranwell (two touring sides). H.M.S. Fearless, Army Malta and the two regiments, although we beat the latter in return matches later in the season. Most notable of our wins were those mentioned against the two regiments and R.A.F. Malta.

The 7-a-sides were a disgrace. Probably fielding the strongest seven in the competition on paper, both our sides were defeated in the first round. In the Plate the 'A' team could only attain runners up, while the 'B' team once again went out in the first round.

With regard to the players, probably our biggest headache was in replacing 'the Boot' Jackson who, through a knee injury, only made 9 appearances. However, we were fortunate in having a long haired, bearded, at that time, University lecturer who showed us that he could kick. He is, of course, Charlie Rice and he finished up the season with 121 points. Peter Miller was leading try scorer once again."

The pre season training at the Marsa and at Tal Handaq started off on the 17th September, 1968. The main feature of the season was the number of sides touring Malta, including our friends Tripoli (Libya) Nomads and John Evans XV. Investigations made into the possibility of Overseas touring Naples, Sicily or N. Africa came to nought. Unfortunately, we had to turn down an offer from Rugby Reggio to take part in a tournament because the dates were unsuitable for Overseas.

John Evans' party consisted of 19 officials and players and included 8 members who had toured Malta the previous year. Once again the army kindly accommodated them in Lintorn Barracks, Floriana, at a very moderate cost, and once again arrangements were made by Capt. Mike Adams. Their Manager was, of course, John Evans, ex Overseas player, with Assistant Manager, P. R. V. Sinden, ex Durham Medicals. The team was Captained by W. G. D. Morgan. Social Events 1968/69

With three Touring sides visiting Malta during the season there must have been a fair amount of social activity arranged. This is borne by the fact that Mr. P. Ratcliffe, President, when thanking Colin Brown, Social Secretary, and the Social Committee, for their efforts, remarked that it had been an excellent year. However, except for a Hot Pot Supper and a farewell Cocktail Party at Brian Jackson's residence, both organised for John Evans touring party, very little is recorded.

Once again, the film evenings were the mainstay, with all films and equipment provided by Jack Sharp. All Thursday Club Evenings were well attended.

The Annual Dinner Dance was held on the 29th March, 1969, at the United Services Sports Club, Marsa. This proved to be a most successful occasion. A loss of £4, claimed at the time to be the smallest loss on any Club Dinner Dance ever recorded, was a good effort as a total of £22 was spent on tickets for Official guests.

The Club House remained open during the close season.
