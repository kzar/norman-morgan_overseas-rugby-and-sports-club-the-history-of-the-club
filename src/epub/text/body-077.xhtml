Wales. John, an Ex Overseas player, brought an exceptionally talented party of players, including Derek Morgan (England and Barbarians). This was a memorable tour. They were billeted at 234 Signal Squadron Mess, Lintorn Barracks, Floriana, by kind permission of Major Brian Woods, at a very moderate cost to the tourists. Arrangements for accommodation and transport were made by Capt. Mike Adams, Secretary, Army (Malta) R.U. In two extremely hard fought games the visitors remained undefeated. Both games were dominated by the outstanding play and leadership of Derek Morgan, who at times seemed to dictate events almost at will. The outstanding player for Overseas was 'Taffy' Grey, forward, who scored two tries. It was a fitting finale to an enjoyable season.

Arrangements were made by Mr. R. Squires, with the kind permission of Surgeon Captain McLintock, R.N., Royal Naval Hospital, Bighi, for members of the touring teams in Malta over the Easter period to be treated as out-patients and hospitalized in the R.N. Hospital. This service was free under a 'Grace and Favour' scheme.

And so ended perhaps the most memorable playing season of all. Amongst the memories of the normal Saturday and Sunday games, one thinks of two very pretty girls who took the oranges on at half time, Judith Cross and Maureen Jones, they too were part of this season. Also Arthur Cross who took his duties as Trainer on the afternoon of a game very seriously. The Cup for the 'Most Improved Player' was not presented for the 1967/68 season.
