JACK HANDFORD

In November, 1975, Mr. Jack Handford, a Vice-President and one of the Club's longest serving Members, left Malta. Jack left quietly and no official farewell was given. When the General Committee was informed Jack was already away. The omission was partly remedied by the President, Mr. D.J. Taylor, sending a letter from the Committee and a gift from the Club of an engraved paper knife. Jack's club record is quite impressive. He joined the Club in 1962 and when he left in 1975 he could claim the club record of being the longest continuously serving Committeeman, 1963 to 1975. He was a playing member of the Rugby Section for 10 years, from 1962 to 1972, during which time he played 185 games for the Club out of a possible total of 225. This is allowing for the fact that he broke his leg in December, 1965, and could play only 8 games out of 21 in that year, also for the fact that I cannot confirm the number of games he played in 1966/67 and 1971/72. List of games played is produced below. Jack was a hard uncompromising player. He was Team Captain for 3 years, 1963-1966, Vice-Captain for 3 years, 1966-1969, and finally taking over as Vice-Captain for a 4th year when Eddy McClure was injured in November/December of the 1969/70 season. He was a Vice-President for 7 years, 1969-1976. Jack took part as a player (vice-Captain) in the Rugby tour to Kessia in January, 1968, and was in Committee during the temporary disbandment and reformation of the Club in 1972.

Jack also played for the Cricket Section from its inception in 1965 to approximately 1972.
