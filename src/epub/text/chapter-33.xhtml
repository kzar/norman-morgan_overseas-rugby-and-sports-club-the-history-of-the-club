1979/80

Annual General Meeting, Club House, 21 Flagstone Wharf, Marsa, 27th September, 1979.

Present 17 Members.

The A.G.M. opened with Caretaker President, Mr. C. Fox, acting on behalf of the President, Mr. J. Andrews, absent in U.K.

At this A.G.M. it was decided that the privilege of being President should be extended to one of our Maltese Members who has given service to the Club, and that as far as possible the activities of the Club should be run by the young Maltese Members.

Rugby Section 1979/80

Playing Season 1979/80

The rugby season of 1979/80 in fact turned out to be the last season in which an Overseas XV took to the field. Inevitably it proved, after the British Forces left Malta, in spite of valiant efforts to keep the game going, there were too many insurmountable problems for rugby to continue in Malta. The first crack appeared in Committee on the 31st January, 1980, and so, first, I will complete the history to this point. From the 31st January decisions made in Committee gradually planned the Club's Winding-up and then Closure, and in spite of these decisions, a rearguard action was fought by the few rugby enthusiasts to save the game in Malta.

In April, 1979, after the British Forces pulled out, Overseas Secretary, George Attard, reported that within the Club there was a strong determination to carry on, although the immediate problem was the almost complete lack of referees. This problem was fortunately remedied. In May, 1979, the President, Mr. J. Andrews, said it was very difficult to put one completely in the picture as circumstances were changing so quickly. One minute one felt pessimistic about the future of rugby in Malta, and the next, one could be very optimistic. In June, 1979, George Attard again reported that the members were looking to the future.

The reason for this optimism was the belief that a number of teams would be formed to provide opposition. There was the strong possibility that the Armed Forces of Malta would form one or two teams under the guidance of Marcel Cassar, the Army Representative. St. Edwards College and De La Salle College were keen to provide opposition. It was even mentioned that the little town of Senglea would form a team. In May, 1979, the Kuwait Rugby Team had contacted Mr. Charles Bezzina, the Minister of Sport, showing interest in a tour to Malta in November, 1979. With the visit of the Kuwait Club, Overseas hoped to encourage other teams to tour in Malta and so organize a Tournament. The Minister of Sport agreed to help, to provide free transport and accommodation at reduced prices, as long as Overseas saw to the overall organization. The President expressed his belief that, knowing the enthusiasm of Tim Agius-Ferrante, George Attard, David Schembri and Charles Borda, there would be good rugby during the 1979/80 season.

Early in the 1979/80 season Overseas players made use of the old R.A.F. gym at Luqa for indoor training. With permission from the Minister of Sport, Overseas again used the Marsa pitch. The rugby season started for Overseas on the 20th October with a game against the Armed Forces of Malta (No. 3 Battery) which Overseas won by 14 points to nil. Unfortunately, after this game, due to changes within the Force itself, the Army could not again field a team. Then the main blow fell. On the 25th October, 1979, torrential rain fell on Malta, flooding the Marsa pitch and covering it with four or five inches of mud. There was hope that with the use of a spiked roller the pitch could be saved, but even so, at the time it was not available for matches. The Marsa grounds were expected to be out of use for a number of months. Through the kindness of Colonel Spiteri, Commanding Officer of the Armed Forces of Malta, the Safi pitch was made available for Overseas games. Overseas then played two games against St. Edwards College, losing both, the first by 16 points to nil, and even having to borrow players from St. Edwards to field a team in the second. The final game played by an Overseas XV was over the Christmas (1979) period when they beat a Minestrone team by 30 points to 10.

The Kuwait Rugby Club was unable to tour Malta and so the November Tournament had to be cancelled. Of the other potential teams, De la Salle College did not play any game against Overseas, and there was no further word of Senglea forming a team. Early in the season, a serious injury to one of the De la Salle players caused the College to ban rugby. From this it can be seen that the main problem for Overseas was lack of opposition, and, as a consequence, diminishing interest among the players. It was also found that Safi was remote, there were transport difficulties, players failed to turn out, and matches had to be cancelled at short notice. One must also remember that, on top of the problems of fielding a team, the future of Overseas Club House, the focal point for rugby on the Island, hung in the balance.

The season ended on the 19th April, 1980, when a 7-a-side Tournament, won by St. Edwards College, was held at Safi. 5 teams competed, Overseas (2), St. Edwards (2), and the Minestrones.
