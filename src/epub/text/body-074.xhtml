'One of the most memorable moments of the tour was undoubtedly our first glimpse of the 'pitch', as it came into view between the cranes and piles of rusting scrap metal which littered the Messina dockside. No-one could believe it! It was hope- fully stated that that must be their practice ground, until the coach pulled up outside dressing rooms which made the Marsa seem palatial and far- distant. We have been warned that the pitch was not in very good condition owing to the heavy rain, but the mud bath we had expected turned out to be a stretch of gravel half covered by large areas of standing water. Grey skies and persistent rain did not help to improve the picture. In fairness it should be stated, however, that Messina does have an adequate stadium, which unfortunately, and to our hosts' dismay, was unplayable because of the bad weather.'

We were beaten, just, in both games played. We had the obvious handicaps, such as the inability of some of the regular Overseas 1st team players to tour, the pitch, and perhaps, above all, the difference in interpretation of the rules by the Sicilians and ourselves. On the other hand we were up against a good team, continental style, with their forwards as ready to run with the ball as their threequarters. Throughout the first game it rained incessantly with an icy wind blowing straight down from the mountains. For the second game the weather was a little kinder, but still wet and windy with water lying over large areas of the gravel pitch. All Overseas team played well, with P. Parker; P. Miller; J. Handford; T. Ricketts and C. Rice being mentioned in the Captain's dispatches. Peter Parker received well earned praise in the local Messina press.

During both games, the Sicilian players arrived on and left the pitch with such startling rapidity that it seemed scarcely possible that they had only two substitutes. It is customary in Italy to allow two substitutes. On one occasion, during the first game whilst a line out was taking place, there were 19 Sicilians on the field, including the Referee and Trainer. Overseas players were tickled pink, they could scarcely believe it. Two games full of excitement.

Wednesday, 3rd January, 1968

CUS, Messina 13 (2 goals, 1 penalty)
Overseas R.U.F.C. 6 (tries: P. Parker, T. Nicholson)

Friday, 5th January, 1968

CUS, Messina 9 (1 try, 2 penalties)
Overseas R.U.F.C. 8 (tries: R. Squires, I. Dewar,
1 conversion by B. Jackson)

In the first game Brian Jackson badly grazed his rear quarters by sliding on the treacherous surface, very painful and very embarrassing. In spite of being in considerable pain Brian played in the second game, much to the relief of the team and selectors. Even with Brian we had only 17 players.
