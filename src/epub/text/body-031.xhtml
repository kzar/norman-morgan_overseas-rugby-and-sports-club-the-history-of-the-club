Rent of the room was £10 per month. It was decided to open the Club House on Monday, Saturday and Sunday evenings at 7.30 p.m. and on any day after an Overseas match at 5.30 p.m. The Sunday evening opening was soon dropped.

The Club House was closed for the close season as from the 2nd March, 1959, when a final get together was held. All stock was sold at a very much reduced price on this night, a good time was had by all. One or two privates were calling their Commanding Officer by Christian name, and one Captain I know drove three soldiers home like long lost buddies. I do not know what happened next day.

The Club House at Gerry's Bar was a partial success. It was too far away from the grounds for many players to make use of it after a game and it could have been used more when open of an evening (the old cry). From the income point of view the rent was too high and there was little or no profit from the bar during the first season. However, the Committee agreed that a Club House was essential, and that it had contributed a part in reforming and keeping the Club together. The Bar Manager, Bill Polkinghorne, and the Committee, men and ladies, had worked hard, putting in many hours in an attempt to make the Club House popular.

At a pre season meeting held on the 6th October, 1959, the Committee were of the opinion that Gerry's Bar was suitable for a Club House. Unfortunately, continued poor usage of the Club House by members throughout the 1959/60 season suggested otherwise. In spite of lack of bar business the Club House managed a profit of £3.14s, thanks to the Bar Manager, Geoff Rushworth, and the Bar Committee, Messrs T. Goswell, V. Morris and A. Pagan.

AFFILIATION TO THE RUGBY FOOTBALL UNION

On the 16th January, 1959, the first approach to the Rugby Football Union was made requesting affiliation. Mr. F.D. Prentice, then Secretary of the R.F.U., replied to our letter on the 30th January, 1959. Mr Prentice commented on the fact that we were the only Civilian Club in Malta and presumed, correctly (except for touring sides), that our matches were against Service teams. He suggested that when there were one or two Clubs in Malta playing Rugby Union Football, the Union of these Clubs should be called the "Malta Rugby Union" and then it would be possible for his Committee to consider affiliation.

Unless no record has been kept of any other attempt, the next approach to the R.F.U. for affiliation was on the 28th December, 1961, by the Overseas Secretary, Mr. C.V. Morris. No copy has been kept of this letter. In his reply of the 13th December, 1961, Mr Prentice thanked Mr. Morris for his letter and the Fixture Card which he had enclosed. Mr. Prentice regretted that there was little likelihood of there being another Rugby Club in Malta but nevertheless enclosed a nomination form for Membership to the Rugby Football Union. Mr. Prentice ended his letter by saying that our application would then be placed before the Committee of the R.F.U. at a later date for election to membership for season 1962/63.
