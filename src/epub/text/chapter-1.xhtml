Foreword by the President

It is my pleasure in this, my second year as President of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, to write a few words at the beginning of what I think will prove an extremely interesting and factual account of the life of a Club of which we are all very fond. Many of you who have been as fortunate as I in enjoying the fellowship and companionship which have always been the mark of the Overseas tradition, will realise how sad I feel that Overseas is now drawing to a close. Over the years, I have made many friends and acquaintances through the rugby, soccer, hockey and cricket sections, as well as amongst the many social members who have played a distinct role in supporting our club.

Not least of these is my good friend, Norman Morgan. Norman was a member of the Club first of all in 1948 when he completed his first tour here in Malta. He was a rugby-playing member until 1950. He then left the island, only to return in 1956, when he became an even more active member of a Club which became his first love. In 1958, during what was to be a reformation of the Club, he undertook no less than three tasks, those of Honorary Secretary, Team Secretary and Membership Secretary. On leaving the island in 1959, he thought his association with Overseas might be at a close, but this was not to be for in 1965 he returned and immediately became a member of the Bar Management Committee. In 1966, he was made assistant Secretary and the following year, became the Club's extremely active Honorary Secretary. It was during this year that he completely organized a magnificent rugby tour to Sicily. This will long be remembered by those who participated.

On 11th September, 1969, Norman Morgan was awarded what was for him the ultimate honour by the Club - that of Honorary Life Member. Norman again left the island, and, following the withdrawal of the British Services from Malta in January, 1972, thought that this was the end of the Overseas. However, on his return to the island for yet another tour in 1973, he was pleased to see the Club functioning as strongly as ever. He again took up the gavel and became Secretary of Rugby and Cricket Sections, as well as becoming a Vice President and Membership Secretary.

It was during this time that he decided to write this History of the Club and after 5 years of heartache, toil, thousands of letters and phone calls, this wonderful document is the end result. It is a fitting tribute to Norman himself who, along with his and our dear friend, Jack Sharp, must together share the title of 'Mr. Overseas'. Our heartfelt thanks, Norman, for your hard work and efforts on our behalf.

T. N. RICKETTS.

Preface

The Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, Malta, was formed in November, 1946. For 27 years 'Overseas' was purely a rugby club and known as the Overseas Rugby Club or Overseas R.U.F.C. It was not until 1973 that 'Overseas' became a 'Rugby and Sports Club'. Readers must keep this in mind. This history deals almost exclusively with rugby matters up to 1973 and therefore the Club will be referred to by its former name to that point.

A further point to keep in mind is that no record remains of any minutes kept until 1954. I have been informed by Secretaries of the Club prior to 1954 that records were in fact kept and passed on to their successors. It will remain a mystery. Minutes remain of one meeting held on the 27th November, 1954, and then another blank until minutes were kept of a meeting of the 6th September, 1958. Minutes have been kept, and remained in safe keeping of the Club, continuously since September, 1958. No copies of correspondence were retained until 1956. During these early years I was a member of Overseas from November, 1948, to March, 1950, and again from November, 1956, to July, 1959. This still left gaps in knowledge of the Club and even as I started to write this history, I searched and corresponded with former members, including two who were founder members in 1946. Even so, there are still periods when information on the Club is slight, and it is to be hoped that if any former members reading this history can fill in any blanks, he (or she) will contact me or the Overseas Club.

Events in this history will be written in chronological order within the year they happen. On the other hand, any subject considered to be of sufficient importance, and which covers a number of years, I will complete that part of the history whilst writing about the year in which it was initiated. Monetary values will be written in sterling, Lsd or decimal, or Maltese currency, according to the system in use during the period being written about. Malta retained our (U.K.) pre-decimal sterling coins up to the 16th May, 1972, when they changed to their own decimal coins, i.e. 100 cents to the Maltese pound. I have attempted to compile a year by year list of games and results, also to make a chart of all Officials and certain details of the Club from its inception. These are not complete, especially for the years before 1958/59. I have also collected team photos of Overseas XVs and I believe, from correspondence with ex members in an attempt to trace back copies, that I have a copy of a team photo for every year in which one was taken.

Throughout this history the 'Services' are continuously mentioned and unless specifically stated otherwise, this refers to the British Armed Forces. This is obvious to anyone who has lived in Malta, but perhaps not otherwise.
