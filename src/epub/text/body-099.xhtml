From the outset, the building and all items within were insured by Andrews Feeds. In April, 1974, an incident in which water was left running within the Club, and which seeped through into the warehouse below, involved insurance claims which Andrews Feeds dealt with. After this incident, Mr. D. Taylor, Overseas Treasurer, inquired into the necessity of Overseas taking out a separate insurance, only to be told that a blanket insurance was held by Andrews Feeds Malta Ltd.

When Overseas R.U.F.C. became Overseas Rugby and Sports Club in September, 1973, Mr. Armstrong believed we should look into the possibility of purchasing our own premises. Because of the political situation the Committee were against purchasing a property, and in November, 1973, Mr. Armstrong was asked to approach his Company with the view to a long-term rental, depending on Police permission. Fortunately, or unfortunately, the Directors of the Company were against this, but, they had no objection to some form of redecoration under Mr. Armstrong's control. However, before this, was carried out another problem arose. Mr. Armstrong reported that there was a possibility of the area in front of the Club House being turned into a dry-dock with consequential difficulties in approaching the Club House entrance. Once again Mr. Sharp came to the rescue. In an interview with Mr. I. Sant, Minister of Public Buildings & Works, Mr. Sharp was informed that the dry-docks were only in the planning stage and that it would be some time before the scheme affected us. With this assurance, the work went ahead. On the 22nd November, 1973, a Sub-Committee was formed, comprising of Mr. C. C. Fox (Chairman); Mr Armstrong and Mr R. Ward, to deal with redecoration. Mr. Ward, Hockey Section Captain, advised on possible designs. On the 22nd February 1974, with the expert advice of the firm's architect workmen from Andrews Feeds and the R.U.S.C. widened the opening between the two rooms by means of an archway. A number of Club members volunteered their assistance. The alteration and redecoration were completed by the 31st March, 1974.

During December, 1974, the Club House was again re-decorated, this time under a Sub-Committee comprising of Messrs. A. Armstrong, J. Masuero, H. Crossfield and B. Jones. The General Committee authorized that £50 be spent on soft furnishings and decoration. In January, 1975, curtains, tables and chairs were purchased, and at long last the Club House was complete. Again a number of members volunteered their assistance.

Niall Armstrong left Malta in October, 1975. It is thanks to Niall's unceasing effort that the Club, in the first place, received permission from the Directors of Andrews Feeds to use the premises, and later received permission to alter them structurally. It is also thanks to Niall and Jack Sharp for overcoming the problems of obtaining the Police Licence and carrying out alterations.

Finally, at the beginning of the 1975/76 season, the Bar/ Club House Manager, Mr. H. Crossfield, and the Social Secretary, Mr. J. Hughes with the help of other members, gave the Club House another face lift.
