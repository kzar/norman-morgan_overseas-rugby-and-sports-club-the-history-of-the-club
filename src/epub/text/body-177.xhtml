The start of the 1977/78 season, a season which was generally believed to be the last, started in a most unusual way. The Minister of Sport, Mr. L. Sant, in order to promote rugby in Malta, invited a team of ex Italian internationals, S.S. Vecchia Roma, to play games in Malta against the Combined Services, on the 23rd September, and a Maltese Select, on the 25th September. Normally, no rugby is played in Malta until the ground is softened by rain, later in the year as that might be, and the pitches at this time were like concrete. However, two weeks of watering made the pitches playable, although still described as hazardous.

The Maltese Select/S.S. Vecchia Roma game was sponsored by the Minister of Sport, and he invited a Spanish Coach, Senor Gonzales, now resident in Italy, to prepare the Maltese Select players for the great day. The Maltese Select XV, formed by the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, consisting of as many Maltese players as possible, lost a hard but very enjoyable game 18 points to 10. Mr. Sant was present during the whole of this game and was presented to both teams before the kick off. After the game we were honoured by Mr. S. Inglott from the Ministry of Sport visiting Overseas Club House. On the 23rd September, the Combined Services beat S.S. Vecchia Roma by 20 points to 19.

In June, 1977, Overseas sent a letter to the Minister of Sport giving him a brief history of rugby in Malta and its future prospects. In acknowledging this letter the Minister suggested that a meeting be arranged. A similar letter was sent by the President to the Chairman of the Joint Services Sports Board, Colonel Seabrook. In his reply, the Colonel said he recognised the valuable work done by the Club in promoting rugby in Malta. The meeting took place in September, when a representative of the Ministry of Sport met representatives from Overseas and other rugby bodies interested in the game in Malta. Those present included Jack Sharp, James Andrews and Ewan Macpherson, of Overseas Club, and Paul Miller, in charge of R.A.F. rugby, also.
