FORWARD BY THE PRESIDENT

It is my pleasure in this, my second year as President of the Overseas Rugby and Sports Club, to write a few words at the beginning of what I think will prove an extremely interesting and factual account of the life of a Club of which we are all very fond. Many of you who have been as fortunate as I in enjoying the fellowship and companionship which have always been the mark of the Overseas tradition, will realise how sad I feel that Overseas is now drawing to a close. Over the years, I have made many friends and acquaintances through the rugby, soccer, hockey and cricket sections, as well as amongst the many social members who have played a distinct role in supporting our club.

Not least of these is my good friend, Norman Morgan. Norman was a member of the Club first of all in 1948 when he completed his first tour here in Malta. He was a rugby-playing member until 1950. He then left the island, only to return in 1956, when he became an even more active member of a Club which became his first love. In 1958, during what was to be a reformation of the Club, he undertook no less than three tasks, those of Honorary Secretary, Team Secretary and Membership Secretary. On leaving the island in 1959, he thought his association with Overseas might be at a close, but this was not to be for in 1965 he returned and immediately became a member of the Bar Management Committee. In 1966, he was made assistant Secretary and the following year, became the Club's extremely active Honorary Secretary. It was during this year that he completely organized a magnificent rugby tour to Sicily. This will long be remembered by those who participated.

On 11th September, 1969, Norman Morgan was awarded what was for him the ultimate honour by the Club - that of Honorary Life Member. Norman again left the island, and, following the withdrawal of the British Services from Malta in January, 1972, thought that this was the end of the Overseas. However, on his return to the island for yet another tour in 1973, he was pleased to see the Club functioning as strongly as ever. He again took up the gavel and became Secretary of Rugby and Cricket Sections, as well as becoming a Vice President and Membership Secretary.

It was during this time that he decided to write this History of the Club and after 5 years of heartache, toil, thousands of letters and phone calls, this wonderful document is the end result. It is a fitting tribute to Norman himself who, along with his and our dear friend, Jack Sharp, must together share the title of 'Mr. Overseas'. Our heartfelt thanks, Norman, for your hard work and efforts on our behalf.

T. N. RICKETTS.
