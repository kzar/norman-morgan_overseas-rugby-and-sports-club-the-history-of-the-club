SOCIAL EVENTS 1973/74

The amalgamation of the four Sections injected new life into the social activities of the Club. Not only were functions organized by the Hon. General Social Secretary, Mr. P. Joerning, but also additional functions were organized by each of the Section Hon. Social Secretaries.

The Film Evenings at the Club House continued as the main social events and were well supported. From February these evenings were arranged on the 2nd and 4th Thursdays of each month. Once again we must thank Jack Sharp for the supply of films and for arranging for Mr. Maurice Baldacchino to operate the equipment. Also, once it became accepted that there was a clash of interests between the Colts and some of the Senior Members, separate films and functions were arranged for the Junior Section.

An attempt was made to enter an Overseas team in the Bowling League. A dozen members turned out for a trial get together at the Malta Bowling Club on the 9th March. Further practices were arranged with the view of finding out if there was enough interest. A good time was had by all, but the idea came to nought.

As has already been mentioned in the Rugby Section report of 1973/74, we had been asked by the J.S.R.C. to host the touring team, Hirwaun R.F.C. Unfortunately, the Club made extensive preparations for their visit, including a disco at Marsa Lodge on the 24th March, before learning of the cancellation of this tour. This left a void when no social functions were held.

The Annual Dinner Dance was held at the Preluna Hotel Skyroom on the 30th March, 1974, and this event proved a tremendous success, with 120 paying guests attending. As Mr. Joerning said in his end of season report, it was an ideal occasion to honour the 14 members, of all sections, who were leaving the Island. Besides the official guests, Mr. & Mrs. Colin Cauchie, the ladies of the 5 Colts who had played for the 1st team attended. The young ladies were the personal guests of Mr. J. Sharp, Chairman of the Rugby Section. Commenting in Committee, Mr. Sharp said that this dance was the best he had attended in his 15 years association with the Club. All credit was due to Peter Joerning for the organization and success of the dance.

Dinner Dances were also organized by the Football Section at the Crystal Hotel, Bugibba, on the 19th January, and by the Cricket Section at the Marsa Sports Club, on the 27th April. Both dances were a great success and most enjoyable.

A special farewell dinner was held at the Lancer Hotel, Salina, in honour of Charlie and Jacky Rice when they left Malta in June. Charlie had been a popular member of the Club for 7 years and could only be described as a great character. Besides being a star on the Rugby and Football field, Charlie had the odd game of Cricket & Hockey for Overseas.

For the final event of the season Peter Joerning organized a barbecue during the summer at Armier. Peter deserves a great deal of credit for the tireless effort, always with a touch of quiet humour, he put into arranging social activities during the season. In his end of season report the President said 'His [Peter's] organization this year was most efficient as evidenced by the very successful dinner dance the Club held at the Preluna Hotel'.
