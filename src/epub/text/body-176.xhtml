Trevor Ricketts

On a previous page, when I wrote about Trevor being elected an Hon. Life Member, I gave details of all the sterling work he had done, and has continued to do, for Overseas and sport in general in Malta. I reiterate what I said on that occasion. Trevor has given a certain vitality and efficiency to Committee work in his 7 years as Hon. Secretary/Hon. General Secretary, and I believe this has been mirrored in the Committee and the regard in which the Club is held.

Trevor was elected President at the 1976/77 Annual General Meeting, and he celebrated his first year in office by, once again, playing for all sections of the Club. Trevor's term as President has coincided with the run-down of the Club, and this is doubly sad for a person who has done so much to keep the name of Overseas in such high regard in the sporting world on the Island. It is also unfortunate for Overseas that Trevor leaves Malta in July, 1978, but decisions taken before that date will decide the future of the Club, and under his Chairmanship I believe the right decisions will be made.

Francis Chapelle

Francis earns a special mention as the longest continuously serving member of Overseas, a record of 19 years. Joining the Club in 1959, he was an active playing member until 1964, being elected Team Captain in 1960/61, and Vice-Captain in 1961/62. Since 1964, although not always an active member, Francis has remained one of the Club's keenest supporters.
