Social Functions 1976/77

As with all sections of the Club, the Social Life suffered with the gradual run-down of membership, and the Social Secretary Mr. John Hughes, did not find it easy to keep things going. Nevertheless, the season proved to be very full, and very successful. Club nights, especially on film nights, were well supported by members. The films, again provided by Jack Sharp were shown fortnightly throughout the winter months, increasing to one per week during March (1977) in order to raise funds for the proposed rugby tour to Messina. The Social Secretary attempted, to no avail, to get members to accept invitations to other clubs. Functions held during the year included a Bonfire Party & Disco on the 14th November, 1976 and a Children's Christmas Party on the 21st December, 1976. Both were held at the Club House and were very successful. The Children's Party was run efficiently by Mike Newton and Alan Latham, with Bill Wood taking on the vital part of Santa Claus.

The Social life was livened by three successful and enjoyable Dinner Dances and also the main Club function of the year, the Annual Dinner Dance. The Cricket Section dance on the 13th November, 1976, and the Soccer Section dance organised by Tom Jordan, Soccer Section Social Secretary, on the 11th December, 1976, were both held at the Crystal Hotel, Bugibba, and the Rugby Section dance on the 1st March, 1977 was held at the Whisper Knight Club, Mosta. The Rugby Section event ended with an impromptu cabaret consisting of Trevor Ricketts and Bill Wood singing and Alex Darminin playing the piano, followed by that nightmare of all Club proprietors where rugby followers are concerned, the passing around of a rugby ball, produced by Team Captain, Brian Charnley in preparation for the week-end game. The Club Annual Dinner Dance was held at the Malta Sports Club on Saturday, 30th April, 1977, and was a great success enjoyed by 105 members and guests. The venue for the Annual Dinner Dance was chosen for sentimental reasons, as well as the fact that functions held there have always been a success. It was felt by the President and Committee that it would be nice to return to the place which had been our home for such social occasions for many years, in view of the very depressing fact that this could be the last to be held by the Club. The season ended with the usual round of parties given by or for the members leaving and this included Overseas Farewell 'Do' for John Hughes, also for Brian Charnley, Team Captain, on Thursday, 30th June, 1977. John left Malta on the 23rd July, and this was the Club's loss. Not only had he been Social Secretary of the Main Club for the 2 years, 1975-77, but also Social Secretary of the Rugby and Cricket Sections during the same period, plus Social Secretary for the Hockey Section in 1976/77, and he gave a great deal of his time and energy to ensure that all the functions he arranged were a success, socially and financially. John also enjoyed his game of rugby. Brian has been mentioned in the Rugby Section 1976/77. Although, as Social Secretary, John Hughes was the front man, a great deal of credit for a successful season must be given to the Bar Manager, Martin Crossfield, also to James Andrews, Hon. General Treasurer, and who, as General Manager of Andrews Feeds, looked after the Club House premises on behalf of the firm, and did most of the buying and delivering of stock.
