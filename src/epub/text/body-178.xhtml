Prior to the start of the season, Mr. J. Sharp, Rugby Section Chairman, applied for and obtained permission from the Minister of Sport for Overseas to use the rugby pitch at the Mtarfa on Wednesday, Saturday and Sunday afternoons. Through the good offices of the Naval Sports Officer, Lt. Walker, the R.N. Representative on the Rugby Section Committee, C.P.O. M. Kirk, looked after the marking on the pitch.

The season started in earnest for Overseas on the 15th October with a game against H.M.S. Fearless, and even then the ground was described as being extremely hard. As in the previous season, the only teams to provide regular 1st team opposition were the R.A.F. and 41 Commando (Army), but perhaps the most noticeable difference was the lowering of the playing standards through lack of playing strength. This was particularly so with 41 Commando, no longer the dominant side of old. ‘B’ team opposition came mainly from the R.A.F. ‘B’ side, with single appearances by the Army ‘B’, St. Edward's College, De La Salle College, and a Services ‘B’ side. For Overseas it was not a great season resultwise, again the problem was lack of players who would commit themselves to playing regularly, although on the days when we had our best side out we looked as good as any team on the Island. It proved to be another frustrating season for Overseas as we fast became deplete of players as members left the Island with no replacements filling vacancies.

What I have written in the previous paragraph is consistent with all problems created by the run-down and imminent withdrawal of the British Forces from Malta, in the sphere of rugby during this season. That is one side of the coin. The other speaks of the hard work, and the constant living of rugby, by Jack Sharp (Chairman), James Andrews (Captain) Ewan Macpherson (Secretary and Vice-Captain), Marcel Cassar (B Team Captain) and David Scherini, in not only fielding a team throughout the season, but also in building a side for the future. The season started with the possibility that 1977/78 was to be the final rugby season for Overseas, and it ended with a determination, that in spite of the withdrawal, there was a future for Overseas, at least rugbywise, and for continued rugby in Malta. The main reasons for this optimism were (A) At a time when U.K. membership was decreasing there was a welcome increase in the number of Maltese players of reasonable standard in the Overseas teams. (B) The expressed wish of a number of the Maltese members that the Club and the Rugby Section should carry on in 1978/79. (C) The interest the Minister of Sport, Mr. L. Sant, had shown in promoting senior and junior rugby in Malta. I have spoken in this paragraph only of the work done by the Rugby Section Committee members, but all credit is due to our President, and the General Committee, where Section work is discussed, for the work done to keep the Overseas flag flying rugbywise.

In spite of the interest shown by the Minister of Sport, and the repeated offer by Overseas of coaching assistance, the interest shown by the Maltese Colleges during 1977/78 was disappointing, as only St. Edward's College fielded a team on a regular basis. The University had internal problems, although individual players still retained an interest in the game.
