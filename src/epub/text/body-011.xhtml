Mr. F. Hitchcock, Chairman, was one of the mainstays of Overseas from its inception to the time he left Malta in June, 1952. In fact, it has been generally believed over the years that he was the Founder Member. Almost true, but not quite. When starting research into the Club's beginning, I myself believed Mr. Hitchcock to be the initiator of the Club and attempted to trace him. This was to lead to a dead end as far as the Club's history was concerned, but it resulted in gathering very interesting information as regards rugby in Malta pre-war. Fred Hitchcock had been a U.K. Based Industrial (Foreman Electrician) in H.M. Dockyard, Malta, and armed with initial information from Montgomery House, Floriana, Malta, I wrote to Defence Accounts Office, Worcester, England. The DAO informed me that Fred had died in August, 1958. However, from two other sources I was informed of his possible connection with the pre- war "Exiles" and "E.R.A." Club(s), and the possible link between these club(s) and Overseas. When referred to appertaining to the pre-war period, the two clubs "Exiles" and "E.R.A.", always seemed tied together as one club. Further enquiries proved that no connection existed between Overseas and the Exiles/E.R.A.s, But I believe readers will be interested in the information gathered.

First, Commander E. Hamilton-Hill R.N. (Retd) informed me that the formation of Overseas Rugby Club stemmed from the run- down of the Exiles (Cable & Wireless) and the E.R.A. Club which used to run its own rugger side. At the same time the Commander stressed that his was a hazy recollection. Lt. - Cmdr R. Meredith informed me that Mr. Hitchcock was the oldest member at the meeting (November, 1946) and was a former playing member of the pre-war Exiles and E.R.A. Rugby Clubs. In further correspondence Lt. Cmdr Meredith shed more light on who the Exiles and E.R.A.s were by saying, "Mr. Hitchcock played for the 'Exiles' E.R.A.'s in Dockyard Appointments and that Cable & Wireless were the mainstay of the Club, together with Naval Officers on posts like the R.N. School and the Dockyard (Technical College)." I do know that the E.R.A. Rugby Club existed in Malta, as a separate Club, at least from after the war up to the 1954/55 season. Overseas played the E.R.A. club twice during the 1954/55 season, after which there is no further record of the E.R.A.s as rugby opponents.

In following up the "Exiles" club, I first contacted Officials at what was formerly Cable & Wireless, Mercury House, St. George's Road, St. Julians, Malta. Cable & Wireless in Malta became Telecommunication Corporation early in 1975. Here I was given the greatest help by Messrs. G.A. Cassar Trigona, M.Bonnici, A. Tortell and a former member of Cable & Wireless Staff, Mr R. Scerri. Between them they gave me the backgrounds to the Exiles Club, Malta. Mr Scerri, an all round sportsman & a younger man and Malta tennis champion for many years, remembered Cable & Wireless "Exiles" rugby team. He advised me to write to the Exiles Club, London, and to a Mr. A. J. Hatfield who had been a playing member of the Exiles rugby team and prominent in the organisation of the Exiles Club, Malta, in the 1920's. The Exiles Club,London, had no records or photos which would help, and none of the Club members who had been in Malta in the 20's could help answer my queries.
